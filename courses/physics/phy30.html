🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains simple cells, their defects, different types of electric cells, maintenance of batteries, arrangement of cells, emf, terminal potential difference, lost volts, and cell efficiency.

  • A cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy

  • Series adds emf, parallel reduces internal resistance
  • E = V + Ir is one of the most important formulas here
🧠 Big idea

Electric cells supply current because chemical reactions create a potential difference between two electrodes.

1️⃣ Simple Voltaic Cell and Its Defects

What is a simple voltaic cell?

A simple voltaic cell is an early electric cell that converts chemical energy to electrical energy through oxidation and reduction reactions.

Components

  • Zinc rod: negative electrode or anode
  • Copper rod: positive electrode or cathode
  • Dilute sulfuric acid: electrolyte
Simple voltaic cell
Zinc dissolves into the electrolyte and electrons flow externally from zinc to copper.

How it works

  • Oxidation at zinc: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻
  • Reduction at copper: 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → H₂

Two major defects

Defect Cause Effect Solution
PolarizationHydrogen bubbles form on copperReduces emf and currentUse depolarizer like manganese dioxide
Local actionImpurities in zincZinc wastes away even when unusedAmalgamate zinc with mercury

2️⃣ Types of Electric Cells

A. Daniel cell

  • Zinc rod in zinc sulfate solution
  • Copper rod in copper sulfate solution
  • Porous pot separates the solutions
  • Emf = 1.08 V

Advantage: no polarization.

B. Leclanché cell

  • Zinc rod as anode
  • Carbon rod surrounded by MnO₂ and carbon as cathode system
  • Electrolyte is ammonium chloride
  • Emf = 1.5 V

The dry cell is the modern portable form used in torches, toys, and radios.

C. Lead-acid accumulator

  • Negative plate: lead (Pb)
  • Positive plate: lead dioxide (PbO₂)
  • Electrolyte: dilute sulfuric acid
  • Emf = 2.0 V per cell
Discharging reaction
Pb + PbO₂ + 2H₂SO₄ → 2PbSO₄ + 2H₂O

D. Nickel-iron accumulator

  • Anode: iron
  • Cathode: nickel oxide
  • Electrolyte: potassium hydroxide
  • Emf = 1.2 V

E. Modern cells

  • Lithium-ion: lightweight, rechargeable, high energy density
  • Mercury-cadmium: stable voltage but toxic
  • Solar cells: convert sunlight directly to electrical energy

3️⃣ Maintenance of Cells and Batteries

For lead-acid batteries

  • Keep terminals clean
  • Top up with distilled water only
  • Keep battery properly charged
  • Avoid overcharging
  • Store in a cool, dry place

For dry cells

  • Store in a cool, dry place
  • Remove from devices not used for a long time
  • Do not mix old and new batteries

4️⃣ Arrangement of Cells

Series arrangement

Positive terminal of one cell is connected to the negative terminal of the next.

Series formulas
Etotal = E₁ + E₂ + E₃ + ...
rtotal = r₁ + r₂ + r₃ + ...
Cells in series
Series arrangement increases total emf.

Parallel arrangement

All positive terminals are joined together, and all negative terminals are joined together.

Parallel formulas for identical cells
Etotal = E
rtotal = r/n
Cells in parallel
Parallel arrangement keeps emf the same but reduces internal resistance.
🧠 Quick memory

Series adds voltage; parallel adds current capacity.

5️⃣ Efficiency of a Cell

Efficiency compares useful electrical output to total energy supplied by the cell.

Efficiency formulas
η = (Output/Input) × 100%
η = [R/(R + r)] × 100%

Factors affecting efficiency

  • Internal resistance
  • Current drawn
  • Temperature
  • Age and condition of the cell

7️⃣ Worked Examples

Calculation 1: Current, Terminal P.D., and Lost Volts

A cell has emf 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω. It is connected to a 2.5 Ω resistor. Find the current, terminal p.d., and lost volts.

I = E/(R + r) = 1.5/(2.5 + 0.5) = 0.5 A

V = IR = 0.5 × 2.5 = 1.25 V

Lost volts = Ir = 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25 V

Answer: 0.5 A, 1.25 V, 0.25 V
Calculation 2: Finding Internal Resistance

A cell of emf 2.0 V gives terminal p.d. 1.6 V when current is 0.8 A. Find internal resistance.

r = (E - V)/I = (2.0 - 1.6)/0.8 = 0.5 Ω

Answer: 0.5 Ω
Calculation 3: Cells in Series

Three identical cells each have emf 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.2 Ω. They are connected in series to a 4.4 Ω resistor. Find total emf, total internal resistance, current, and terminal p.d.

Etotal = 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 V

rtotal = 3 × 0.2 = 0.6 Ω

I = 4.5/(4.4 + 0.6) = 0.9 A

V = IR = 0.9 × 4.4 = 3.96 V

Answer: 4.5 V, 0.6 Ω, 0.9 A, 3.96 V
Calculation 4: Cells in Parallel

Four identical cells each have emf 2.0 V and internal resistance 0.4 Ω. They are connected in parallel to a 1.5 Ω resistor. Find total emf, total internal resistance, current, and current per cell.

Etotal = 2.0 V

rtotal = r/n = 0.4/4 = 0.1 Ω

I = 2.0/(1.5 + 0.1) = 1.25 A

I per cell = 1.25/4 = 0.3125 A

Answer: 2.0 V, 0.1 Ω, 1.25 A, 0.31 A
Calculation 5: Mixed Series-Parallel Arrangement

Six identical cells of emf 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.3 Ω are arranged in two parallel groups of three cells in series. If connected to an external resistance of 2.1 Ω, find total emf, total internal resistance, and current.

Emf per branch = 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 V

Resistance per branch = 3 × 0.3 = 0.9 Ω

Two equal branches in parallel give rtotal = 0.9/2 = 0.45 Ω

I = 4.5/(2.1 + 0.45) = 1.76 A

Answer: 4.5 V, 0.45 Ω, 1.76 A
Calculation 6: Maximum Power Transfer

A cell has emf 3.0 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω. Find the external resistance for maximum power transfer and the maximum power.

For maximum power, R = r = 0.5 Ω

Pmax = E²/4r = 3.0²/(4 × 0.5) = 4.5 W

Answer: 0.5 Ω and 4.5 W
Calculation 7: Efficiency

A battery has emf 12 V and internal resistance 0.4 Ω. It delivers current 2 A. Find total power, useful power, and efficiency.

Ptotal = EI = 12 × 2 = 24 W

R = E/I - r = 12/2 - 0.4 = 5.6 Ω

Puseful = I²R = 2² × 5.6 = 22.4 W

η = (22.4/24) × 100% = 93.3%

Answer: 24 W, 22.4 W, 93.3%
Calculation 8: Choosing the Best Arrangement

Four identical cells each have emf 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.2 Ω. A bulb is rated 3.0 V, 0.3 A. Which arrangement is best?

Bulb resistance: R = V/I = 3.0/0.3 = 10 Ω

Best option is two cells in series, with two such branches in parallel.

Etotal = 3.0 V and rtotal = 0.2 Ω

I = 3.0/(10 + 0.2) = 0.29 A

Answer: 2 cells in series with 2 such branches in parallel

8️⃣ Quick Memory Tips

High-score reminders

  • Zinc-Copper-Acid for the simple cell
  • Primary = use once, Secondary = rechargeable
  • Series = add voltage
  • Parallel = same emf, lower internal resistance
  • E = V + Ir means emf equals useful voltage plus lost volts
⚠️ Common JAMB tricks

Terminal p.d. drops as current increases because lost volts increase. Also, maximum power is transferred when external resistance equals internal resistance.

9️⃣ Practice Questions for JAMB

Polarization in a simple cell is caused by:

(a) Impurities in zinc
(b) Hydrogen bubbles on copper
(c) Low electrolyte level
(d) Broken circuit

Answer: (b) Hydrogen bubbles on copper
For identical cells connected in parallel, the total emf is:

(a) Zero
(b) Double one cell
(c) Equal to one cell
(d) Sum of all cells

Answer: (c) Equal to one cell
Maximum power is delivered to the load when:

(a) R > r
(b) R = r
(c) R < r
(d) R = 0

Answer: (b) R = r
