🔍 Refraction of Light at Plane and Curved Surfaces

JAMB Physics • Snell’s law • refractive index • prisms • critical angle • total internal reflection • lenses

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains the laws of refraction, refractive index, prisms, minimum deviation, critical angle, total internal reflection, lenses, and optical instruments like microscopes and telescopes.

  • Refraction is the bending of light between media of different optical density
  • Snell’s law is n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r
  • Critical angle helps explain total internal reflection
  • The lens formula is 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
🧠 Big idea

Light bends because its speed changes when it enters a different medium.

1️⃣ Laws of Refraction

What is refraction?

Refraction is the bending of light as it moves from one transparent medium to another of different optical density.

The two laws of refraction

  • First law: The incident ray, refracted ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
  • Second law: The ratio involving the sines of the angles is constant for a given pair of media.
Snell’s law
n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r

Key terms

  • Incident ray: incoming ray
  • Refracted ray: ray inside the second medium
  • Normal: line perpendicular to the surface
  • Angle of incidence: angle between incident ray and normal
  • Angle of refraction: angle between refracted ray and normal
Refraction at a boundary
When light enters a denser medium, it bends toward the normal.

2️⃣ Refractive Index

Definition

Refractive index measures how much light slows down in a medium compared with vacuum.

Refractive index formulas
n = c/v
n = sin i / sin r (for air to medium)

Higher refractive index means lower light speed in that medium and stronger bending toward the normal.

Calculation 1: Basic Snell’s Law

Light travels from air into water at an angle of incidence of 40°. Take n₁ = 1.0 and n₂ = 1.33. Find the angle of refraction.

1.0 × sin 40° = 1.33 × sin r

sin r = 0.6428 / 1.33 = 0.4833

r = sin⁻¹(0.4833) = 28.9°

Answer: about 29°

3️⃣ Light Through Prisms

Triangular prism

When light passes through a prism, it bends toward the base of the prism and becomes deviated from its original path.

Light through a triangular prism
The ray bends toward the base and emerges deviated from its original direction.

Angle of deviation

Deviation formulas
D = (i₁ + i₂) - (r₁ + r₂)
D = i₁ + i₂ - A

Minimum deviation

Minimum deviation occurs when the ray passes symmetrically through the prism.

  • i₁ = i₂
  • r₁ = r₂
  • The refracted ray inside is parallel to the base
At minimum deviation
Dₘ = 2i - A
n = sin[(A + Dₘ)/2] / sin(A/2)
Calculation 2: Minimum Deviation

A prism of angle 60° has a minimum deviation of 40°. Find the refractive index.

n = sin[(60 + 40)/2] / sin(60/2)

n = sin 50° / sin 30° = 0.7660 / 0.5 = 1.532

Answer: 1.53

4️⃣ Critical Angle and Total Internal Reflection

Critical angle

The critical angle is the angle of incidence in the denser medium for which the angle of refraction in the rarer medium is 90°.

Critical angle formulas
sin C = n₂/n₁ where n₁ > n₂
sin C = 1/n for denser medium to air

Total internal reflection

Total internal reflection happens when:

  • Light travels from denser to rarer medium
  • Angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle
Critical angle and total internal reflection
At the critical angle, the refracted ray grazes the surface; beyond it, total internal reflection occurs.

Applications of total internal reflection

  • Optical fibers
  • Prismatic binoculars
  • Diamond sparkle
  • Mirage formation
Calculation 3: Critical Angle of Water

Find the critical angle for water with refractive index 1.33.

sin C = 1/1.33 = 0.7519

C = sin⁻¹(0.7519) = 48.8°

Answer: about 49°
Calculation 4: Diamond Critical Angle

Diamond has refractive index 2.42. Find its critical angle.

sin C = 1/2.42 = 0.4132

C = sin⁻¹(0.4132) = 24.4°

Answer: 24.4°
💎 Why diamonds sparkle

Diamond has a very small critical angle, so light undergoes many internal reflections before emerging.

Convex and concave lenses
A convex lens converges rays, while a concave lens diverges them.
Lens formula
1/f = 1/u + 1/v

For lenses, real images have v > 0 and virtual images have v < 0.

Calculation 5: Convex Lens

An object is 30 cm from a convex lens of focal length 20 cm. Find image position and magnification.

Given: u = 30 cm, f = +20 cm

1/20 = 1/30 + 1/v

1/v = 1/20 - 1/30 = 1/60

v = 60 cm

m = v/u = 60/30 = 2

Answer: image at 60 cm, real, inverted, magnified 2 times
Calculation 6: Lens Power

A lens has focal length 25 cm. Find its power in diopters.

Convert: 25 cm = 0.25 m

P = 1/f = 1/0.25 = 4 D

Answer: +4 D

6️⃣ Optical Instruments

Simple microscope

  • Uses a convex lens of short focal length
  • Object is placed between the focal point and the lens
  • Image is virtual, erect, and magnified
Magnifying power of simple microscope
M = D/f for relaxed eye
M = 1 + D/f for near point

Compound microscope

Uses two convex lenses: the objective and the eyepiece. The objective forms a real magnified image which is then magnified again by the eyepiece.

Astronomical telescope

Uses two convex lenses. The objective has a long focal length and the eyepiece a short focal length.

Telescope magnification
M = f₀/fₑ
Calculation 7: Microscope Magnification

A simple microscope has focal length 5 cm. Find its magnifying power for relaxed eye and near point.

Relaxed eye: M = 25/5 = 5

Near point: M = 1 + 25/5 = 6

Answer: 5× and 6×
Calculation 8: Telescope Magnification

An astronomical telescope has objective focal length 100 cm and eyepiece focal length 5 cm. Find the magnifying power.

M = 100/5 = 20

Answer: 20×

7️⃣ Quick Revision Tips

Memory aids

  • Fast to slow: light bends toward the normal
  • Slow to fast: light bends away from the normal
  • sin C = 1/n when going from dense medium to air
  • Minimum deviation occurs when the path through a prism is symmetric
⚠️ Common JAMB traps

Critical angle exists only when light goes from denser to rarer medium. Also remember lens sign convention differs from mirrors.
