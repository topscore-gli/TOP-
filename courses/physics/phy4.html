PHYSICS

🌍 Gravitational Field

JAMB Physics • gravitation • field strength • potential • escape velocity • satellites • Kepler’s laws

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains how masses attract one another, how gravitational field strength works, why gravitational potential is negative, and how planets and satellites move.

  • Gravitational force is always attractive
  • Field strength falls with distance
  • Potential is negative
  • Satellite motion depends on gravity
🧠 Big idea

Gravity is weak between small objects, but it dominates when masses become huge — like planets, moons, and stars.

1️⃣ Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation

Every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Gravitational force formula
F = Gm₁m₂ / r²
G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²kg⁻²
m₁, m₂ = masses
r = distance between centres
Two masses attracting each other
The force acts along the line joining the centres of the two masses.
Example 1: Force Between Two Masses

Find the gravitational force between 100 kg and 200 kg masses 2 m apart.

Step 1: F = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(100)(200)/2²

Step 2: F = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(20,000)/4

Step 3: F = 3.335 × 10⁻⁷ N

Answer: 3.335 × 10⁻⁷ N

2️⃣ Gravitational Field Strength

Gravitational field strength at a point is the force per unit mass on a small test mass placed there.

Field strength formula
g = F/m = GM/r²

Its unit is N/kg or m/s².

On Earth’s surface, g ≈ 9.8 m/s² or sometimes 10 m/s² in JAMB calculations.

g decreases with distance
As distance from Earth’s centre increases, gravitational field strength decreases.
Example 2: Field Strength at Height

Calculate g at 640 km above Earth’s surface.

Step 1: R = 6400 km, h = 640 km

Step 2: gₕ/g = (R/(R+h))² = (6400/7040)² = (10/11)² = 100/121

Step 3: gₕ = 9.8 × 100/121 ≈ 8.1 m/s²

Answer: 8.1 m/s²

3️⃣ Gravitational Potential

Gravitational potential at a point is the work done in bringing a unit mass from infinity to that point.

Potential formula
V = -GM/r

The negative sign is very important.

  • Potential at infinity is taken as zero
  • Potential near a mass is negative
  • The field does work when a body falls inward
🧠 Why negative?

You must supply energy to remove a mass completely from the gravitational field to infinity.

Example 3: Gravitational Potential at Earth’s Surface

Step 1: V = -(6.67×10⁻¹¹)(6.0×10²⁴)/(6.4×10⁶)

Step 2: V = -6.25 × 10⁷ Jkg⁻¹

Answer: -6.25 × 10⁷ Jkg⁻¹ = -62.5 MJkg⁻¹

4️⃣ Escape Velocity

Escape velocity is the minimum speed needed for an object to leave a planet’s gravitational field without further propulsion.

Escape velocity formula
vₑ = √(2GM/R)
or vₑ = √(2gR)

Escape velocity does not depend on the mass of the object being launched.

Example 4: Earth’s Escape Velocity

Step 1: Use vₑ = √(2GM/R)

Step 2: Substitute Earth values

Step 3: vₑ ≈ 11,200 m/s

Answer: about 11.2 km/s
🧠 Quick memory value

Earth’s escape velocity is about 11 km/s.

5️⃣ Satellite Motion

For a satellite moving in a circular orbit, gravity provides the centripetal force.

Satellite formulas
GMm/r² = mv²/r
v = √(GM/r)
T² = 4π²r³/GM
Satellite in circular orbit
The satellite moves tangentially, while gravity pulls it inward and keeps it in orbit.
Example 5: Geostationary Satellite

A geostationary satellite has period 24 hours. Find its orbital radius.

Step 1: T = 24 × 3600 = 86400 s

Step 2: Use T² = 4π²r³/GM

Step 3: Solving gives r ≈ 4.23 × 10⁷ m

Step 4: Height above Earth = 4.23×10⁷ - 6.4×10⁶ ≈ 3.59×10⁷ m

Answer: orbital radius ≈ 42,300 km, height ≈ 35,900 km

6️⃣ Kepler’s Laws of Planetary Motion

Law Statement Meaning
First Law Planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus Orbits are not perfect circles
Second Law The line joining planet and Sun sweeps equal areas in equal times Planets move faster when closer to the Sun
Third Law T² ∝ r³ The ratio T²/r³ is constant for planets about the same central body

Variation of g

At height h
gₕ = GM/(R+h)²
For small h: gₕ ≈ g(1 - 2h/R)
At depth d
g_d = g(1 - d/R)
🧠 Very important point

g is maximum at Earth’s surface. It decreases both above the surface and below the surface.

9️⃣ JAMB-Style Practice Problems

Problem 1: Proportionality

If gravitational force between two masses is F and the distance is halved, what is the new force?

Step 1: F ∝ 1/r²

Step 2: If r → r/2, then force becomes 4F

Answer: 4F
Problem 2: Energy to Move Satellite

Find the energy needed to move a 1000 kg satellite from Earth’s surface to orbit of radius 2R.

Step 1: Change in potential energy = GMm(1/R - 1/2R)

Step 2: = GMm/2R = ½mgR

Step 3: = ½(1000)(9.8)(6.4×10⁶)

Step 4: = 3.14 × 10¹⁰ J

Answer: 3.14 × 10¹⁰ J
Problem 3: Binary System

Two stars of mass 4M and M rotate about a common centre of mass at separation d. The period comes out as:

T² = 4π²d³ / 5GM

Key result: T² = 4π²d³ / 5GM
