🔥 Heat Transfer

JAMB Physics • conduction • convection • radiation • thermal conductivity • thermos flask • land and sea breeze

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains the three modes of heat transfer, thermal conductivity, temperature gradient, heat flux, radiation and surface effects, thermos flask operation, land and sea breezes, and heat transfer in engines.

  • Conduction transfers heat through contact
  • Convection transfers heat by fluid movement
  • Radiation transfers heat by electromagnetic waves
  • The main conduction formula is Q/t = kAΔT/d
🧠 Big idea

Heat always flows from hotter regions to colder regions, but the way it moves depends on the material and the situation.

1️⃣ Three Modes of Heat Transfer

Mode Mechanism Medium Required Examples
Conduction Heat passes by molecular vibrations and contact Mainly solids Metal spoon in hot soup
Convection Heat transfer by movement of fluid particles Liquids and gases only Boiling water, sea breeze
Radiation Heat transfer by electromagnetic waves No medium needed Sun heating the Earth

The three modes
Conduction needs contact, convection needs fluid motion, and radiation can travel through space.

2️⃣ Temperature Gradient, Conductivity, and Heat Flux

Temperature gradient

The temperature gradient is the rate of change of temperature with distance.

Temperature gradient
ΔT/Δx or dT/dx

Thermal conductivity

Thermal conductivity tells how well a material conducts heat.

  • Symbol: k
  • Unit: W m⁻¹ K⁻¹
  • High k means good conductor
  • Low k means poor conductor or insulator

Heat flux

Fourier’s law
q = Q/A = -k(dT/dx)
Total heat flow rate
Q/t = kA(T₁ - T₂)/d

This is one of the most important formulas in JAMB heat transfer questions.

6️⃣ Land and Sea Breeze

Sea breeze

During the day, land heats faster than sea. Air above land becomes warm and rises, so cooler air from the sea moves toward the land.

Land breeze

At night, land cools faster than sea. Air above the sea is relatively warmer and rises, so cooler air from the land moves toward the sea.

🧠 Easy memory

Sea by day, land by night.

Sea breeze and land breeze
Sea breeze is caused by convection currents due to unequal heating of land and sea.

7️⃣ Engines

Internal combustion engines

Fuel burns inside the cylinder. Heat is produced and converted to mechanical work. Cooling systems remove excess heat by conduction and convection.

Jet engines

Air is compressed, mixed with fuel, and ignited. Hot gases rush backward and produce thrust.

Rockets

Rockets carry their own oxidizer and can work in space. Heat transfer occurs by conduction and radiation, while convection acts inside the combustion chamber.

JAMB often focuses on the fact that engines convert heat energy into mechanical work and require cooling systems.

8️⃣ Essential Calculations for JAMB

Calculation 1: Basic Heat Conduction

Find the heat flow rate through a brick wall of area 20 m² and thickness 0.3 m if the two sides are at 35°C and 15°C. Take k = 0.6 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹.

Step: Q/t = kAΔT/d = (0.6 × 20 × 20)/0.3 = 800 W

Answer: 800 W
Calculation 2: Finding Temperature Difference

A copper rod of length 2 m and area 0.5 cm² conducts heat at 100 W. If k = 400 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹, find the temperature difference.

Step 1: Convert area: 0.5 cm² = 5 × 10⁻⁵ m²

Step 2: ΔT = (Q/t × d)/(kA)

ΔT = (100 × 2)/(400 × 5×10⁻⁵) = 10000 K

Answer: 10,000 K
Calculation 3: Heat Flux

A metal plate has temperature gradient 50°C per cm. If k = 200 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹, find the heat flux.

Step 1: Convert gradient: 50°C/cm = 5000 K/m

Step 2: q = k(dT/dx) = 200 × 5000 = 10⁶ W m⁻²

Answer: 1.0 × 10⁶ W m⁻²
Calculation 4: Comparing Materials

Two identical rods are made of copper and iron with k = 400 and 80 respectively. Find the ratio of their heat flow rates.

Step: Since length, area, and temperature difference are the same, ratio = 400/80 = 5:1

Answer: 5:1
Calculation 5: Composite Wall

A wall has brick of thickness 0.2 m and k = 0.6, and plaster of thickness 0.05 m and k = 0.3. Outer temperatures are 30°C and 10°C. Find the heat flux.

Step 1: Total resistance R = d₁/k₁ + d₂/k₂ = 0.2/0.6 + 0.05/0.3 = 0.5

Step 2: q = ΔT/R = 20/0.5 = 40 W m⁻²

Answer: 40 W m⁻²
Calculation 6: Time to Transfer Energy

How long will it take 5000 J to pass through an aluminium window of area 1.5 m² and thickness 4 mm if ΔT = 25°C and k = 237 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹?

Step 1: d = 0.004 m

Step 2: Q/t = kAΔT/d = (237 × 1.5 × 25)/0.004 = 2,221,875 W

Step 3: t = Q/(Q/t) = 5000/2,221,875 ≈ 0.00225 s

Answer: about 0.00225 s
Calculation 7: Finding Thickness

A freezer wall must limit heat gain to 50 W. If outside is 25°C, inside is -5°C, area is 4 m², and k = 0.04 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹, find the thickness.

Step 1: ΔT = 30 K

Step 2: d = kAΔT/(Q/t)

d = (0.04 × 4 × 30)/50 = 0.096 m

Answer: 0.096 m or 9.6 cm

🔟 Common JAMB Question Patterns

  • Best conductor? Usually silver or copper
  • Why are thermos walls silvered? To reduce radiation
  • Heat flow through wall? Use Q/t = kAΔT/d
  • Wind direction near coast at noon? Sea breeze
  • Why are solar collectors black? Good absorbers of radiation

Final tips

  • Master Q/t = kAΔT/d
  • Visualize convection with rising warm fluid and sinking cool fluid
  • Distinguish clearly between conduction, convection, and radiation
  • Convert all units before calculation
