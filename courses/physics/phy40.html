💡 Part A: Fiber Optics

1. What is fiber optics?

Fiber optics is the transmission of light through thin flexible fibers made of glass or plastic. It carries signals over long distances with very little loss.

Think of optical fiber as a light pipe that guides light even around bends.

2. How it works: Total internal reflection

Light travels inside the core and reflects repeatedly at the core-cladding boundary when the angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle.

Light guiding in an optical fiber
Light reflects internally many times and stays trapped in the core.

2️⃣ How Lasers Work

Absorption

An atom absorbs a photon and moves from a lower energy state to a higher energy state.

Spontaneous emission

An excited atom returns to a lower level on its own, emitting a random photon.

Stimulated emission

An incoming photon triggers an excited atom to emit another identical photon.

Stimulated emission idea
Stimulated emission produces photons with the same direction, phase, and energy.
🧠 Three S's

Stimulated emission gives photons with the Same direction, Same phase, and Same energy.

3️⃣ Conditions for Laser Action

  • Population inversion: more atoms in excited state than in ground state
  • Pumping: external energy source to excite atoms
  • Metastable state: excited atoms stay long enough to be stimulated
  • Optical cavity: mirrors reflect photons back and forth for amplification

Population inversion is essential because ordinary matter naturally has more atoms in the lower energy state.

🎯 Past Question Tips

Common exam patterns

  • Explain total internal reflection in fiber optics
  • Calculate critical angle from refractive indices
  • Differentiate spontaneous and stimulated emission
  • Define population inversion and explain why it is necessary
  • List applications of lasers or fiber optics
  • Compare laser light with ordinary light
Sample quick answer

Total internal reflection occurs when light travels from a denser medium to a less dense medium at an angle greater than the critical angle, causing complete reflection. In fiber optics, this keeps light trapped inside the core so it can travel long distances with little loss.

🧠 Memory Aids

For fiber optics

TIR keeps the light in. Also remember: the core must have a higher refractive index than the cladding.

For lasers

LASER = Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation. Also, population inversion means more atoms are up than down.
