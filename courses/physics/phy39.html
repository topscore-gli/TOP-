🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains band theory, classification of materials, intrinsic and extrinsic semiconductors, p-n junction diodes, rectification, transistors, and amplification for JAMB success.

  • Metals conduct easily because their bands overlap
  • Semiconductors have a small band gap
  • Diodes allow current mainly in one direction
  • Transistors are used for amplification and switching
🧠 Big idea

Electronics is built on controlling the movement of electrons and holes inside semiconductor materials.

1️⃣ Classification of Materials

Band theory concept

Electrons occupy energy bands in solids. The two important bands are:

  • Valence band: where electrons are normally bound
  • Conduction band: where electrons move freely and conduct

The gap between them is called the band gap or forbidden energy gap.

Band gap idea
The band gap determines how easily electrons can move and conduct electricity.

2️⃣ Intrinsic Semiconductors

An intrinsic semiconductor is a pure semiconductor such as silicon or germanium with no deliberate impurity added.

  • Electrons and holes are present in equal numbers
  • Conductivity increases with temperature
  • At 0 K it behaves like an insulator
🧠 Memory aid

When an electron leaves the valence band, it leaves a vacancy called a hole. A hole acts like a positive charge carrier.

3️⃣ Extrinsic Semiconductors

Extrinsic semiconductors are formed by adding small amounts of impurity atoms through doping.

🧠 Memory trick

n-type means negative carriers are majority. p-type means positive holes are majority.

Even after doping, both n-type and p-type semiconductors remain electrically neutral overall.

4️⃣ The p-n Junction Diode

Formation of the junction

  • Electrons diffuse from n-side to p-side
  • Holes diffuse from p-side to n-side
  • A depletion region forms at the junction
  • A barrier potential develops

Typical barrier potentials are about 0.7 V for silicon and 0.3 V for germanium.

p-n junction concept
The depletion region has very few free charge carriers.

⚡ JAMB point

A diode behaves like a one-way valve for current.

5️⃣ Rectification

Rectification is the conversion of alternating current to direct current using diodes.

Half-wave rectification

  • Uses one diode
  • Only one half-cycle of A.C. passes
  • Output is pulsating D.C.

Full-wave rectification

  • Uses both half-cycles of A.C.
  • Can be done with center-tap or bridge rectifier
  • Output frequency is twice the input frequency

Half-wave vs full-wave output idea
Full-wave rectification uses both halves of the input signal, giving smoother output than half-wave.

Smoothing is done with a capacitor placed across the load to reduce ripple in the rectified output.

6️⃣ Transistors and Amplification

What is a transistor?

A transistor is a three-layer semiconductor device used for amplification and switching.

Types

  • npn transistor: p-type between two n-type layers
  • pnp transistor: n-type between two p-type layers

Terminals

  • Emitter: heavily doped, emits carriers
  • Base: thin and lightly doped, controls current
  • Collector: collects carriers
Transistor terminals
A transistor has three terminals: emitter, base, and collector.

Current gain

Current gain
β = Ic/Ib

A small base current controls a much larger collector current. That is why a transistor can amplify.

🔊 Amplifier idea

Small changes in base current produce large changes in collector current, so the output signal becomes stronger than the input signal.
