🌈 Dispersion of Light and Colors

JAMB Physics • prism dispersion • angular dispersion • dispersive power • color mixing • rainbow • electromagnetic spectrum

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains dispersion by prisms, angular dispersion, dispersive power, color mixing, rainbow formation, and the electromagnetic spectrum for JAMB success.

  • White light is made up of many colors
  • Violet bends more than red in a prism
  • Angular dispersion measures color separation
  • Dispersive power has no unit
🧠 Big idea

Dispersion happens because different colors of light have different wavelengths, so they refract by different amounts in glass.

1️⃣ What is Dispersion?

Principle

Dispersion is the splitting of white light into its component colors when it passes through a prism or another refracting medium.

White light contains the seven colors: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet — ROYGBIV.

White light through a prism
Red deviates least, while violet deviates most.

Why it happens

The refractive index of glass depends on wavelength. Since violet light has shorter wavelength than red light, it has a higher refractive index and bends more.

Cauchy’s formula
n = n₀ + A/λ²

nviolet > nred, so violet bends more than red.

2️⃣ Deviation and Angular Dispersion

For a small-angled prism of angle A:

Mean deviation for yellow light
δy = (ny - 1)A
Angular dispersion
θ = (nv - nr)A
Dispersive power
ω = (nv - nr)/(ny - 1)
ω ≈ θ/δy
Calculation 1: Angular Dispersion

A prism has angle 6°. The refractive indices are 1.514 for red and 1.523 for violet. Find the angular dispersion.

θ = (1.523 - 1.514) × 6°

θ = 0.009 × 6° = 0.054°

Answer: 0.054°
Calculation 2: Dispersive Power

A prism has nr = 1.51, ny = 1.52, and nv = 1.53. Find its dispersive power.

ω = (1.53 - 1.51)/(1.52 - 1)

ω = 0.02/0.52 = 0.0385

Answer: 0.0385
Calculation 3: Refraction at Minimum Deviation

For a prism with angle 60°, find the angle of refraction at minimum deviation.

At minimum deviation, r = A/2

r = 60°/2 = 30°

Answer: 30°
Calculation 4: Deviation Without Dispersion

Two prisms are combined to give deviation without dispersion. Prism 1 has A = 5°, nr = 1.51, nv = 1.53. Prism 2 has n'r = 1.61, n'v = 1.63. Find A'.

(nv - nr)A = (n'v - n'r)A'

0.02 × 5 = 0.02 × A'

A' = 5°

Answer: 5°

3️⃣ Production of Pure Spectrum

A pure spectrum is one in which the colors are separated clearly without overlap.

  • Use a narrow slit
  • Use a collimator to produce parallel rays
  • Pass the rays through a prism
  • View with a screen or telescope

An impure spectrum forms when colors overlap, often because the incident rays are not parallel or the slit is too wide.

4️⃣ Colour Mixing

Primary colours of light

The primary colours of light are Red, Green, and Blue (RGB).

  • Red + Green = Yellow
  • Red + Blue = Magenta
  • Green + Blue = Cyan
  • Red + Green + Blue = White
Additive colour mixing
In additive mixing, the combination of RGB gives white light.

Subtractive mixing

The primary pigments are Cyan, Magenta, and Yellow (CMY). Filters and pigments work by absorbing some colours and transmitting or reflecting others.

5️⃣ Colour of Objects

An object appears a certain color because it reflects that color and absorbs the others.

  • A red object reflects red light
  • A white object reflects all colors
  • A black object absorbs all colors
🧠 Quick memory

A filter transmits its own color and absorbs most other colors.

6️⃣ The Rainbow

A rainbow is a natural spectrum formed when sunlight interacts with water droplets.

  • Light is refracted as it enters the droplet
  • Dispersion separates the colors
  • Internal reflection occurs inside the droplet
  • Light is refracted again as it leaves
Rainbow formation in a water droplet
Primary rainbow has one internal reflection, with red outside and violet inside.
  • Primary rainbow: one internal reflection, red outside, violet inside
  • Secondary rainbow: two internal reflections, colors reversed, fainter

9️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Violet bends more than red in a prism
  • Dispersive power has no unit
  • At minimum deviation, r = A/2
  • Know RGB for light and CMY for pigments
  • Know the full EM spectrum order
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not confuse deviation with dispersion, and do not forget that secondary rainbow has reversed colors and is fainter.
