PHYSICS

🔭 Optical Instruments

JAMB Physics • camera • projector • microscope • telescope • human eye • magnifying power • eye defects

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains the principles of cameras, projectors, microscopes, telescopes, and the human eye, including calculations involving magnifying power, near point, far point, and lens formulas.

  • Most optical instruments use convex lenses
  • Camera forms a real, inverted image on film or sensor
  • Simple microscope gives a virtual, erect, magnified image
  • Telescope magnification is M = fₒ/fₑ
🧠 Big idea

Optical instruments work by controlling how lenses form real or virtual images and how the eye views those images.

1️⃣ The Camera

Principle

A camera uses a convex lens to form a real, inverted, diminished image on film or an electronic sensor.

Camera image formation
The camera forms a real inverted image on the sensor or film.

Key components

  • Convex lens: forms the image
  • Aperture: controls amount of light entering
  • Shutter: controls exposure time
  • Film/Sensor: records the image
  • Focusing ring: changes lens-to-film distance
Camera lens formula
1/f = 1/u + 1/v
F-number
f/# = f/D

Smaller f-number means a larger aperture and more light entering the camera.

Calculation 1: Camera Focusing

A camera has focal length 50 mm. How far must the film be from the lens to photograph a distant mountain?

For a distant object, u ≈ ∞, so v ≈ f.

v = 50 mm

Answer: 50 mm
Calculation 2: Close-up Photography

The same camera has focal length 50 mm and photographs a flower 30 cm away. Find how far the lens moves from the distant-object position.

Convert: 50 mm = 5 cm

1/5 = 1/30 + 1/v

1/v = 1/5 - 1/30 = 1/6

v = 6 cm = 60 mm

Movement = 60 - 50 = 10 mm

Answer: 10 mm away from the film position

2️⃣ The Projector

Principle

A projector uses a convex lens to form a real, inverted, magnified image of a small slide on a distant screen.

Projector image formation
The slide is placed just beyond the focal point to produce a large image on the screen.

Key points

  • Uses a bright light source
  • Slide is placed just beyond the focal length
  • Large image distance gives large magnification
Projector magnification
m = v/u = hᵢ/hₒ
Calculation 3: Projector Setup

A projector has focal length 10 cm. A slide 2 cm tall is placed 10.5 cm from the lens. Find the image distance and image size.

1/10 = 1/10.5 + 1/v

1/v = 1/10 - 1/10.5 = 0.5/105

v = 210 cm

m = v/u = 210/10.5 = 20

Image height = 20 × 2 = 40 cm

Answer: 210 cm and 40 cm

3️⃣ The Simple Microscope

Principle

A simple microscope or magnifying glass uses a convex lens of short focal length. The object is placed between the lens and its focus, so the image formed is virtual, erect, and magnified.

Simple microscope
The magnifying glass forms a larger virtual image on the same side as the object.
Angular magnification
M = D/f for relaxed eye
M = 1 + D/f for near point

Here, D = 25 cm is the least distance of distinct vision.

Calculation 4: Simple Microscope

A magnifying glass has focal length 5 cm. Find its magnifying power for relaxed eye and near point.

Relaxed eye: M = 25/5 = 5×

Near point: M = 1 + 25/5 = 6×

Answer: 5× and 6×

4️⃣ The Compound Microscope

Construction

A compound microscope uses two convex lenses:

  • Objective: short focal length, close to the specimen
  • Eyepiece: longer focal length, close to the eye
Compound microscope
The objective makes a real enlarged image, and the eyepiece magnifies it further.

Image formation

  • Objective forms a real, inverted, magnified image
  • Eyepiece acts like a simple microscope
  • Final image is virtual, inverted, and highly magnified
Compound microscope magnification
M = Mₒ × Mₑ
M ≈ (L/fₒ) × (D/fₑ)
Calculation 5: Compound Microscope

A compound microscope has objective focal length 4 mm and eyepiece focal length 2 cm. Tube length is 16 cm. Find the total magnification for relaxed eye.

Convert: 4 mm = 0.4 cm

Mₒ = L/fₒ = 16/0.4 = 40×

Mₑ = D/fₑ = 25/2 = 12.5×

M = 40 × 12.5 = 500×

Answer: 500×

5️⃣ The Astronomical Telescope

Construction

An astronomical telescope uses two convex lenses on the same axis:

  • Objective: large diameter, long focal length
  • Eyepiece: short focal length
Astronomical telescope
The objective forms a real image, and the eyepiece magnifies it for the eye.

Image formation

  • Objective forms a real, inverted image at its focal plane
  • Eyepiece magnifies this image
  • Final image is virtual, inverted, and usually at infinity for relaxed eye
Telescope formulas
M = fₒ/fₑ
L = fₒ + fₑ
Calculation 6: Astronomical Telescope

A telescope has objective focal length 100 cm and eyepiece focal length 5 cm. Find the magnifying power and tube length.

M = 100/5 = 20×

L = 100 + 5 = 105 cm

Answer: 20× and 105 cm

6️⃣ The Human Eye

Structure and function

  • Cornea: transparent front part
  • Aqueous humor: fluid between cornea and lens
  • Iris: controls pupil size
  • Lens: fine-tunes focus
  • Ciliary muscles: change lens shape
  • Retina: light-sensitive screen
  • Optic nerve: carries signals to the brain
Human eye structure
The eye focuses light on the retina, where the image is formed.

Accommodation

Accommodation is the ability of the eye lens to change shape so that objects at different distances can be focused clearly on the retina.

  • Near object: lens becomes thicker, focal length decreases
  • Distant object: lens becomes thinner, focal length increases

Calculation 7: Correcting Myopia

A short-sighted person cannot see clearly beyond 2 m. Find the power of lens needed.

The correcting lens must form a virtual image of a distant object at 2 m.

f = -2 m

P = 1/f = -0.5 D

Answer: -0.5 D
Calculation 8: Correcting Hypermetropia

A long-sighted person has near point at 50 cm. Find the power of lens needed to read at 25 cm.

The lens should make an object at 25 cm appear at 50 cm.

1/f = 1/0.25 + 1/(-0.5) = 4 - 2 = 2

f = 0.5 m

P = +2 D

Answer: +2 D

8️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Camera and projector both use a convex lens, but object positions differ
  • Simple microscope uses M = D/f
  • Telescope uses M = fₒ/fₑ
  • Myopia is corrected with a concave lens
  • Hypermetropia is corrected with a convex lens
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not confuse linear magnification with angular magnification, and remember that an astronomical telescope forms an inverted final image.

9️⃣ Final Check

  • Know the principle of each instrument
  • Memorize the main formulas
  • Understand near point and far point
  • Know which lenses correct eye defects
  • Convert units carefully before calculating power
