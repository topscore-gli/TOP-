🔄 Simple A.C. Circuits

JAMB Physics • alternating current • RMS values • reactance • impedance • power factor • resonance

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains alternating current, RMS values, reactance, impedance, power in A.C. circuits, resonance, and common JAMB calculations.

  • A.C. changes direction periodically
  • RMS values are the effective values used in power calculations
  • Inductors cause lag, capacitors cause lead
  • At resonance, Xₗ = Xᴄ and the circuit behaves like a pure resistor
🧠 Big idea

Simple A.C. circuits are all about how resistance and reactance combine to control current, phase, and power.

1️⃣ Understanding Alternating Current

Alternating current is current that reverses direction periodically, unlike direct current which flows in one direction only.

Sinusoidal equations
I = I₀ sin(ωt)
V = V₀ sin(ωt)
ω = 2πf
A.C. sine wave
A.C. varies sinusoidally with time and changes direction every half cycle.

Calculation 1: RMS and Peak Values

An A.C. voltage has a peak value of 311 V. Find the RMS voltage.

Vrms = V₀/√2 = 311/1.414 = 220 V

Answer: 220 V

3️⃣ Components in A.C. Circuits

A. Resistor in A.C.

  • Voltage and current are in phase
  • Opposition is ordinary resistance R
  • Power is dissipated as heat

B. Inductor in A.C.

  • Current lags voltage by 90°
  • Opposition is inductive reactance
Inductive reactance
Xₗ = 2πfL = ωL
🧠 Memory tip

L for Lag — current comes late in an inductor.

Calculation 2: Inductive Reactance

A 0.5 H inductor is connected to a 50 Hz supply. Find Xₗ.

Xₗ = 2πfL = 2 × 3.142 × 50 × 0.5 = 157.1 Ω

Answer: 157.1 Ω

C. Capacitor in A.C.

  • Current leads voltage by 90°
  • Opposition is capacitive reactance
Capacitive reactance
Xᴄ = 1/(2πfC) = 1/(ωC)
🧠 Memory tip

C for Current Comes first in a capacitor.

Calculation 3: Capacitive Reactance

A 10 μF capacitor is connected to a 50 Hz supply. Find Xᴄ.

Xᴄ = 1/(2πfC)

Xᴄ = 1/(2 × 3.142 × 50 × 10 × 10⁻⁶) = 318.3 Ω

Answer: 318.3 Ω

4️⃣ Impedance in A.C. Circuits

Impedance is the total opposition to current in an A.C. circuit.

Series R-L circuit
Z = √(R² + Xₗ²)
tan φ = Xₗ/R
Series R-C circuit
Z = √(R² + Xᴄ²)
tan φ = Xᴄ/R
Series R-L-C circuit
Z = √(R² + (Xₗ - Xᴄ)²)
tan φ = (Xₗ - Xᴄ)/R
  • If Xₗ > Xᴄ, the circuit is inductive and current lags
  • If Xᴄ > Xₗ, the circuit is capacitive and current leads
  • If Xₗ = Xᴄ, the circuit is at resonance
Reactance idea in RLC circuit
Mentally place resistance on the horizontal axis, inductive reactance upward, and capacitive reactance downward.
Calculation 4: RLC Circuit Impedance

A series RLC circuit has R = 10 Ω, L = 0.1 H, and C = 100 μF at 50 Hz. Find the impedance.

Xₗ = 2π × 50 × 0.1 = 31.42 Ω

Xᴄ = 1/(2π × 50 × 100 × 10⁻⁶) = 31.83 Ω

Z = √(10² + (31.42 - 31.83)²) ≈ 10 Ω

Answer: 10 Ω

Power factor
cos φ = R/Z = Real Power/Apparent Power
  • Power factor ranges from 0 to 1
  • At unity power factor, all power is real power
  • Low power factor means less efficient power use
Calculation 5: Real Power

A circuit draws 2 A from a 100 V supply with power factor 0.8. Find the real power.

P = VI cos φ = 100 × 2 × 0.8 = 160 W

Answer: 160 W

6️⃣ Resonance in Series R-L-C Circuits

Resonance occurs when inductive reactance equals capacitive reactance.

Resonant frequency
f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC))

At resonance

  • Xₗ = Xᴄ
  • Impedance is minimum: Z = R
  • Current is maximum: I = V/R
  • Power factor is 1
  • Phase angle is 0°
Calculation 6: Resonance Frequency

Find the resonant frequency for L = 1 H and C = 10 μF.

f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC))

f₀ = 1/(2π√(1 × 10 × 10⁻⁶)) = 50.3 Hz

Answer: 50.3 Hz

8️⃣ Comprehensive JAMB Practice Question

Series RLC Calculation

A series circuit consists of R = 30 Ω, L = 0.4 H, and C = 20 μF connected to a 100 V, 50 Hz supply. Find:

(a) Xₗ, (b) Xᴄ, (c) Z, (d) Current, (e) Phase angle, (f) Power factor.

(a) Xₗ = 2πfL = 2 × 3.142 × 50 × 0.4 = 125.7 Ω

(b) Xᴄ = 1/(2πfC) = 159.2 Ω

(c) Z = √(30² + (125.7 - 159.2)²) = 45.0 Ω

(d) I = V/Z = 100/45 = 2.22 A

(e) tan φ = (Xₗ - Xᴄ)/R = -33.5/30 = -1.117

φ = -48.1° (capacitive, current leads)

(f) cos φ = R/Z = 30/45 = 0.667

Answers: 125.7 Ω, 159.2 Ω, 45 Ω, 2.22 A, -48.1°, 0.667

9️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Always convert μF to F and mH to H
  • Unless stated otherwise, A.C. voltages are usually RMS values
  • At resonance, use Z = R
  • Power factor is between 0 and 1
  • Pure resistor: no phase difference
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not mix up lead and lag, and do not forget that higher frequency increases Xₗ but decreases Xᴄ.

🔟 Quick Practice Questions

Q1. Calculate the peak current if RMS current is 5 A.

I₀ = √2 × Irms = 1.414 × 5 = 7.07 A

Answer: 7.07 A
Q2. At what frequency would a 10 H inductor have reactance of 2000 Ω?

Xₗ = 2πfL

f = Xₗ/(2πL) = 2000/(2 × 3.142 × 10) = 31.83 Hz

Answer: 31.83 Hz
Q3. Find the power factor of a circuit with R = 60 Ω and Z = 100 Ω.

cos φ = R/Z = 60/100 = 0.6

Answer: 0.6
