PHYSICS

🪞 Reflection of Light at Plane and Curved Surfaces

JAMB Physics • laws of reflection • plane mirrors • curved mirrors • mirror formula • magnification • optical instruments

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains the laws of reflection, image formation by plane, concave and convex mirrors, the mirror formula, magnification, and applications in instruments like periscope, kaleidoscope, and sextant.

  • Reflection is the bouncing back of light from a surface
  • For reflection, i = r
  • Plane mirrors form virtual upright images
  • Curved mirrors can form real or virtual images depending on position
🧠 Big idea

All image formation by mirrors comes from one key behavior: light reflects in a predictable way.

1️⃣ Laws of Reflection

What is reflection?

Reflection is the bouncing back of light rays when they strike a smooth surface such as a mirror.

The two laws of reflection

  • First law: The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
  • Second law: The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
Reflection law
i = r

Key terms

  • Incident ray: ray coming toward the mirror
  • Reflected ray: ray leaving the mirror
  • Normal: line perpendicular to the mirror at the point of incidence
  • Angle of incidence: angle between incident ray and normal
  • Angle of reflection: angle between reflected ray and normal
Incident ray, normal, and reflected ray
The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection, both measured from the normal.

2️⃣ Plane Mirrors

Characteristics of images in plane mirrors

  • Virtual
  • Erect or upright
  • Same size as the object
  • Laterally inverted
  • Image distance equals object distance from the mirror
Image in a plane mirror
The image appears behind the mirror, upright and equal in size to the object.

Lateral inversion

In a plane mirror, right appears left and left appears right. This is why words on ambulances are often reversed.

Multiple reflections

Calculation 1: Multiple Images

Two plane mirrors are inclined at 72°. Find the number of images formed.

Step: n = (360/72) - 1 = 5 - 1 = 4

Answer: 4 images
Calculation 2: Distance in Plane Mirror

A man stands 2 m from a plane mirror. He walks 0.5 m toward it. Find the initial and new distance between the man and his image.

Initial: 2 + 2 = 4 m

New object distance: 2 - 0.5 = 1.5 m

New man-image distance: 1.5 + 1.5 = 3 m

Answer: 4 m and 3 m
Calculation 3: Minimum Mirror Height

A man is 1.8 m tall. Find the minimum mirror height needed to see his full image.

Rule: Minimum mirror length = half of body height

= 1.8/2 = 0.9 m

Answer: 0.9 m or 90 cm

Focal length and radius
f = R/2
R = 2f

Sign convention used here

  • Distances are measured from the pole
  • For mirrors, object distance for a real object is usually negative
  • Concave mirror: f is negative
  • Convex mirror: f is positive
Calculation 4: Focal Length from Radius

A concave mirror has radius of curvature 60 cm. Find the focal length.

f = R/2 = 60/2 = 30 cm

Answer: 30 cm or -30 cm by sign convention
Calculation 5: Radius from Focal Length

A convex mirror has focal length 20 cm. Find the radius of curvature.

R = 2f = 2 × 20 = 40 cm

Answer: 40 cm

4️⃣ Ray Diagrams for Curved Mirrors

Concave mirror rules

  • Ray parallel to principal axis reflects through focus
  • Ray through focus reflects parallel to axis
  • Ray through centre of curvature retraces its path
  • Ray to the pole reflects with equal angle

Convex mirror rules

  • Ray parallel to axis appears to come from focus behind mirror
  • Ray directed toward focus reflects parallel to axis
  • Ray directed toward centre of curvature retraces its path

5️⃣ Mirror Formula and Magnification

Mirror formula
1/f = 1/u + 1/v
Linear magnification
m = hᵢ/hₒ = -v/u
  • |m| > 1 → magnified
  • |m| < 1 → diminished
  • |m| = 1 → same size
  • m > 0 → erect
  • m < 0 → inverted
Calculation 6: Concave Mirror, Real Image

An object is 30 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 10 cm. Find image position and magnification.

Given: u = -30 cm, f = -10 cm

1/(-10) = 1/(-30) + 1/v

1/v = -1/10 + 1/30 = -2/30 = -1/15

v = -15 cm

m = -v/u = -(-15)/(-30) = -0.5

Answer: image at 15 cm in front, real, inverted, m = -0.5
Calculation 7: Convex Mirror

A convex mirror has radius 40 cm. An object is placed 20 cm from it. Find image position and magnification.

Given: R = +40 cm, f = +20 cm, u = -20 cm

1/20 = 1/(-20) + 1/v

1/v = 1/20 + 1/20 = 1/10

v = +10 cm

m = -10/(-20) = +0.5

Answer: image 10 cm behind mirror, virtual, erect, m = +0.5
Calculation 8: Concave Mirror, Virtual Image

An object is 10 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find image position and magnification.

Given: u = -10 cm, f = -15 cm

1/(-15) = 1/(-10) + 1/v

1/v = -1/15 + 1/10 = 1/30

v = +30 cm

m = -(30)/(-10) = +3

Answer: image 30 cm behind mirror, virtual, erect, magnified 3 times
Calculation 9: Finding Focal Length from Magnification

An object 20 cm from a concave mirror produces a real image twice the size of the object. Find the focal length.

Given: u = -20 cm, m = -2

-2 = -v/u gives v = -40 cm

1/f = 1/(-20) + 1/(-40) = -3/40

f = -40/3 = -13.33 cm

Answer: 13.33 cm in magnitude, concave so f = -13.33 cm
Calculation 10: Object at Centre of Curvature

An object 3 cm tall is placed at the centre of curvature of a concave mirror with R = 40 cm.

Given: f = -20 cm, u = -40 cm

1/(-20) = 1/(-40) + 1/v

v = -40 cm

m = -(-40)/(-40) = -1

hᵢ = m × hₒ = -1 × 3 = -3 cm

Answer: image at 40 cm in front, real, inverted, same size
Calculation 11: Object Position for a Real Image Magnified 3 Times

Where should an object be placed before a concave mirror of focal length 20 cm to form a real image magnified 3 times?

Given: f = -20 cm, m = -3

-3 = -v/u so v = 3u

1/(-20) = 1/u + 1/(3u) = 4/(3u)

u = -26.67 cm

Answer: 26.67 cm in front of the mirror
Calculation 12: Speed of Image in Plane Mirror

A boy runs toward a plane mirror at 2 m/s. Find the speed at which his image approaches him.

Step: Boy approaches mirror at 2 m/s, image approaches at 2 m/s

Relative speed = 2 + 2 = 4 m/s

Answer: 4 m/s

6️⃣ Applications of Reflection

Periscope

Uses two plane mirrors set at 45° so that light can be viewed over obstacles.

Periscope
The mirrors redirect light twice so the observer can see over or around obstacles.

Kaleidoscope

Uses three plane mirrors inclined at 60° to form multiple symmetrical patterns by repeated reflection.

Sextant

A navigational instrument that uses mirror reflection to measure angular separation.

Other uses

  • Concave mirrors: solar cookers, makeup mirrors, ophthalmoscopes, headlights
  • Convex mirrors: vehicle side mirrors, security mirrors
Calculation 13: Solar Cooker Application

A solar cooker uses a concave mirror of radius 2 m. Where should the cooking pot be placed for maximum heating?

f = R/2 = 2/2 = 1 m

Answer: place the pot 1 m from the mirror, at the focus

7️⃣ Quick Revision Tips

Memory aids

  • Virtual is VIP: plane mirror images are virtual; convex mirror images are always virtual
  • Remember f = R/2
  • Concave mirrors can give both real and virtual images
  • Convex mirrors always give virtual, erect, diminished images
⚠️ Common traps

Do not confuse focal length with radius, and always check whether the mirror is concave or convex before applying signs.
