JAMB Physics • Faraday’s law • Lenz’s law • induced e.m.f • generators • transformers • inductance

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains electromagnetic induction, Faraday’s law, Lenz’s law, induced e.m.f calculations, transformers, self and mutual inductance, and practical applications for JAMB success.

  • A changing magnetic flux induces an e.m.f
  • Faraday’s law gives the magnitude of induced e.m.f
  • Lenz’s law gives the direction of induced current
  • Transformers work only with changing current
🧠 Big idea

No change in magnetic flux means no induced e.m.f. Change is the key word in this chapter.

1️⃣ Introduction to Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction is the production of an electromotive force in a conductor whenever the magnetic flux linking it changes.

Michael Faraday discovered that a changing magnetic field can produce an electric current.

Changing flux induces e.m.f
Moving a magnet relative to a coil changes the flux linkage and induces an e.m.f.

2️⃣ Faraday’s Law of Electromagnetic Induction

The induced e.m.f is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage.

Faraday’s law
ℰ = -N(dΦ/dt)

Magnetic flux

Flux formula
Φ = BA cos θ
  • ℰ = induced e.m.f in volts
  • N = number of turns
  • Φ = magnetic flux
  • θ = angle between field and the normal to the coil
Calculation 1: Basic Faraday’s Law

A coil of 50 turns and area 0.02 m² is perpendicular to a field. If the magnetic flux density changes from 0.1 T to 0.4 T in 0.5 s, find the induced e.m.f.

ℰ = -N(ΔΦ/Δt) = -50[(0.4 - 0.1) × 0.02]/0.5

ℰ = -0.6 V

Answer: 0.6 V

3️⃣ Lenz’s Law

The induced current flows in such a direction as to oppose the change causing it.

  • If magnetic flux increases, the induced current opposes the increase
  • If magnetic flux decreases, the induced current opposes the decrease
🧠 Quick memory

The negative sign in Faraday’s law is there because of Lenz’s law.

Lenz’s law is really the law of conservation of energy showing up in induction.

4️⃣ Induced e.m.f in Special Cases

Rotating coil

Maximum generator e.m.f
ℰmax = NBAω

Moving conductor

Motional e.m.f
ℰ = Blv
Simple AC generator idea
A rotating coil cuts magnetic field lines continuously and produces alternating e.m.f.
Calculation 2: Rotating Coil

A rectangular coil of 100 turns, dimensions 0.1 m by 0.05 m, rotates at 50 rev/s in a 0.2 T field. Find the maximum e.m.f.

A = 0.1 × 0.05 = 0.005 m²

ω = 2πf = 100π rad/s

ℰmax = NBAω = 100 × 0.2 × 0.005 × 100π = 31.4 V

Answer: 31.4 V
Calculation 3: Moving Conductor

A conductor of length 0.5 m moves at 20 m/s perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.4 T. Find the induced e.m.f.

ℰ = Blv = 0.4 × 0.5 × 20 = 4.0 V

Answer: 4.0 V

5️⃣ Self and Mutual Inductance

Mutual inductance

When changing current in one coil induces e.m.f in another nearby coil, mutual induction occurs.

Mutual induction formula
ℰs = -M(dIp/dt)

Self inductance

When a changing current in a coil induces back e.m.f in the same coil, self-induction occurs.

Self-induction formula
ℰ = -L(dI/dt)
Inductance of a solenoid
L = (μ₀N²A)/l
Energy stored in an inductor
E = ½LI²
Calculation 4: Mutual Inductance

Two coils have mutual inductance 0.5 H. If current in the primary changes from 2 A to 8 A in 0.2 s, find the induced e.m.f in the secondary.

ℰs = -0.5[(8 - 2)/0.2] = -15 V

Answer: 15 V
Calculation 5: Self-Inductance

A coil of self-inductance 0.4 H has current changing at 5 A/s. Find the back e.m.f.

ℰ = -L(dI/dt) = -0.4 × 5 = -2.0 V

Answer: 2.0 V

7️⃣ Transformers

A transformer works by mutual induction between two coils wound on the same iron core.

Basic transformer
Alternating current in the primary creates changing flux in the core, inducing e.m.f in the secondary.
Voltage ratio
Vs/Vp = Ns/Np
Current ratio for an ideal transformer
Is/Ip = Np/Ns = Vp/Vs
Efficiency
η = (VsIs)/(VpIp) × 100%
Calculation 6: Transformer

A transformer has 1000 turns in the primary and 200 turns in the secondary. If the primary voltage is 240 V and current is 2 A, find the secondary voltage and current assuming 100% efficiency.

Vs/240 = 200/1000

Vs = 48 V

VpIp = VsIs

240 × 2 = 48 × Is

Is = 10 A

Answer: 48 V and 10 A

Step-up transformer: Ns > Np. Step-down transformer: Ns < Np.

🔟 JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • The negative sign in Faraday’s law stands for Lenz’s law
  • Maximum e.m.f occurs at the position of fastest flux change
  • Step-up transformers increase voltage and reduce current
  • Transformers do not work with direct current
  • Always check if the question needs magnitude only
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not forget to convert frequency to angular velocity using ω = 2πf, and be careful with area and time units.

1️⃣1️⃣ Quick Practice Questions

Q1. A coil of area 0.05 m² with 200 turns is placed perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.3 T. Calculate the magnetic flux linkage.

Φ = BA = 0.3 × 0.05 = 0.015 Wb

NΦ = 200 × 0.015 = 3.0 Wb-turns

Answer: 3.0 Wb-turns
Q2. An e.m.f of 12 V is induced when the magnetic flux changes at 0.6 Wb/s. How many turns are in the coil?

ℰ = N(dΦ/dt)

12 = N × 0.6

N = 20

Answer: 20 turns
Q3. A transformer is 80% efficient. If input power is 1000 W and output voltage is 200 V, find the output current.

Poutput = 0.8 × 1000 = 800 W

I = P/V = 800/200 = 4 A

Answer: 4 A
