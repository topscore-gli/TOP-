1️⃣ Core Concepts: Electrical Energy and Power

Electrical energy

Electrical energy is the work done by an electric current.

Energy formulas
E = I²Rt
E = (V²/R)t
E = VIt
E = Pt

Electrical power

Power is the rate of doing work or consuming energy.

Power formulas
P = E/t
P = I²R
P = V²/R
P = VI
Calculation 1: Power

A 240 V electric kettle draws 5 A. Find its power.

P = VI = 240 × 5 = 1200 W = 1.2 kW

Answer: 1.2 kW
Calculation 2: Energy in Joules

A 60 W bulb is on for 8 hours. Find the energy consumed in joules.

t = 8 × 3600 = 28,800 s

E = Pt = 60 × 28,800 = 1.728 × 10⁶ J

Answer: 1.728 × 10⁶ J
Calculation 3: Resistance from Power

An electric heater is rated 2000 W, 240 V. Find its resistance.

R = V²/P = 240²/2000 = 28.8 Ω

Answer: 28.8 Ω
Calculation 4: Alternative Power Formula

A current of 4 A flows through a 10 Ω resistor. Find the power dissipated.

P = I²R = 4² × 10 = 160 W

Answer: 160 W

Calculation 5: Basic kWh

A 100 W bulb is used for 10 hours. Find the energy consumed in kWh.

E = (100 × 10)/1000 = 1 kWh

Answer: 1 kWh
Calculation 6: Electricity Bill

A house uses per day: 5 bulbs of 60 W for 6 h, TV of 150 W for 4 h, refrigerator of 200 W for 24 h, and iron of 1000 W for 0.5 h. Find the monthly bill for 30 days at ₦50 per kWh.

Bulbs = 1.8 kWh

TV = 0.6 kWh

Fridge = 4.8 kWh

Iron = 0.5 kWh

Daily total = 7.7 kWh

Monthly = 7.7 × 30 = 231 kWh

Bill = 231 × 50 = ₦11,550

Answer: 231 kWh and ₦11,550
Calculation 7: kWh to Joules

Convert 5 kWh to joules.

E = 5 × 3.6 × 10⁶ = 1.8 × 10⁷ J

Answer: 1.8 × 10⁷ J
Calculation 8: Operating Time

How long will a 2 kW heater take to consume 10 kWh?

t = E/P = 10/2 = 5 h

Answer: 5 hours

3️⃣ Electric Power Transmission

Electric power is generated at power stations, stepped up to very high voltage, transmitted over long distances, and then stepped down for domestic use.

Power transmission chain
Power is transmitted at high voltage and low current to reduce losses.
Transmission power loss
Ploss = I²R

High transmission voltage means lower current for the same power, so heat loss is greatly reduced.

Calculation 9: Transmission Current

A power station generates 100 MW at 25 kV. Find the current.

I = P/V = (100 × 10⁶)/(25 × 10³) = 4000 A

Answer: 4000 A
Calculation 10: Power Loss in Transmission

This 100 MW is stepped up to 330 kV and the transmission line resistance is 5 Ω. Find the new current, power loss, and efficiency.

I = (100 × 10⁶)/(330 × 10³) = 303.03 A

Ploss = I²R = (303.03)² × 5 ≈ 459,136 W ≈ 459 kW

η = [(100,000 - 459)/100,000] × 100 ≈ 99.54%

Answer: 303.03 A, 459 kW, 99.54%
Calculation 11: Comparing Transmission Voltages

Compare the power loss when 50 MW is transmitted through a 2 Ω cable at 50 kV and 200 kV.

At 50 kV: I = 1000 A, Ploss = 2 MW

At 200 kV: I = 250 A, Ploss = 125 kW

Answer: 2 MW loss at 50 kV, 125 kW loss at 200 kV

Nichrome is used because it has high resistivity, high melting point, and resists oxidation.

Calculation 12: Joule’s Law

Find the heat produced when 3 A flows through a 20 Ω resistor for 5 minutes.

t = 5 × 60 = 300 s

H = 3² × 20 × 300 = 54,000 J

Answer: 54,000 J
Calculation 13: Water Heating Time

A 2000 W kettle heats 2 kg of water from 20°C to 100°C. Take c = 4200 J/kgK. Find the time required.

Q = mcΔT = 2 × 4200 × 80 = 672,000 J

t = E/P = 672,000/2000 = 336 s = 5.6 min

Answer: 5.6 minutes
Calculation 14: Heating Element

An electric iron draws 4 A from 240 V mains. Find its resistance and the heat produced in 10 minutes.

R = V/I = 240/4 = 60 Ω

H = I²Rt = 4² × 60 × 600 = 576,000 J

Answer: 60 Ω and 576,000 J

Calculation 15: Parallel Circuit Load

Three bulbs of 60 W, 100 W, and 40 W are connected in parallel to 240 V. Find the current through each bulb, total current, and total resistance.

I₁ = 60/240 = 0.25 A

I₂ = 100/240 = 0.417 A

I₃ = 40/240 = 0.167 A

Itotal = 0.834 A

Rtotal = V/Itotal = 240/0.834 = 287.8 Ω

Answer: 0.25 A, 0.417 A, 0.167 A; total 0.834 A; 287.8 Ω
Calculation 16: Circuit Load Limit

A 240 V lighting circuit has a 5 A fuse. Find the maximum power it can safely handle.

Pmax = VI = 240 × 5 = 1200 W

Answer: 1200 W

6️⃣ Use of Fuses

A fuse is a safety device containing a thin wire that melts when current exceeds a safe value, breaking the circuit.

Choosing fuse rating

Fuse current
I = P/V

Choose the next standard fuse rating slightly above the operating current.

Type Rating Use

Always connect the fuse in the live wire. Never bypass a fuse with metal wire or coin.

Calculation 17: Fuse Rating

An electric kettle is rated 2000 W, 240 V. Determine a suitable fuse rating.

I = P/V = 2000/240 = 8.33 A

Choose the next standard rating: 10 A or 13 A

Answer: 10 A or 13 A
Calculation 18: Multiple Appliances

A 240 V power circuit has appliances of 200 W, 150 W, 80 W, and 300 W. Find the minimum fuse rating.

Ptotal = 730 W

I = 730/240 = 3.04 A

Next standard fuse = 5 A

Answer: 5 A fuse
Calculation 19: Checking Circuit Safety

A 240 V, 15 A circuit already powers a 3000 W water heater. Can a 2000 W iron be added safely?

Iheater = 3000/240 = 12.5 A

Iiron = 2000/240 = 8.33 A

Itotal = 20.83 A

This is greater than 15 A.

Answer: No, it is not safe
Calculation 20: Fuse Wire Properties

Why should fuse wire have low melting point and relatively high resistance?

Low melting point makes it melt quickly during overload. High resistance helps it heat up sufficiently when too much current flows.

Answer: For quick melting and effective circuit protection

8️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Always remember 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J
  • For transmission, mention Ploss = I²R
  • Homes use parallel wiring, not series wiring
  • Choose the next higher standard fuse rating
  • Convert time to seconds for joules, but keep hours for kWh
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not confuse power with energy, and do not forget that high voltage reduces current and therefore reduces transmission losses.

9️⃣ Practice Questions for JAMB

The commercial unit of electrical energy is:

(a) Joule
(b) Watt
(c) Kilowatt-hour
(d) Volt

Answer: (c) Kilowatt-hour
Electric power transmission is done at high voltage mainly to:

(a) Increase current
(b) Reduce current and power loss
(c) Increase resistance
(d) Avoid transformers

Answer: (b) Reduce current and power loss
A fuse should be connected in the:

(a) Earth wire
(b) Neutral wire
(c) Live wire
(d) Any wire

Answer: (c) Live wire
