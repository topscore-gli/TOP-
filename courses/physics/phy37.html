🧪 Conduction Through Liquids and Gases

JAMB Physics • electrolysis • Faraday’s laws • electroplating • gas discharge • practical applications

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains conduction of electricity through liquids and gases, electrolysis, Faraday’s laws, electroplating, gas discharge, and common applications for JAMB success.

  • Liquids conduct by movement of ions
  • Gases conduct only after ionization
  • Electrolysis causes chemical decomposition
  • 96,500 C = 1 Faraday
🧠 Big idea

Conduction in liquids and gases is very different from conduction in metals because it depends on ions, electrons, and chemical change.

Solid ionic compounds like sodium chloride do not conduct because the ions are fixed in place. They conduct only when molten or dissolved.

2️⃣ The Concept of Electrolysis

Electrolysis is the decomposition of a chemical compound by passing direct current through its molten state or solution.

Electrolytic cell setup

  • Anode (+): positive electrode, oxidation occurs here
  • Cathode (−): negative electrode, reduction occurs here
  • Electrolyte: liquid containing ions
  • D.C. source: drives the ions to the electrodes
Simple electrolytic cell
Positive ions move to the cathode, while negative ions move to the anode.
🧠 Memory aid

CAT-ions go to the CAT-hode.

3️⃣ Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis

First law

The mass of substance deposited is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity passed.

Faraday’s first law
m = ZIt = ZQ
Electrochemical equivalent
Z = M/(n × 96,500)

Second law

When the same quantity of electricity passes through different electrolytes, the masses deposited are proportional to their equivalent masses.

Faraday’s second law
m₁/m₂ = E₁/E₂
E = M/n
Calculation 1: Mass Deposited

Calculate the mass of copper deposited when 2 A flows through CuSO₄ for 30 minutes. Take Cu = 64, n = 2.

Q = It = 2 × (30 × 60) = 3600 C

Z = 64/(2 × 96,500)

m = ZQ = (64 × 3600)/193,000 = 1.19 g

Answer: 1.19 g
Calculation 2: Using Second Law

If 0.54 g of silver is deposited in the same time as 0.16 g of copper, find the equivalent mass of copper. Take silver equivalent mass as 108.

0.16/0.54 = Ecu/108

Ecu = (0.16 × 108)/0.54 = 32

Answer: 32

⚡ Part B: Conduction Through Gases

1. Discharge Through Gases

Gases are normally insulators because they have no free ions or electrons. They conduct only after ionization.

How gases become conductors

  • High voltage: ionizes gas atoms
  • Low pressure: allows charges to move more easily
  • Ionization: produces positive ions and free electrons
Discharge tube idea
At low pressure and high voltage, gases glow and conduct because of ionization.

Observations at different pressures

📝 Quick Practice Questions

Q1. Calculate the mass of silver deposited when 3 A flows for 20 minutes. Take Ag = 108, n = 1.

Q = It = 3 × 1200 = 3600 C

Z = 108/96,500

m = ZQ ≈ 4.02 g

Answer: 4.02 g
Q2. How many Faradays are required to deposit 1 mole of Al from Al³⁺?

Since aluminium has valency 3, it needs 3 moles of electrons.

Answer: 3 Faradays
Q3. Why does NaCl conduct when molten but not when solid?

In solid NaCl, ions are fixed in a crystal lattice. When molten, the ions become free to move and carry charge.

Answer: Because the ions become mobile when molten
