Topics

Measurements and Units

Standardized reference systems for quantifying physical properties to ensure consistent, accurate communication in science and everyday use.

Scalars and Vectors

Scalars are quantities with magnitude only (e.g., mass, temperature); vectors have both magnitude and direction (e.g., velocity, force).

Motion

The change in position of an object over time relative to a reference point.

Gravitational Field

A region of space where a mass experiences a force due to another mass's gravitational attraction.

Equilibrium of Forces

A state where the net force on an object is zero, resulting in no acceleration (at rest or moving at constant velocity)..

Friction

The resistive force opposing relative motion between two surfaces in contact.

Work, Energy and Power

Work is force causing displacement; energy is the capacity to do work; power is the rate of doing work.

Simple Machines

Basic mechanical devices (lever, pulley, wheel, inclined plane, wedge, screw) that multiply force or change its direction to make work easier.

Elasticity

A material's ability to deform under stress and return to its original shape when the stress is removed.

Pressure

Force per unit area exerted perpendicular to a surface.

Liquids at Rest

The study of stationary fluids, focusing on pressure, density, and buoyancy forces in equilibrium.

Temperature and Its Measurement

A measure of the average kinetic energy of particles in a substance, quantified using thermometric scales and instruments.

Quantity of Heat

The total thermal energy transferred between objects due to temperature difference, measured in joules or calories.

Change of State

The physical transition of matter between solid, liquid, and gas phases through melting, freezing, vaporization, condensation, or sublimation.

Thermal Expansion

The increase in a material's size (length, area, or volume) due to a rise in temperature.

Gas Laws

Mathematical relationships (Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, Combined, Ideal) describing how gas pressure, volume, and temperature interrelate.

Vapours

The gaseous form of a substance that exists below its critical temperature and can be liquefied by pressure alone.

Structure of Matter and Kinetic Theory

The particle model of matter, explaining properties of solids, liquids, and gases through the random motion and interactions of atoms and molecules.

Heat Transfer

The movement of thermal energy through conduction, convection, or radiation from a hotter to a cooler body.

Waves

Propagating disturbances that transfer energy through a medium or space without permanent displacement of matter.

Propagation of Sound Waves

The transmission of longitudinal mechanical waves through a medium via compressions and rarefactions of particles.

Characteristics of Sound Waves

Properties including pitch (frequency), loudness (amplitude), quality (timbre), and speed, which determine how sound is perceived and behaves.

Light Energy

Electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye, enabling vision and photosynthesis, traveling in waves and exhibiting properties of both waves and particles.

Reflection of Light at Plane and Curved Surfaces

The change in light direction when it strikes a surface, governed by the law of reflection, forming images through mirrors.

Refraction of Light Through Plane and Curved Surfaces

The bending of light as it passes between media of different densities, described by Snell's law, causing image formation through lenses.

Optical Instruments

Devices (microscopes, telescopes, cameras, eyes) that use lenses and mirrors to manipulate light for enhanced vision or image capture.

Dispersion of Light, Colours and Electromagnetic Spectrum

The separation of white light into constituent colours by wavelength, and the full range of electromagnetic radiation from radio waves to gamma rays.

Electrostatics

The study of stationary electric charges, their forces, fields, and potentials.

Capacitors

Devices that store electric charge and energy in an electric field between two conductive plates separated by an insulator.

Electric Cells

Devices that convert chemical energy into electrical energy through electrochemical reactions to produce a continuous electric current.

Current Electricity

The flow of electric charge through a conductor, governed by voltage, resistance, and Ohm's law.

Electrical Energy and Power

Energy consumed or work done by electric current over time; power is the rate of this energy transfer.

Magnets and Magnetic Fields

Magnets produce invisible fields of magnetic force that exert attraction or repulsion on ferromagnetic materials and moving charges.

Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor in a Magnetic Field

A current-carrying wire experiences a mechanical force perpendicular to both the current direction and magnetic field, described by Fleming's left-hand rule.

Electromagnetic Induction

The generation of an electromotive force (voltage) in a conductor by a changing magnetic field or relative motion between a conductor and a magnet.

Simple A.C. Circuits

Electrical circuits with alternating current, involving resistors, capacitors, and inductors, characterized by periodically reversing voltage and current.

Conduction of Electricity Through Liquids

The flow of electric current through electrolytes via the movement of ions, enabling electrolysis and electrochemical processes.

Elementary Modern Physics

Fundamental concepts of 20th-century physics including quantum theory, atomic structure, radioactivity, and special relativity.

Introductory Electronics

The study and application of semiconductor devices (diodes, transistors, integrated circuits) to control and manipulate electric signals in circuits.

Introduction to Fibre Optics and Lasers

Light transmission through thin glass/plastic fibres via total internal reflection, and the generation of coherent, monochromatic light through stimulated emission.
