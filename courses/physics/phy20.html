🌊 Waves

JAMB Physics • wave motion • frequency • wavelength • phase • progressive waves • beats • Doppler effect

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains how waves are produced and propagated, how they transfer energy, the difference between particle motion and wave motion, wave equations, wave types, stationary and progressive waves, interference, beats, and the Doppler effect.

  • Waves transfer energy without transferring matter
  • The key wave equation is v = fλ
  • Progressive waves travel, standing waves do not
  • Beats come from two close frequencies interfering
🧠 Big idea

In wave motion, the disturbance travels through space or a medium, while the particles of the medium only oscillate around their fixed positions.

1️⃣ Production and Propagation of Waves

What is wave motion?

A wave is a disturbance that travels through a medium or space and transfers energy from one point to another without any overall transfer of matter.

Think of spectators doing the wave in a stadium: the people do not move round the stadium, but the disturbance does.

Vibrating systems as sources

  • Plucked guitar string → sound wave
  • Dropped pebble in water → ripple
  • Radio transmitter → electromagnetic wave

2️⃣ Fundamental Wave Equations

Main wave equation
v = fλ
  • v = wave speed in m/s
  • f = frequency in Hz
  • λ = wavelength in m

Phase difference

Phase difference is the difference in position between two points on a wave at the same time.

  • 0° or 0 rad = in phase
  • 180° or π rad = out of phase
  • 360° or 2π rad = one complete cycle
Phase difference formula
φ = (2π/λ)Δx

Wave number

Wave number
k = 2π/λ

Angular frequency

Angular frequency
ω = 2πf

Progressive wave equation

Progressive wave
y = A sin(ωt ± kx)
y = A sin[(2π/λ)(vt ± x)]

4️⃣ Characteristics and Properties of Waves

Reflection

Reflection is the bouncing back of a wave from a surface.

Refraction

Refraction is the bending of a wave when it enters a new medium and its speed changes.

Diffraction

Diffraction is the spreading out of waves after passing through an opening or around an obstacle.

Polarization

Polarization is the restriction of vibrations to one plane. Only transverse waves can be polarized.

Interference and superposition

When two waves overlap, the resultant displacement is the algebraic sum of the individual displacements.

  • Constructive interference: waves in phase, amplitude increases
  • Destructive interference: waves out of phase, amplitude decreases

5️⃣ Beats and Doppler Effect

Beats

Beats are produced when two waves of slightly different frequencies interfere, giving alternating loud and soft sounds.

Beat frequency
fbeat = |f₁ - f₂|
  • Used in tuning musical instruments
  • Also useful in radar and some medical applications

Doppler effect

The Doppler effect is the apparent change in frequency when there is relative motion between the source and the observer.

Situation Observed Frequency Example
Source approachingHigherAmbulance coming closer
Source recedingLowerAmbulance moving away
Observer movingChanges accordinglyListener in a moving car

6️⃣ Essential Calculations for JAMB

Calculation 1: Basic Wave Equation

A wave has frequency 50 Hz and wavelength 2 m. Find its speed.

Step: v = fλ = 50 × 2 = 100 m/s

Answer: 100 m/s
Calculation 2: Finding Wavelength

A radio wave travels at 3 × 10⁸ m/s with frequency 100 MHz. Find its wavelength.

Step 1: f = 100 × 10⁶ = 10⁸ Hz

Step 2: λ = v/f = (3 × 10⁸)/(10⁸) = 3 m

Answer: 3 m
Calculation 3: Progressive Wave Parameters

A wave is given by y = 0.5 sin[2π(4t - 0.5x)]. Find amplitude, frequency, wavelength, and speed.

Compare with: y = A sin[2π(ft - x/λ)]

Amplitude: A = 0.5 m

Frequency: f = 4 Hz

Since 0.5x = x/2, λ = 2 m

Speed: v = fλ = 4 × 2 = 8 m/s

Answer: 0.5 m, 4 Hz, 2 m, 8 m/s
Calculation 4: Phase Difference

Two points are 0.5 m apart on a wave of wavelength 2 m. Find the phase difference.

Step: φ = (2π/λ)Δx = (2π/2) × 0.5 = π/2 rad

Answer: π/2 rad or 90°
Calculation 5: Beat Frequency

Two tuning forks have frequencies 256 Hz and 260 Hz. Find the beat frequency.

Step: fbeat = |256 - 260| = 4 Hz

Answer: 4 Hz
Calculation 6: Standing Waves on a String

A string of length 1 m is fixed at both ends. If wave speed is 100 m/s, find the fundamental frequency.

Step 1: For the fundamental, λ = 2L = 2 m

Step 2: f = v/λ = 100/2 = 50 Hz

Answer: 50 Hz
Calculation 7: Wave Number and Angular Frequency

A wave has wavelength 0.5 m and frequency 100 Hz. Find wave number, angular frequency, and speed.

Wave number: k = 2π/0.5 = 4π rad/m

Angular frequency: ω = 2π × 100 = 200π rad/s

Speed: v = fλ = 100 × 0.5 = 50 m/s

Answer: 4π rad/m, 200π rad/s, 50 m/s
Calculation 8: Sound Wave Parameters

A sound wave in air has frequency 440 Hz. If speed of sound is 340 m/s, find wavelength, period, and number of oscillations in 2 s.

Wavelength: λ = 340/440 = 0.773 m

Period: T = 1/440 = 0.00227 s

Oscillations: N = ft = 440 × 2 = 880

Answer: 0.773 m, 0.00227 s, 880 oscillations
