⏲️ Pressure

JAMB Physics • atmospheric pressure • liquid pressure • barometer • manometer • Pascal’s principle

🎯 Learning Objective

By the end of this chapter, you should understand atmospheric pressure, pressure in liquids, Pascal’s principle, barometers, manometers, and common pressure calculations for JAMB.

  • Pressure tells how much force acts on a given area
  • Pressure in liquids depends on density, gravity, and depth
  • Atmospheric pressure decreases with height
  • Hydraulic machines work by Pascal’s principle
🧠 Big idea

Pressure becomes large when force is concentrated on a small area, and liquid pressure becomes larger as depth increases.

1️⃣ Basic Definition of Pressure

Pressure is the force acting normally per unit area.

Basic pressure formula
P = F/A
P = pressure
F = force
A = area

The SI unit of pressure is the pascal (Pa).

1 pascal means 1 newton per square metre.

2️⃣ Atmospheric Pressure

Atmospheric pressure is the pressure exerted by the weight of air on any surface in contact with it.

Common units

  • Pascal (Pa)
  • mmHg
  • atm
  • bar

Pressure-measuring instruments

Instrument How it works
Simple mercury barometer Glass tube filled with mercury inverted in a dish. Atmospheric pressure supports the mercury column.
Aneroid barometer Uses a sealed evacuated metal box that expands and contracts with pressure changes.
Manometer U-shaped tube containing liquid used to measure pressure difference.

Variation with height

Atmospheric pressure decreases as altitude increases because there is less air above you.

🧠 Easy memory

Higher altitude → less air above → lower atmospheric pressure.

Barometer as altimeter

Since pressure changes with height, barometers can be used to measure altitude, especially in aircraft and mountain climbing.

3️⃣ Pressure in Liquids

The pressure in a liquid at depth depends on the liquid’s density, gravity, and vertical depth.

Pressure in a liquid
P = ρgh
ρ = density of liquid
g = acceleration due to gravity
h = depth

Properties of liquid pressure

  • Pressure increases with depth
  • Pressure depends on density of liquid
  • Pressure acts equally in all directions
  • At the same depth in the same liquid, pressure is the same
  • Pressure is independent of the container’s shape
Same depth, same pressure
Different containers can give the same pressure at the same depth if they contain the same liquid.

4️⃣ Pascal’s Principle

Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally and undiminished to every part of the fluid and to the walls of the container.

Hydraulic relation
P₁ = P₂
F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂

Applications

  • Hydraulic press
  • Hydraulic lift
  • Car jack
  • Vehicle braking system
Hydraulic press idea
A small force on the small piston can produce a much larger force on the large piston.

5️⃣ Essential JAMB Calculations

Calculation 1: Absolute Pressure at Depth

Find the absolute pressure at the bottom of a lake 32.8 m deep. Take ρ = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 m/s², atmospheric pressure = 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa.

Step 1: Liquid pressure = ρgh = 1000 × 10 × 32.8 = 3.28 × 10⁵ Pa

Step 2: Absolute pressure = P_atm + ρgh

Step 3: 1.01×10⁵ + 3.28×10⁵ = 4.29×10⁵ Pa

Answer: 4.29 × 10⁵ Pa
Calculation 2: Total Pressure in Ocean

The atmospheric pressure is 1.3 × 10⁶ N/m². Find total pressure at the bottom of an ocean 10 m deep. Take ρ = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 m/s².

Step 1: Liquid pressure = 1000 × 10 × 10 = 1.0 × 10⁵ N/m²

Step 2: Total = 1.3×10⁶ + 0.1×10⁶ = 1.4×10⁶ N/m²

Answer: 1.4 × 10⁶ N/m²
Calculation 3: Depth for One Atmosphere Change

At what depth in sea water will pressure change equal one atmosphere? Take ρ = 1013 kg/m³, g = 10 m/s², 1 atm = 1.01 × 10⁵ N/m².

Step 1: h = P/(ρg)

Step 2: h = (1.01×10⁵)/(1013×10)

Step 3: h ≈ 9.97 m ≈ 10.0 m

Answer: 10.0 m
Calculation 4: Hydraulic Press

A pump piston exerts pressure of 100 Pa on a liquid. What force acts on a second piston of area 3 m²?

Step 1: P = F/A

Step 2: F = PA = 100 × 3 = 300 N

Answer: 300 N
Calculation 5: Hydraulic Car Lift

A small piston has radius 0.05 m and a large piston has radius 0.25 m. If 100 N is applied to the small piston, what load can be lifted?

Step 1: A₁ = π(0.05)², A₂ = π(0.25)²

Step 2: F₂ = F₁(A₂/A₁)

Step 3: F₂ = 100 × 25 = 2500 N

Answer: 2500 N = 2.5 kN
Calculation 6: Pressure in Mercury Column

Find the pressure at the bottom of a mercury column 760 mm high. Take ρ = 13600 kg/m³, g = 9.8 m/s².

Step 1: Convert height: 760 mm = 0.760 m

Step 2: P = ρgh = 13600 × 9.8 × 0.760

Step 3: P ≈ 1.013 × 10⁵ Pa

Answer: 1.013 × 10⁵ Pa
Calculation 7: Comparing Two Liquids

Liquid X has density 800 kg/m³ and liquid Y has density 1200 kg/m³. At what depth in X will pressure equal the pressure at 0.6 m in Y?

Step 1: For equal pressures, ρₓghₓ = ρᵧghᵧ

Step 2: 800hₓ = 1200 × 0.6

Step 3: hₓ = 0.9 m

Answer: 0.9 m

7️⃣ Quick Study Tips

How to score better in pressure questions

  • Always convert to SI units before calculating
  • Use vertical depth, not slant length
  • Remember water density = 1000 kg/m³
  • Remember mercury density = 13600 kg/m³
  • Add atmospheric pressure only when absolute pressure is required
  • Draw a small diagram of the liquid column when stuck

8️⃣ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing atmospheric pressure with liquid pressure
  • Using slant height instead of vertical depth
  • Mixing density values for water and mercury
  • Forgetting that pressure acts in all directions
  • Forgetting to add atmospheric pressure for absolute pressure
