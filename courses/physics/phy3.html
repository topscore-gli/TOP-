2️⃣ Scalars vs Vectors

This is a quick reminder from the previous chapter.

  • Scalars: speed, distance, mass, time
  • Vectors: velocity, displacement, acceleration, force

Important: Speed is scalar, but velocity is vector. Distance is scalar, but displacement is vector.

4️⃣ The Big Five Equations of Motion

These are the most important formulas for uniformly accelerated motion.

Must-memorize equations
v = u + at
s = ½(u + v)t
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as
s = vt - ½at²

Quick usage guide

  • If s is not given, try v = u + at
  • If a is not given, try s = ½(u+v)t
  • If v is not given, try s = ut + ½at²
  • If t is not given, try v² = u² + 2as

5️⃣ Worked Examples in Linear Motion

Example 1: Basic Acceleration

A car starts from rest and accelerates at 2 m/s² for 10 s. Find the distance covered.

Given: u = 0, a = 2, t = 10

Step 1: Use s = ut + ½at²

Step 2: s = (0)(10) + ½(2)(10)²

Step 3: s = 100 m

Answer: 100 m
Example 2: Final Velocity

A train moving at 20 m/s accelerates at 0.5 m/s² for 30 s. Find the final velocity.

Step 1: Use v = u + at

Step 2: v = 20 + (0.5)(30)

Step 3: v = 35 m/s

Answer: 35 m/s
Example 3: Motion Under Gravity

A stone is thrown vertically upward with velocity 30 m/s. Find: (a) maximum height (b) time to reach maximum height. Take g = 10 m/s².

At maximum height: v = 0

(a) Use v² = u² + 2as

0 = 30² + 2(-10)s

0 = 900 - 20s so s = 45 m

(b) Use v = u + at

0 = 30 - 10t so t = 3 s

Answers: 45 m and 3 s

7️⃣ Projectile Motion

A projectile is an object given an initial velocity and allowed to move freely under gravity.

Horizontal Projectile

Formulas for horizontal projection
T = √(2h/g)
R = uT = u√(2h/g)
v = √(u² + 2gh)

Projectile at an angle

Formulas for angled projection
T = (2usinθ)/g
H = (u²sin²θ)/(2g)
R = (u²sin2θ)/g
Rmax = u²/g at 45°
Projectile path
A projectile has constant horizontal velocity and vertical motion controlled by gravity.
Example 4: Horizontal Projectile

A ball is projected horizontally from a 45 m high cliff with velocity 10 m/s. Take g = 10 m/s².

(a) Time of flight = √(2h/g) = √(2×45/10) = √9 = 3 s

(b) Horizontal distance = uT = 10 × 3 = 30 m

Answers: 3 s and 30 m

Example 5: Force Calculation

A 5 kg mass is accelerated from rest to 10 m/s in 2 s. Find the force.

Step 1: a = (v-u)/t = (10-0)/2 = 5 m/s²

Step 2: F = ma = 5 × 5 = 25 N

Answer: 25 N
Example 6: Connected Bodies

Two masses 3 kg and 2 kg are connected over a smooth pulley. Find the acceleration. Take g = 10 m/s².

Step 1: Net force = (3×10) - (2×10) = 10 N

Step 2: Total mass = 3 + 2 = 5 kg

Step 3: a = F/m = 10/5 = 2 m/s²

Answer: 2 m/s²

Conservation of momentum
m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
If they stick together: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁+m₂)v
Example 7: Collision

A 2 kg mass moving at 5 m/s collides with a stationary 3 kg mass. They move together after collision.

Step 1: Initial momentum = (2×5) + (3×0) = 10

Step 2: Combined mass = 2 + 3 = 5 kg

Step 3: 10 = 5v

Step 4: v = 2 m/s

Answer: 2 m/s

Circular motion
In circular motion, velocity is tangential but centripetal force is always directed toward the centre.
Example 8: Centripetal Force

A 0.5 kg stone is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1 m at 2 rev/s. Find the centripetal force.

Step 1: ω = 2πf = 2π × 2 = 4π rad/s

Step 2: Fc = mω²r = 0.5 × (4π)² × 1

Step 3: Fc = 8π² ≈ 79 N

Answer: about 79 N

Simple harmonic motion
In SHM, the object moves to and fro about the equilibrium position. Velocity is maximum at the centre, acceleration is maximum at the extremes.
Example 9: Simple Pendulum

A simple pendulum has period 2 s. Find its length. Take g = π² m/s².

Step 1: Use T = 2π√(L/g)

Step 2: 2 = 2π√(L/π²)

Step 3: 1 = √L

Step 4: L = 1 m

Answer: 1 m

1️⃣3️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

How to avoid common mistakes

  • Draw diagrams for projectiles and circular motion
  • Convert km/h to m/s when necessary
  • For graphs, slope = rate of change and area = total effect
  • Maximum projectile range occurs at 45°
  • Total momentum before collision = total momentum after collision
  • In SHM, velocity is maximum at equilibrium
  • In SHM, acceleration is maximum at the extreme points
  • Centripetal force always points toward the centre

1️⃣4️⃣ Practice Questions

1. A car accelerates uniformly from 10 m/s to 30 m/s over 100 m. Find the acceleration.
Answer: 4 m/s²
2. A ball is thrown upward at 20 m/s. How long will it take to return to the thrower?
Answer: 4 s
3. Two bodies of masses 4 kg and 6 kg collide and stick together. The 4 kg body moves at 3 m/s and the 6 kg body moves at 2 m/s in the opposite direction. Find their final velocity.
Answer: 0 m/s
4. A stone of mass 2 kg is whirled in a vertical circle of radius 0.5 m at 4 m/s. Calculate the tension at the lowest point.
Answer: 78.4 N
