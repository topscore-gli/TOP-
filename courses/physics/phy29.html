🔋 Capacitors

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains what capacitors are, how capacitance is calculated, factors affecting capacitance, capacitor arrangements, and energy stored in capacitors.

  • A capacitor stores charge and electrical energy
  • Capacitance is defined by C = Q/V
  • Dielectrics increase capacitance
  • Energy is stored in the electric field between plates
🧠 Big idea

A capacitor is like a temporary store for charge, and the bigger the capacitance, the more charge it can hold per volt.

1️⃣ What is a Capacitor?

A capacitor is a device used to store electric charge and electrical energy in an electric field.

Basic structure

  • Two conducting plates
  • An insulating material between them called the dielectric
  • One plate becomes positively charged and the other negatively charged when connected to a battery
Simple capacitor structure
A capacitor consists of two plates separated by an insulating medium.

2️⃣ Capacitance

Capacitance is the ability of a capacitor to store charge per unit potential difference.

Basic capacitance formula
C = Q/V
  • C = capacitance in farads (F)
  • Q = charge in coulombs (C)
  • V = voltage in volts (V)

Common units

  • 1 μF = 10⁻⁶ F
  • 1 nF = 10⁻⁹ F
  • 1 pF = 10⁻¹² F
💡 Quick note

1 farad is very large, so most practical capacitors are in microfarads, nanofarads, or picofarads.

3️⃣ Parallel Plate Capacitor

This is the most important capacitor type for JAMB. It has two large parallel plates separated by a distance d.

Parallel plate capacitor formula
C = εA/d

Where:

  • ε = ε₀εᵣ
  • ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m
  • εᵣ = relative permittivity
  • A = plate area
  • d = separation of plates
Medium Formula Note
Air/VacuumC = ε₀A/dεᵣ = 1
With dielectricC = ε₀εᵣA/dεᵣ > 1

Calculation 1: Basic Capacitance

A parallel plate capacitor has area 0.04 m² and plate separation 2 mm in air. Find the capacitance.

Convert: 2 mm = 0.002 m

C = ε₀A/d = (8.85 × 10⁻¹² × 0.04)/0.002

C = 1.77 × 10⁻¹⁰ F = 177 pF

Answer: 177 pF
Calculation 2: Effect of Dielectric

A capacitor has capacitance 50 pF in air. What is its capacitance when filled with wax of relative permittivity 2.2?

Cnew = Cair × εᵣ = 50 × 2.2

Cnew = 110 pF

Answer: 110 pF

4️⃣ Capacitors in Series and Parallel

Series connection

In series:

  • Charge is the same on all capacitors
  • Total voltage is shared
Series formula
1/Ceq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃ + ...

The equivalent capacitance in series is always less than the smallest individual capacitor.

Capacitors in series
In series, the same charge flows onto each capacitor.

Parallel connection

In parallel:

  • Voltage is the same across all capacitors
  • Total charge is the sum of charges on each capacitor
Parallel formula
Ceq = C₁ + C₂ + C₃ + ...

The equivalent capacitance in parallel is always greater than the largest individual capacitor.

Capacitors in parallel
In parallel, each capacitor has the same potential difference across it.
Calculation 3: Series Combination

Three capacitors of 2 μF, 4 μF, and 6 μF are in series across 12 V. Find the equivalent capacitance and the charge on the 4 μF capacitor.

1/Ceq = 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/6 = 11/12

Ceq = 12/11 ≈ 1.09 μF

Q = CeqV = 1.09 × 10⁻⁶ × 12 = 13.09 μC

In series, all capacitors have the same charge.

Answer: 1.09 μF and 13.09 μC
Calculation 4: Parallel Combination

Two capacitors of 3 μF and 6 μF are connected in parallel across 9 V. Find the total energy stored.

Ceq = 3 + 6 = 9 μF

E = ½CV² = 0.5 × 9 × 10⁻⁶ × 9²

E = 364.5 × 10⁻⁶ J

Answer: 364.5 μJ
Calculation 5: Mixed Combination

C₁ = 6 μF and C₂ = 3 μF are in series, and that combination is in parallel with C₃ = 4 μF. Find the equivalent capacitance.

1/Cseries = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/2

Cseries = 2 μF

Ceq = 2 + 4 = 6 μF

Answer: 6 μF

5️⃣ Energy Stored in a Capacitor

A charged capacitor stores electrical potential energy in its electric field.

Energy formulas
E = ½QV
E = ½CV²
E = Q²/2C

8️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Series capacitors: same charge
  • Parallel capacitors: same voltage
  • Dielectric insertion increases capacitance
  • Series result is smaller than the smallest capacitor
  • Parallel result is greater than the biggest capacitor

If the battery stays connected, voltage remains constant. If disconnected, charge remains constant.

⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not mix up series and parallel rules, and always convert mm to m and μF to F before calculating.

9️⃣ Practice Questions for JAMB

The unit of capacitance is:

(a) Volt
(b) Farad
(c) Ohm
(d) Coulomb

Answer: (b) Farad
For capacitors in parallel, the same quantity across each capacitor is:

(a) Charge
(b) Current
(c) Voltage
(d) Energy

Answer: (c) Voltage
In a parallel plate capacitor, increasing plate separation will:

(a) Increase capacitance
(b) Decrease capacitance
(c) Keep capacitance constant
(d) Double capacitance

Answer: (b) Decrease capacitance
