⚛️ Structure of Matter and Kinetic Theory

JAMB Physics • atoms • molecules • Brownian motion • diffusion • surface tension • capillarity • gas theory

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains atoms and molecules, molecular theory, Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, capillarity, adhesion, cohesion, angle of contact, and how kinetic theory explains gases and phase changes.

  • Atoms are the basic units of elements
  • Molecules are groups of atoms that can exist independently
  • Molecular motion explains many everyday effects
  • Kinetic theory links temperature to particle motion
🧠 Big idea

Matter is made of tiny moving particles, and many visible physical effects are explained by how these particles move and interact.

2️⃣ Molecular Theory

Molecular theory says matter is made of tiny particles that are always moving and interacting. This helps explain several physical phenomena.

a) Brownian Motion

Brownian motion is the random zigzag motion of tiny particles suspended in a fluid.

  • Caused by irregular collisions with fluid molecules
  • Shows that molecules are in constant motion
  • Observed by Robert Brown
Brownian motion
The visible suspended particle moves randomly because invisible molecules strike it unevenly.

b) Diffusion

Diffusion is the spreading of molecules from a region of high concentration to a region of low concentration until they are evenly distributed.

  • Examples: perfume smell spreading, ink in water, cooking aroma
  • Faster at higher temperature
  • Lighter molecules diffuse faster
  • Fastest in gases, slower in liquids, slowest in solids
Diffusion
Particles spread from crowded regions to less crowded regions until the mixture becomes uniform.

c) Surface Tension

Surface tension is the force acting per unit length at right angles to an imaginary line on the liquid surface.

Molecules on the surface experience a net inward pull due to cohesion, making the surface act like a stretched skin.

d) Capillarity

Capillarity is the rise or fall of a liquid in a narrow tube due to the balance between adhesive and cohesive forces.

e) Adhesion vs Cohesion

Property Definition Example
AdhesionAttraction between unlike moleculesWater sticking to glass
CohesionAttraction between like moleculesWater droplets forming

f) Angle of Contact

The angle of contact is the angle between the tangent to the liquid surface and the solid surface, measured inside the liquid.

  • Water in glass gives an acute angle, so it wets the surface
  • Mercury in glass gives an obtuse angle, so it does not wet the surface

4️⃣ Kinetic Theory of Gases

The kinetic theory explains gases in terms of particles moving randomly and continuously.

Assumptions of kinetic theory

  • Gas molecules are tiny particles in constant random motion
  • The volume of the molecules is negligible compared with container volume
  • There are no intermolecular forces except during collisions
  • Collisions are perfectly elastic
  • Average kinetic energy is proportional to absolute temperature
🧠 Key rule

Higher absolute temperature means greater average kinetic energy of molecules.

5️⃣ Explaining Physical Phenomena with Kinetic Theory

a) Pressure exerted by a gas

Gas pressure is produced when molecules collide with the walls of the container.

Pressure formula
P = ⅓ρc²

Pressure increases when:

  • There are more molecules
  • Temperature increases
  • Volume decreases

b) Boyle’s law explanation

At constant temperature, reducing volume makes molecules hit the walls more often, so pressure increases.

Boyle’s law
P ∝ 1/V
P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

c) Charles’s law explanation

At constant pressure, increasing temperature makes molecules move faster, so the gas expands and volume increases.

Charles’s law
V ∝ T
V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

d) Melting

When heated, particles in a solid vibrate more strongly until they break free from fixed positions and form a liquid.

e) Boiling

At boiling point, molecules gain enough energy to overcome intermolecular forces and form vapour bubbles throughout the liquid.

f) Vaporization / Evaporation

Evaporation happens at the surface at any temperature. Fast molecules escape and remove energy, producing cooling.

g) Temperature changes

Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of molecules.

  • Higher temperature → faster molecules
  • Lower temperature → slower molecules

6️⃣ Sample Calculation

Charles’s Law Application

A gas occupies 300 cm³ at 27°C. What is its volume at 127°C if pressure is constant?

Step 1: Convert to Kelvin

T₁ = 27 + 273 = 300 K

T₂ = 127 + 273 = 400 K

Step 2: Use V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

300/300 = V₂/400

V₂ = 400 cm³

Answer: 400 cm³

8️⃣ Common JAMB Exam Traps

  • Do not confuse an atom with a molecule: O is an atom, O₂ is a molecule
  • Do not confuse diffusion with osmosis
  • Evaporation is different from boiling
  • Adhesion is between unlike molecules; cohesion is between like molecules
  • Kinetic theory assumes negligible molecular volume and no intermolecular forces for ideal gases
