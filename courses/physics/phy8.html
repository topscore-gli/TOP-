📋 What is a Simple Machine?

A simple machine is a basic device that makes work easier by changing the size, direction, or speed of a force.

It does not create energy. It only helps us use our effort more effectively.

Main idea: A machine does not reduce total work. It only makes the work easier to do.

Basic work idea
Work = Force × Distance
  • The force you apply is called the effort
  • The heavy object moved is called the load
  • Machines make work easier by trading force for distance
🧠 Easy picture

If you cannot lift something directly, a machine lets you use a smaller force over a longer distance.

1️⃣ Types of Simple Machines

There are six main simple machines.

1. Lever

A lever is a rigid bar that turns about a fixed point called the fulcrum.

  • First class lever: fulcrum in the middle, e.g. seesaw
  • Second class lever: load in the middle, e.g. wheelbarrow
  • Third class lever: effort in the middle, e.g. tweezers

2. Wheel and axle

This is a large wheel fixed to a smaller axle. Turning the wheel helps turn the axle with less effort.

Example: doorknob, steering wheel.

3. Pulley

A pulley is a wheel with a rope or chain passing over it.

It may change the direction of force or multiply force.

4. Inclined plane

An inclined plane is a sloping surface used to raise loads with less effort.

Example: ramp.

5. Wedge

A wedge is like two inclined planes joined together. It is used for cutting or splitting.

Example: knife, axe.

6. Screw

A screw is an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder.

It changes turning motion into forward motion.

Example: screw jack, bottle cap.

Quick recognition trick

  • If it pivots → lever
  • If it uses a rope over a wheel → pulley
  • If it is a slope → inclined plane
  • If it cuts → wedge
  • If it twists → screw

2️⃣ Mechanical Advantage (MA)

Mechanical advantage tells us how much a machine multiplies force.

Formula for MA
MA = Load / Effort

It has no unit.

Meaning of MA

If a machine has MA = 4, it means the machine makes your effort 4 times more effective.

So an effort of 100 N can lift a load of 400 N.

Lever formula

For a lever
MA = Effort arm / Load arm

Inclined plane formula

For an inclined plane
MA = Load / Effort

The bigger the MA, the easier the machine makes the work.

3️⃣ Velocity Ratio (VR)

Velocity ratio tells us how far the effort moves compared to how far the load moves.

Formula for VR
VR = Distance moved by effort / Distance moved by load

It has no unit.

Meaning of VR

If VR = 5, the effort moves 5 times the distance moved by the load.

Inclined plane

For inclined plane
VR = Length of plane / Height

Pulley

For pulley system
VR = Number of supporting ropes

Screw

For screw jack
VR = 2πr / p
r = radius of handle path
p = pitch of screw
🧠 Important difference

MA deals with forces. VR deals with distances.

4️⃣ Efficiency of Machines

Efficiency tells us how well a machine converts input work into useful output work.

Efficiency formula
Efficiency = (Useful work output / Work input) × 100%

Since work output and work input can be linked to MA and VR:

Most common machine formula
Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%
  • Efficiency is always less than 100% for real machines
  • This is because of friction and energy losses
  • For an ideal machine, efficiency = 100%

For a perfect machine: MA = VR

For a real machine: MA < VR

5️⃣ Why Machines Are Not 100% Efficient

Some energy is always wasted when using real machines.

  • Friction between moving parts
  • Heat loss
  • Air resistance
  • Sound loss
  • Weight of the machine itself

Very common JAMB idea

  • Machines are less than 100% efficient because friction reduces the useful output.

7️⃣ 10 JAMB-Style Examples with Step-by-Step Solutions

1. Which of the following is NOT a simple machine?

A. Lever B. Pulley C. Wedge D. Generator

Step 1: Recall the six simple machines: lever, pulley, wedge, screw, inclined plane, wheel and axle.

Step 2: Generator is not a simple machine. It is a more complex machine.

Answer: D. Generator
2. An inclined plane of angle θ has a velocity ratio of

A. sin θ B. 1/sin θ C. cos θ D. 1/cos θ E. sin θ cos θ

Step 1: VR = Length of plane / Height

Step 2: In the triangle, sin θ = height / length

Step 3: Therefore length / height = 1/sin θ

Answer: B. 1/sin θ
3. The efficiency of a machine is always less than 100% because the

A. Velocity ratio is always greater than the mechanical advantage B. Work output is always greater than the work input C. Effort applied is always greater than the load lifted D. Load lifted is always greater than the effort applied

Step 1: Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%

Step 2: In real machines, friction causes MA to be less than VR

Step 3: So VR is greater than MA

Answer: A. Velocity ratio is always greater than the mechanical advantage
4. A lever lifts a load of 400 N with an effort of 100 N. Calculate its mechanical advantage.

A. 0.25 B. 4 C. 2 D. 0.5

Step 1: MA = Load / Effort

Step 2: MA = 400 / 100 = 4

Answer: B. 4
5. A machine does 600 J of useful work when 800 J of work is supplied. What is its efficiency?

A. 75% B. 133% C. 60% D. 80%

Step 1: Efficiency = (Useful work output / Work input) × 100%

Step 2: Efficiency = (600 / 800) × 100%

Step 3: Efficiency = 75%

Answer: A. 75%
6. If the effort moves 5 m while the load moves 1 m in a machine, find the velocity ratio.

A. 5 B. 0.2 C. 1 D. 6

Step 1: VR = Distance moved by effort / Distance moved by load

Step 2: VR = 5 / 1 = 5

Answer: A. 5
7. A machine has a mechanical advantage of 3 and a velocity ratio of 6. Find its efficiency.

A. 50% B. 200% C. 18% D. 100%

Step 1: Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%

Step 2: Efficiency = (3 / 6) × 100%

Step 3: Efficiency = 50%

Answer: A. 50%
8. A screw jack, 25% efficient, has pitch 0.4 cm and handle radius 40 cm. Calculate VR, MA, and effort for a 1000 N load.

A. VR ≈ 628, MA ≈ 157, Effort ≈ 6.37 N B. VR = 100, MA = 25, Effort = 40 N C. VR = 200, MA = 50, Effort = 20 N D. VR = 314, MA = 78.5, Effort = 12.74 N

Step 1: VR = 2πr / p

Step 2: VR = 2 × 3.14 × 40 / 0.4 ≈ 628

Step 3: Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%

Step 4: 25 = (MA / 628) × 100

Step 5: MA = 0.25 × 628 ≈ 157

Step 6: Effort = Load / MA = 1000 / 157 ≈ 6.37 N

Answer: A. VR ≈ 628, MA ≈ 157, Effort ≈ 6.37 N
9. A pulley system has VR = 4 and requires 800 N effort to lift 1600 N load. Calculate efficiency.

A. 50% B. 200% C. 25% D. 100%

Step 1: MA = Load / Effort = 1600 / 800 = 2

Step 2: Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%

Step 3: Efficiency = (2 / 4) × 100 = 50%

Answer: A. 50%
10. What type of simple machine is a seesaw?

A. Pulley B. Inclined plane C. Lever D. Wedge

Step 1: A seesaw turns about a pivot called fulcrum

Step 2: That makes it a lever

Step 3: More specifically, it is a first-class lever

Answer: C. Lever

How to score high in JAMB simple machines

  • Know the six simple machines very well
  • Memorise MA = Load / Effort
  • Memorise VR = Distance by effort / Distance by load
  • Memorise Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%
  • Remember that real machines are never 100% efficient
  • Practise pulley, inclined plane, and screw jack questions often
