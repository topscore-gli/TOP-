🔊 Propagation of Sound Wave

🎯 Learning Objective

This chapter explains the nature of sound, how sound travels in different media, echo and reverberation, factors affecting sound speed, and important JAMB calculations involving sound propagation.

  • Sound is a mechanical longitudinal wave
  • Sound needs a material medium to travel
  • Sound travels fastest in solids and slowest in gases
  • The key echo formula is v = 2d/t
🧠 Big idea

Sound is produced by vibrations and moves through matter as compressions and rarefactions.

1️⃣ Nature and Properties of Sound Waves

What is sound?

Sound is a mechanical wave that requires a material medium such as a solid, liquid, or gas. It cannot travel through a vacuum because there are no particles to vibrate.

Production of sound

Sound is produced by vibrating objects. These vibrations create alternating regions of high pressure and low pressure in the medium.

  • Compression (C): region where particles are close together
  • Rarefaction (R): region where particles are spread apart
Compressions and rarefactions
A sound wave moves through a medium as alternating compressions and rarefactions.

Key properties of sound

3️⃣ Echo and Reverberation

Echo

An echo is a reflected sound heard distinctly after the original sound.

Echo formula
v = 2d/t
  • The factor 2 appears because sound travels to the surface and back
  • The reflecting surface should be at least about 17 m away for a distinct echo
Echo from a cliff
Sound travels to the reflecting surface and back, producing an echo.

Reverberation

Reverberation is the persistence of sound in an enclosed space due to many reflections arriving too quickly to be heard separately.

Echo is a distinct reflected sound, while reverberation is an overlapping continuation of sound.

5️⃣ Applications of Sound Propagation

  • SONAR: detecting underwater objects
  • Ultrasound scanning: medical imaging
  • Echo sounding: measuring ocean depth
  • Architectural acoustics: designing halls and theatres
SONAR idea
SONAR sends sound into water and uses the echo to detect distance or depth.

6️⃣ Essential Calculations for JAMB

Calculation 1: Speed of Sound Using Echo

A man stands 85 m from a cliff and hears the echo after 0.5 s. Find the speed of sound.

Step: v = 2d/t = (2 × 85)/0.5 = 340 m/s

Answer: 340 m/s
Calculation 2: Finding Depth

A ship sends a sound signal to the seabed and receives the echo after 4 s. If speed of sound in water is 1500 m/s, find the depth.

Step: d = vt/2 = (1500 × 4)/2 = 3000 m

Answer: 3000 m or 3 km
Calculation 3: Temperature Effect

Find the speed of sound in air at 30°C.

Step: v = 331 + 0.6(30) = 349 m/s

Answer: 349 m/s
Calculation 4: Multiple Echoes

A man between two cliffs hears the first echo after 2 s and the second after 3 s. If v = 340 m/s, find the distances to the nearer cliff, farther cliff, and the distance between cliffs.

Nearer cliff: d₁ = vt₁/2 = 340 × 2 / 2 = 340 m

Farther cliff: d₂ = vt₂/2 = 340 × 3 / 2 = 510 m

Between cliffs: d₁ + d₂ = 850 m

Answer: 340 m, 510 m, 850 m
Calculation 5: Frequency and Wavelength

A sound wave has frequency 500 Hz and speed 340 m/s. Find its wavelength.

Step: λ = v/f = 340/500 = 0.68 m

Answer: 0.68 m

8️⃣ Practice Questions for JAMB

Why can’t sound travel through a vacuum?

Because sound needs particles of a material medium to vibrate, and a vacuum has no particles.

Find the wavelength of sound with frequency 1000 Hz traveling at 330 m/s.

λ = v/f = 330/1000 = 0.33 m

Answer: 0.33 m
A boy stands 680 m from a wall. How long after clapping will he hear the echo if v = 340 m/s?

t = 2d/v = (2 × 680)/340 = 4 s

Answer: 4 s

9️⃣ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting the factor of 2 in echo calculations
  • Mixing Celsius and Kelvin in formulas where Kelvin is required
  • Confusing echo with reverberation
  • Thinking sound travels faster in gases than in solids
🧠 Final success tips

Master v = 2d/t, remember solids > liquids > gases, and keep in mind that speed of sound in air increases by about 0.6 m/s for every 1°C rise.
