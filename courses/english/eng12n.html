🧠 One-line recall

Bepo gets to the airport, dreams of the Point of No Return, wakes up, and chooses not to leave Nigeria after all.

1️⃣ Opening: Departure Day

The chapter opens on a Saturday, the day Bepo is supposed to leave Nigeria for the UK.

  • He is booked on a 10:00 pm Emirates flight to London
  • Boarding begins around 5:00 pm
  • He plans to leave home by 3:00 pm to avoid Lagos traffic

Important detail: he remembers a past mistake when he once missed a flight because of time confusion, so he is extra careful now.

2️⃣ Packing for the UK

Bepo packs carefully for life abroad, carrying Nigerian food items his wife has advised him to take along.

  • iru (locust beans)
  • egusi
  • ground crayfish
  • dry snail
🧠 Why this matters

These items show he is preparing seriously for a new life abroad, not just talking about leaving.

3️⃣ Farewell from Landlord and Family

Just as Bepo is about to call an Uber, his landlord, Mr. Ogunwale, and his family come out to say goodbye.

  • Mr. Ogunwale prays briefly for safe travel
  • He offers to drive Bepo to the airport in his Honda Pilot
  • The children, especially Jide and Kemi, are emotional
“You want to Japa!”
— Kemi’s innocent remark to Bepo

This line captures the whole migration theme of the novel in simple everyday language.

4️⃣ Colleagues Join the Farewell

Four of Bepo’s colleagues from Stardom Schools meet him at the Ikeja Underbridge and continue the trip with him to the airport.

  • Mrs. Apeh
  • Mr. Audu
  • Mr. Oyelana
  • Mr. Amos

They all go with him to Murtala Mohammed International Airport (MM2), showing how loved he is.

5️⃣ At the Airport: Sleep and Dream

After checking in, Bepo sits at the boarding gate and dozes off. He then has a vivid and symbolic dream.

  • The dream takes him to the Badagry slave museum
  • He sees slaves tortured and marched away
  • He watches them being taken to the Point of No Return
  • They are loaded onto ships
“Point of No Return”
— Symbolic phrase repeated through the dream
🧠 Symbolic meaning

The dream compares old forced migration with modern voluntary migration, making Bepo question whether leaving is really freedom or another painful separation.

6️⃣ The Twist: Bepo Does Not Board

An airline official wakes Bepo for boarding. But instead of entering the plane, he makes a surprising decision — he does not board the flight.

Major twist: Bepo turns back from the journey and returns to Stardom Schools.

7️⃣ Monday Morning Return

On the following Monday, students, staff, and the Managing Director feel his absence — until he suddenly reappears.

  • Everyone is shocked and overjoyed
  • Bepo explains that his heart belongs to the school
  • He chooses his educational mission in Nigeria over migration

This ending confirms Bepo’s deep patriotism, his commitment to education, and his emotional belonging at Stardom.

8️⃣ Themes in Chapter 12

  • Migration vs patriotism: leave for better life or stay and serve?
  • Dedication to education: Bepo chooses teaching and his students
  • Belonging: his true home is the school community
  • Symbolism: the slave dream deepens the meaning of departure
  • Hope: the novel ends on a hopeful note of recommitment
🧠 Theme line

The final chapter shows that Bepo’s real destination is not London but the calling he cannot abandon.

9️⃣ Character Development of Bepo

Across the novel, Bepo moves from confusion and pressure to clarity and commitment.

  • At first, he is torn between family pressure and school duty
  • He prepares seriously to relocate
  • But in the end, he realizes that leaving would betray his deepest values

Final growth: Bepo becomes a symbol of commitment, patriotism, and purpose.
