🎵 Rhymes & Homophones Guide

JAMB 2026 • rhyme words • same sounds • homophones • easy practice • fast scoring

📋 Quick Overview

JAMB Oral English tests your ability to recognize rhyming words and homophones.

  • Rhymes = words with the same ending sound
  • Homophones = words that sound the same but have different spellings or meanings

Common JAMB question types

What they usually ask

  • Choose the word that rhymes with the underlined word
  • Which pair of words are homophones?
  • Select the option with the same sound as...

Easy step-by-step learning method

Read each set aloud slowly. Listen for the same ending sound. Then study the homophones and notice that the sound is the same but the meaning and spelling are different.

🧠 Golden tip

Do not trust spelling alone. In Oral English, your ears matter more than your eyes.

1️⃣ Understand the Difference

What is a rhyme?
Meaning: Two or more words end with the same sound.
Example: light, night, right
What is a homophone?
Meaning: Two or more words sound the same but have different spellings and meanings.
Example: right, write, rite

Easy way to remember

  • Rhymes → same ending sound
  • Homophones → same whole sound

4️⃣ Tricky Homophones Explained Simply

there / their / they’re
there = place → The book is over there.
their = belongs to them → Their car is new.
they’re = they are → They’re coming soon.
your / you’re
your = belongs to you → Your bag is here.
you’re = you are → You’re welcome.
its / it’s
its = belongs to it → The dog wagged its tail.
it’s = it is → It’s raining.
to / too / two
to = direction → I am going to school.
too = also / excessively → I want to go too.
two = number 2 → I have two pens.
right / write / rite
right = correct / direction
write = put words on paper
rite = ceremony or ritual
hear / here
hear = receive sound
here = in this place
see / sea
see = use the eyes
sea = ocean

5️⃣ Where JAMB Usually Tests Rhymes & Homophones

Most repeated patterns

  • Rhymes with -ate, -ight, -ee, -air, -ook, -all
  • Homophones like there/their/they’re, right/write, hear/here
  • Questions that mix same spelling pattern with different sounds

Important: JAMB tests sound, not spelling shape. Two words may look different and still rhyme or sound exactly the same.

6️⃣ Mini Self-Quiz

1. Which word rhymes with light?
A. late
B. right
C. lot
D. let
Answer: B — right. Both end with /aɪt/.
2. Which pair are homophones?
A. bear / beer
B. sea / see
C. look / lock
D. late / let
Answer: B — sea / see.
3. Which word rhymes with book?
A. boot
B. bake
C. cook
D. back
Answer: C — cook. Both end with /ʊk/.
4. Which is correct as a homophone set?
A. there / their / they’re
B. right / rough
C. fair / fear
D. sea / say
Answer: A — there / their / they’re.
5. Which word rhymes with hair?
A. hear
B. hare
C. her
D. hour
Answer: B — hare. It also forms a homophone pair with hair.

7️⃣ Quick JAMB Revision Tips

Best way to score high

  • Read rhyme sets aloud slowly
  • Memorise common homophones by meaning, not only sound
  • Practise daily with hear/here, right/write, there/their/they’re
  • Focus on repeated rhyme patterns from past JAMB questions
  • Test yourself by covering one word and predicting the rhyme or pair
