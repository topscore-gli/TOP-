Topics

Chapter 1 (DUSK)🌇

Principal Bepo Adewale breaks down in tears during morning assembly at Stardom Schools, shocking students and staff. Unable to explain his distress, he's taken to his office where he mutters "Oluwa gba mi o!" ("God, save me!"). The MD, Mrs. Ibidun Gloss, arrives to manage the crisis while staff speculate about marital or emotional troubles as the cause.

Chapter 2 (The Enticement)🤧

Bepo reveals he's under pressure to relocate to the UK to join his wife Seri and their two children, who've already moved there. Despite his love for Stardom and his dream of starting his own Nigerian school, colleagues encourage him to accept the opportunity, highlighting the salary difference between his ₦400,000 monthly pay and potential £3,600 abroad.

Chapter 3 (Migration Tales)🛣️

Bepo explores the realities of migration through stories from Nigerians abroad. He learns about flexible hourly wages, high living costs, and mixed outcomes—some thrive while others struggle. A late-night call from Mrs. Ignatius about her family's visa denial due to a paternity scandal further illustrates migration's emotional toll.

Chapter 4 (A Case Of Visa Denied)💳🙅

Mrs. Ignatius confides in Bepo about her family's shattered UK relocation plans after a DNA test revealed her husband wasn't Favour's biological father. Meanwhile, student Bibi develops nightmares about her government teacher Mr. Ayesoro due to his intimidating tribal marks, leading to his eventual transfer.

Chapter 5 (Snake In The Roof)🐍

The MD discovers staff using school land as a parking lot for 17 vehicles purchased through the cooperative society's ₦95 million fund. Alarmed by potential misuse, the board caps loans at ₦250,000 and demands stricter oversight, with Chief Bayo warning this is like "hanging a snake in the roof and going to bed."

Chapter 6 (Ade as Well as Jide COMES vs. COME)👬

English teacher Mr. Fafore faces dismissal when the MD misinterprets his grammar lesson. Bepo intervenes to explain that "Ade as well as Jide comes" is actually correct, resolving the tense situation with humor while highlighting the pressures teachers face.

Chapter 7 (Ritualist)☠️

Bepo recalls witnessing a live cow burial ritual at his former school, Beesway Group, and his moral dilemma about reporting it. The chapter connects to a news report implicating a familiar figure in ritual murder, while also recounting Bepo's failed "Fruitful Future" school venture due to infrastructural challenges.

Chapter 8 (Mission Unaccomplished)❌

Prefect elections at Stardom turn dramatic when candidate Banky insults rival Tosh by referencing his father's imprisonment. Chief Ogba demands legal action and a public apology, creating unresolved tension as Bepo prepares to leave. He also worries about the Invention Club's "Breath Project" making phones from recycled materials.

Chapter 9 (Laughing Waterfall)🌊

Students visit Nigerian heritage sites including Ikogosi Warm Springs and Badagry's First Storey Building. Bepo compares modern migration to "a new form of slavery" and is moved to anger and sadness at the Black Heritage Museum, reinforcing his conflicted feelings about leaving.

Chapter 10 (Passport Pain)🛂😭

Bepo endures Nigeria's bureaucratic nightmare renewing his passport—corruption, network failures at the NIN office, and demands for bribes. He travels to Ibadan to avoid Lagos chaos, paying ₦100,000 to expedite the process, highlighting systemic dysfunction.

Chapter 11 (Point Of No Return)🚶‍♂️

Stardom hosts emotional farewell events for Bepo, presenting him with a $10,000 cheque. At the airport, he dreams of the Heritage Slave Museum and screams "Noooo!" in his sleep. Despite boarding his flight, he ultimately cannot leave.

Chapter 12 (DAWN)🌅

Bepo returns to Stardom unexpectedly after his departure, declaring "I am back to complete my mission!" Students celebrate by singing the school victory song. The novel ends with Bepo choosing his educational mission in Nigeria over family reunification abroad, reaffirming his dedication to his students and country.
