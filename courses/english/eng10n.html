🧠 One-line recall

Bepo’s passport renewal becomes a painful journey through queues, agents, extra fees, NIN problems, and the pressure of the japa rush.

1️⃣ Main Focus of the Chapter

This chapter centers on Mr. Bepo Adewale’s frustrating attempt to renew his passport as part of his relocation plans to the UK.

“Passport pains”
— Chapter title capturing stress, frustration, and obstacles
  • The chapter shows that leaving Nigeria is not just an emotional issue
  • It is also a practical struggle filled with bureaucracy and delay

2️⃣ Why Bepo Delayed Renewal

Bepo’s passport had expired two years earlier, but he did not renew it because he was not originally serious about relocating.

  • He had no urgent plan to travel abroad
  • He heard many stories about long queues and harsh new rules
  • He kept postponing the process

Important point: once his decision to relocate becomes firm, passport renewal becomes unavoidable.

3️⃣ COVID-19 and the Japa Rush

The chapter links Bepo’s difficulties to wider national problems.

  • COVID-19 caused delays and disruptions in passport services
  • The “Japa rush” made passport offices overcrowded
  • Mass migration pressure increased frustration everywhere
“Japa rush”
— Phrase describing the overwhelming demand for passports and migration processing

4️⃣ Corruption as the “Easy Option”

Bepo learns that he could avoid much of the stress if he is willing to bribe officials.

“up to N200,000 to N250,000”
— Amount he could pay for a quick renewal through bribery

Why this matters

  • It shows corruption as a common shortcut in broken systems
  • Bepo considers it but still tries to find a less corrupt path

5️⃣ Trip to Ibadan Instead of Lagos

To avoid the terrible crowds in Lagos, Bepo chooses to process the passport renewal in Ibadan, Oyo State.

“six-passenger Toyota Sienna from Ojota park”
— Description of his trip on the Lagos-Ibadan route
🧠 Why this detail matters

It reflects the everyday struggle of ordinary Nigerians trying to solve official problems.

6️⃣ Agent Tai and the Hidden Extra Cost

In Ibadan, Bepo connects with an agent called Tai, who promises a faster process.

“N100,000 instead of the official N70,000”
— Tai’s charge for helping with the passport renewal

This reveals how middlemen and unofficial charges grow around public services.

7️⃣ New Problem: NIN Validation

Even though the process in Ibadan starts more smoothly than Lagos, another obstacle appears.

  • Bepo’s NIN must be validated at a separate office
  • This creates more delay
  • There are network glitches and system problems
“NIN validation”
— New stage that complicates the process
“network glitches”
— Phrase describing technical/system failure

8️⃣ Rising Frustration and Risk to Travel Plans

The extra delays now threaten Bepo’s planned travel date.

Key effect: the process that should prepare him for departure instead increases his anxiety and uncertainty.

“at risk”
— Phrase showing how the delay threatens his travel plans

9️⃣ Themes in the Chapter

  • Bureaucratic inefficiency: simple official tasks become exhausting
  • Corruption: bribes, agents, and hidden fees shape the process
  • Mass migration pressure: the japa wave increases demand and chaos
  • Persistence: Bepo keeps trying despite obstacles
  • Systemic frustration: public institutions make survival and progress harder
🧠 Theme line

This chapter shows that even before leaving Nigeria, the system itself can exhaust a person emotionally and financially.
