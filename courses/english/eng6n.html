🧠 One-line recall

Open Day + angry parent + “comes vs come” + MD sacks teacher + Bepo proves rule + teacher returns.

1️⃣ Setting: Open Day at Stardom Schools

The chapter takes place during Open Day—when parents visit to check progress, meet teachers, and sometimes bring gifts.

  • Teachers have mixed feelings: some expect praise/gifts
  • Others fear complaints and criticism

2️⃣ The Conflict: Mr. Guta vs Mr. Fafore

A parent, Mr. Guta, storms in furious about an English note his son copied from Mr. Fafore.

“Ade as well as Jide comes early”.
— Sentence in the student’s note (the disputed line)

Mr. Guta’s claim: it should be “Ade as well as Jide come early”.

Threat

  • He threatens to withdraw his children unless Mr. Fafore is sacked.

3️⃣ MD’s Reaction: Hasty Dismissal

The MD, Mrs. Ibidun Gloss, reacts strongly. She calls Mr. Fafore incompetent and orders his immediate dismissal without careful investigation.

  • Mr. Bepo informs Mr. Fafore while walking to the MD’s office
  • Mr. Fafore is shocked and confused (Mr. Guta checked the book and left quietly earlier)
  • Fafore goes to the staff room to pack his belongings
“there was no job security in the establishment.”
— Staff conclusion after the sudden sacking

4️⃣ The Meeting: Grammar Rule Explained

In a meeting, the MD repeats the disputed sentence as the “error.” Mr. Bepo defends Mr. Fafore calmly and explains the grammar rule.

“When you use ‘and’, the verb that follows is ‘come’. But when you use ‘as well as’, ‘together with’, ‘alongside’, etc., we go for the singular verb with ‘s’.”
— Mr. Bepo’s explanation (as given in your notes)
“The clause is in the subjunctive mood and does not align with normal grammar rules.”
— Mr. Bepo (as given in your notes)

Key rule: “as well as” does not make a compound subject like “and”; the verb agrees mainly with the first subject (Ade) → comes.

5️⃣ Verification: Phones Confirm the Truth

The MD asks everyone to check the rule on their phones. They confirm Mr. Bepo and Mr. Fafore are correct.

  • MD becomes deflated and regretful
  • Mr. Audu cracks a joke to ease tension
  • MD reverses the decision and re-employs Mr. Fafore

✍️ JAMB Grammar Box: “As well as” Agreement

Correct: Ade, as well as Jide, comes early.

Correct with AND: Ade and Jide come early.

🧠 Memory trick

“As well as” is extra information; it doesn’t change the main subject.

🧩 Mini Practice (1 Question)

Choose the correct sentence:
1) The teacher as well as the students are happy.
2) The teacher as well as the students is happy.
Answer: (2) is correct — “as well as” keeps the verb singular with “teacher”.
