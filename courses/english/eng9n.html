🧠 One-line recall

This chapter celebrates Bepo’s belief that students should know Nigeria deeply through excursions before dreaming only of abroad.

1️⃣ Main Idea of the Chapter

Chapter 9 is a reflective and descriptive chapter showing Mr. Bepo Adewale’s love for experiential learning. He believes students should not learn only in classrooms but should also travel, see places, and understand Nigeria’s beauty, history, and diversity.

  • Stardom Schools is famous for excellent excursion planning
  • Bepo ensures at least one major excursion every year
  • The aim is to help students appreciate Nigeria before traveling abroad

2️⃣ The “Laughing Waterfalls” Excursion Focus

The chapter pays special attention to excursions to waterfalls across Nigeria. These waterfalls symbolize joy, beauty, and the richness of nature.

Waterfall / Site Location Important note
Ikogosi Warm Springs Ekiti State Discovered in 1852 by Rev. John S. McGee
Erin Ijesha Waterfalls Osun State Popular excursion site
Owu Waterfalls Kwara State Highest in West Africa; 120m above water level, 330ft down escarpment
Gurara Falls Niger State Another notable natural site

3️⃣ Planned/Future Waterfall Visits

The chapter also mentions other waterfalls Bepo hopes students will visit in future excursions.

  • Kwa Falls in Anegeje, Calabar
  • Aesop in Plateau
  • Karu in Nasarawa
  • Barup in Gembu, Taraba

4️⃣ Other Excursion Sites Beyond Waterfalls

Stardom’s excursions are not limited to waterfalls. Students also visit important historical, cultural, and recreational places.

  • Yankari Games Reserve in Bauchi
  • National War Museum in Umuahia
  • Ooni of Ife Palace
  • Badagry and the Akran of Badagry
  • Whispering Palms Resort

These excursions help students learn history, culture, and tourism in real life rather than only from books.

5️⃣ The Banana Island vs Ajegunle Lesson

One important part of the chapter is Bepo’s use of Lagos contrasts — from wealthy Banana Island to poorer Ajegunle — to teach students about hope, determination, and social mobility.

"Being born in a place like this does not condemn one to a life of penury."
— Mr. Bepo, speaking about Ajegunle
"If you are determined, committed to education, and make the best use of your talent, time will move you from slum to limelight. You will be surprised to learn that many of those you see on Banana Island started off in slums somewhere in the country. Life is about movement; it is ever ready to move upward folks who make the best use of their talents, money and opportunities."
— Mr. Bepo’s motivational speech to students
🧠 Core lesson

Your starting point in life does not have to be your final destination.

6️⃣ Themes in Chapter 9

  • Experiential learning: learning by seeing and doing
  • Patriotism: knowing Nigeria before being carried away by foreign dreams
  • Social mobility: education and determination can change one’s status
  • Exposure: students learn about nature, culture, inequality, and history
  • Holistic education: education is not only academic; it also shapes values and perspective
🧠 Theme line

Bepo wants students to see Nigeria with their own eyes so they can value their country and their own potential.
