📢 Emphatic Stress Mastery Guide

JAMB 2026 • sentence stress • contrastive stress • golden rule • worked examples

📋 What is Emphatic Stress?

Emphatic stress is the extra strong stress placed on one whole word in a sentence to show importance, correction, or contrast.

In JAMB, the stressed word is usually written in CAPITAL LETTERS.

Example
The teacher wants MY pen.
Meaning: Not his pen. Not your pen. My pen.
  • It shows importance
  • It can correct a wrong idea
  • It can show contrast

1️⃣ Difference Between Word Stress and Emphatic Stress

Word Stress
Stress on one syllable inside a word.
Example: COMmercial, deCIDE
Emphatic Stress
Stress on a whole word inside a sentence.
Example: The teacher wants MY pen.

Important for JAMB

  • This section tests emphatic stress, not ordinary word stress.
  • So your job is to find which word is being corrected or contrasted.

2️⃣ Exact JAMB Question Format

JAMB usually asks:

“In each of the following questions, the word in capital letters has the emphatic stress. Choose the option to which the given sentence relates.”

This means you must choose the option that brings out the correction or contrast in the capitalised word.

3️⃣ The Golden Rule

🌟 Never forget this

The correct option is the one that contradicts or replaces the CAPITALISED word.

  • The answer usually does not repeat the same capitalised word exactly
  • It usually changes that word to another person, action, place, time, object, or quality
  • The correct answer is the one the sentence is correcting
Example
JOHN bought a new car yesterday.
Meaning: It was John, not Peter or another person.
Correct related question: Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?

4️⃣ Quick Test Method

Use these 3 steps

  • Spot the word in CAPITAL LETTERS
  • Ask yourself: “What is this sentence correcting?”
  • Choose the option that changes exactly that word
🧠 Shortcut

If the stressed word is the subject, choose the option that changes the subject. If it is the verb, choose the option that changes the action. If it is the object, place, time, or quality, choose the option that changes that part.

5️⃣ Type 1 — Emphatic Stress on the Subject

Here, the sentence is correcting who did something or who is being talked about.

1. JOHN bought a new car yesterday.
A. Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?
B. Did John sell a new car yesterday?
C. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
D. Did John buy a new car today?
Answer: A
Why: It changes JOHN to Peter.
2. MY mother cooked the rice.
A. Did your father cook the rice?
B. Did your mother eat the rice?
C. Did your mother cook the beans?
D. Did your sister cook the rice?
Answer: A
Why: It changes MY mother to your father.
3. THE accused has been tried.
A. Has the witness been tried?
B. Has the accused been sentenced?
C. Has the judge been tried?
D. Has the accused been released?
Answer: A
Why: It changes the accused to the witness.
4. EMEKA is a lawyer.
A. Is Chinedu a lawyer?
B. Is Emeka a doctor?
C. Was Emeka a lawyer?
D. Is Emeka the judge?
Answer: A
Why: It changes EMEKA to Chinedu.
5. ADAMU is leaving a car behind.
A. Is Musa leaving a car behind?
B. Is Adamu driving a car?
C. Is Adamu leaving a house behind?
D. Is Adamu taking a car?
Answer: A
Why: It changes ADAMU to Musa.
6. HIS wife works in a bank.
A. Does her husband work in a bank?
B. Does his sister work in a bank?
C. Does his wife work in a school?
D. Does his mother work in a bank?
Answer: A
Why: It changes HIS wife to her husband.
7. THE Nigerian football team has many foreign players.
A. Does the Ghanaian football team have many foreign players?
B. Does the Nigerian volleyball team have many foreign players?
C. Does the Nigerian football team have local players?
D. Does the Nigerian basketball team have many foreign players?
Answer: A
Why: It changes Nigerian to Ghanaian.
8. SHE works at the hospital.
A. Does he work at the hospital?
B. Does she work at the school?
C. Does she teach at the hospital?
D. What does she do at the hospital?
Answer: A
Why: It changes SHE to he.
9. THE fire destroyed many lives.
A. Did the flood destroy many lives?
B. Did the fire destroy few lives?
C. Did the fire save many lives?
D. Did the accident destroy many lives?
Answer: A
Why: It changes fire to flood.
10. DADDY plays tennis on Saturday afternoons.
A. Does mummy play tennis on Saturday afternoons?
B. Does daddy play football on Saturday afternoons?
C. Does daddy play tennis on Sunday afternoons?
D. Does uncle play tennis on Saturday afternoons?
Answer: A
Why: It changes DADDY to mummy.

6️⃣ Type 2 — Emphatic Stress on the Verb

Here, the sentence is correcting the action.

1. John BOUGHT a new car yesterday.
A. Did John sell a new car yesterday?
B. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
C. Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?
D. Did John wash a new car yesterday?
Answer: A
Why: It changes BOUGHT to sell.
2. The accused hasn’t even been TRIED.
A. Was the accused sentenced?
B. Has the accused been released?
C. Has the suspect been apprehended?
D. Was the accused handed over to the police?
Answer: A
Why: It changes TRIED to sentenced.
3. John must PAY for the broken glass immediately.
A. Must John repair the broken glass immediately?
B. Must Jane pay for the broken glass immediately?
C. Must John pay for the broken window?
D. Must John steal the broken glass?
Answer: A
Why: It changes PAY to repair.
4. The workers agreed to forgo their December salaries.
Verb focus: forgo
A. Did the workers agree to claim their December salaries?
B. Did the workers refuse to forgo their December salaries?
C. Did the workers agree to forgo their March salaries?
D. Did the employers agree to forgo their December salaries?
Answer: A
Why: It changes forgo to claim.
5. The cat DEVOURED the rat.
A. Did the cat pet the rat?
B. Did the rat devour the cat?
C. Did the dog devour the rat?
D. Did the cat chase the rat?
Answer: A
Why: It changes DEVOURED to pet.
6. She WORKS at the hospital.
A. Does she teach at the hospital?
B. Does she work at the school?
C. Does he work at the hospital?
D. What does she do at the hospital?
Answer: A
Why: It changes WORKS to teach.
7. The landlord REFUSED to eject the tenant.
A. Did the landlord agree to eject the tenant?
B. Did the caretaker refuse to eject the tenant?
C. Did the landlord refuse to accept the tenant?
D. Did the landlord eject the guest?
Answer: A
Why: It changes REFUSED to agree.
8. Adon stated she had a RIGHT to her privacy.
A. Did Adon state she had no right to her privacy?
B. Did Adon state she had a duty to her privacy?
C. Did Kella state she had a right to her privacy?
D. Did Adon state she had a right to her money?
Answer: A
Why: It changes RIGHT to no right.
9. The accountant PAID the workers July salary in September.
A. Did the cashier pay the workers July salary in September?
B. Did the accountant receive the workers July salary in September?
C. Did the accountant pay the workers August salary in September?
D. Did the accountant pay the workers July salary in October?
Answer: B
Why: It changes PAID to receive. This is the true verb contrast.
10. The car CRUSHED him.
A. Did the car hit him?
B. Did the lorry crush him?
C. Did the car miss him?
D. Did the car kill him?
Answer: A
Why: It changes CRUSHED to hit.

A. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
B. Did John buy a new house yesterday?

D. Did John sell a new car yesterday?

A. Does the teacher want his pen?
B. What does the teacher want?
C. Does the teacher want your ruler?
D. Who wants my pen?

Why: It changes MY to his.
3. His wife works in a BANK.
A. Does his wife work in a school?
B. Does his wife work in an office?
C. Does his sister work in a bank?

4. The Nigerian football team has many FOREIGN players.

B. Does the Ghanaian football team have many foreign players?

D. Does the Nigerian basketball team have many foreign players?

Why: It changes FOREIGN to local.
5. The workers agreed to forgo their DECEMBER salaries.

B. Did the workers agree to claim their December salaries?
C. Did the employers agree to forgo their December salaries?

8. The bed is in the ROOM.
A. Is the bed in the parlour?

9. My mother’s FRIEND hates pets.
A. Does your mother’s boss hate pets?
B. Does your mother’s friend love pets?
C. Does your father’s friend hate pets?
D. Does your mother’s friend hate dogs?
Answer: A

10. The landlord refused to eject the TENANT.
A. Did the landlord refuse to eject the guest?
B. Did the caretaker refuse to eject the tenant?

9️⃣ Final Tips for JAMB Exam Day

Exam strategy

  • Read the CAPITALISED word first
  • Do not be distracted by the whole sentence
  • Find the option that changes only that stressed word
  • Do not choose an option that mainly changes another part
  • Remember: the answer is the word or idea being corrected
🧠 Best shortcut

The sentence with emphatic stress is like saying: “Not this one — that one.” So your answer must show the “wrong idea” being corrected.

🔟 Mini Self-Quiz

1. JOHN bought a new car yesterday. What is being corrected?
A. The time
B. The person
C. The car
D. The action
Answer: B — the person.
2. John BOUGHT a new car yesterday. Which option relates best?
A. Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?
B. Did John sell a new car yesterday?
C. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
D. Did John buy a new car today?
Answer: B — it changes the action bought to sell.
3. The teacher wants MY pen. Which idea is being corrected?
A. The object
B. Ownership
C. The action
D. The place
Answer: B — ownership.
4. Daddy plays tennis on SATURDAY afternoons. Which option relates best?
A. Does daddy play tennis on Sunday afternoons?
B. Does mummy play tennis on Saturday afternoons?
C. Does daddy play football on Saturday afternoons?
D. Does daddy play tennis on Saturday mornings?
Answer: A — it changes SATURDAY to Sunday.
5. Golden rule: the correct answer usually...
A. Repeats the capitalised word exactly
B. Ignores the capitalised word
C. Contradicts or replaces the capitalised word
D. Changes every part of the sentence
Answer: C — that is the golden rule.
