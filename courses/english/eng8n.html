🧠 One-line recall

Bepo is preparing to leave, but he feels bad about two unfinished missions: the Banky–Tosh feud and the future of the Breath Project.

1️⃣ Meaning of “Missions Unaccomplished”

This chapter shows Mr. Bepo Adewale reflecting on important issues he has not resolved before relocating to the UK.

“Missions Unaccomplished”
— Chapter title referring to unresolved duties and regrets
  • He feels emotionally burdened by unfinished school matters
  • His regret shows how dedicated he is to Stardom Schools

2️⃣ The Major Unresolved Conflict: Banky vs Tosh

One major issue troubling Bepo is a long-standing feud between the families of two students: Banky and Tosh.

  • Both are in SSS2
  • They are rivals in the contest for Social Prefect
  • The conflict has lasted about three years

Important note: this is not just a student quarrel; it is a family rivalry spilling into school life.

3️⃣ Roots of the Feud

The rivalry between the two students goes beyond the election.

  • They had earlier clashed in a JSS 3 Best Dancer competition
  • Banky won by 3–2 votes
  • Their mothers also fought over a PTA election
  • Their fathers belong to opposing political parties
🧠 Easy memory

Student fight + mothers’ fight + fathers’ political rivalry = deep family feud.

5️⃣ The Campaign Insult That Changed Everything

During her campaign speech for Social Prefect, Banky publicly attacks Tosh.

“the son of an ex-convict”
— The hurtful phrase Banky uses against Tosh
“Instead of voting for the son of an ex-convict, cast your vote for me. You will never regret it.”
— Banky’s full campaign statement

Immediate reactions

  • Students cheer
  • Bepo stands up to condemn the statement
  • Tosh walks out of the hall in anger

6️⃣ Why the Insult Was Serious

Banky’s comment refers to Tosh’s father, Chief Didi Ogba.

  • He was detained for 36 months
  • The case involved alleged misappropriation of a N2.5 billion contract
  • He was later released because the court ruled he was not directly guilty

This makes the insult deeply personal and unfair, because it drags an adult’s public scandal into a student election.

7️⃣ Another Mission: The Breath Project

Bepo is also worried about the school’s Invention Club and its important innovation project called the Breath Project.

  • The project is about five years old
  • It aims to produce phones using recycled panels and chips
  • It is supported by an NGO called Life Grid
🧠 Why this matters

Bepo fears that once he leaves, the project may lose direction and die.
