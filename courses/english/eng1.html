1️⃣ Core Rules & Strategies

Comprehension – Universal Method

  • Read the 5 questions first
  • Underline key words like: attitude, purpose, implies, main idea, inference, word meaning
  • Skim the passage in 1–2 minutes
  • Find main idea, tone, and transitions
  • Go back to the exact line for each answer

Eliminate Common Traps

Watch out for:

  • Extreme words: always, never
  • Opposite meaning options
  • Half-true answers
  • Outside knowledge
  • Personal opinion

Passage Types & What to Look For

  • Descriptive → senses, imagery, adjectives, tone
  • Narrative → story, characters, conflict, resolution, moral
  • Expository → facts, reasons, benefits, explanation
  • Argumentative → writer’s position, reasons, rejected opposition

Summary Rules

  • Use your own words only
  • Write short complete sentences
  • Include only main points
  • Use third person
  • Answer exactly what is asked
🧠 Time Goal

Spend 7–10 minutes total on one passage, 5 questions, and summary if included.

2️⃣ Descriptive Passage 1 – Village Square at Dusk

The village square at dusk was a living painting. Tall palm trees swayed gently in the evening breeze, their fronds whispering secrets to one another. Women in brightly coloured wrappers balanced enamel bowls of garri and vegetable soup on their heads, laughing and greeting passers-by. The air carried the sweet smell of roasting plantain mixed with the earthy scent of fresh palm wine. Children chased one another around the ancient iroko tree, their bare feet kicking up red dust that glowed golden in the setting sun. In the distance, the sound of talking drums from the chief’s compound floated across the square, calling the villagers home after a long day on the farm. Everything felt alive, warm and timeless.

1. The writer’s main purpose is to
A. persuade people to visit the village
B. describe the beauty and atmosphere of the village square
C. explain why villagers love palm wine
D. argue that city life is better
Correct: B
Pure description using senses to show beauty and lively atmosphere.
2. The word “whispering” means
A. shouting loudly
B. making soft sounds
C. arguing angrily
D. singing songs
Correct: B
The context is gentle and calm.
3. The writer’s attitude is
A. critical and disappointed
B. indifferent
C. warm and appreciative
D. fearful
Correct: C
Words like “living painting,” “warm,” and “alive” show admiration.
4. From the passage, it is clear that
A. the villagers are unhappy with their lives
B. the square is empty at dusk
C. the scene is peaceful and full of life
D. only children visit the square
Correct: C
Laughter, greetings, play, and drums show active but peaceful life.
5. The expression “living painting” suggests
A. has many artists
B. looks beautiful and natural like a picture
C. is painted with bright colours
D. changes colour every evening
Correct: B
It is a metaphor for beauty and scenic life.

3️⃣ Descriptive Passage 2 – Harmattan Season

Yet, the cool morning air feels refreshing after months of heat. Children fly kites made from old newspapers, and the smell of roasting suya fills the air. Despite the dust, the season brings a special quiet beauty that only northerners truly understand.

3. The writer’s tone is
A. angry
B. neutral and informative

When Chinedu’s name was announced as the winner, the whole school cheered. His mother cried with joy. From that day, Chinedu believed that hard work and quiet determination could change anyone’s story.

5️⃣ Narrative Passage 2 – Amina Refuses Ride

He offered her a ride, but Amina remembered her mother’s warning never to enter strangers’ cars. She politely refused and ran under a nearby shop shed. Ten minutes later, the rain stopped. She reached home safely and told her mother what happened. Her mother hugged her and said, “Well done, my daughter. Safety first.”
