🔊 Vowel Sounds Mastery Guide

JAMB 2026 • monophthongs • diphthongs • triphthongs • RP pronunciation • easy revision

📋 Quick Overview

JAMB Oral English tests your ability to identify and pronounce vowel sounds correctly in Received Pronunciation (RP), the British standard.

  • 12 Monophthongs → pure single vowel sounds
  • 8 Diphthongs → gliding two-vowel sounds
  • 5 Triphthongs → gliding three-sound vowels

Easy step-by-step learning method

How to learn each sound

  • Look at the sound symbol carefully
  • Say the example words slowly and clearly
  • Compare it with a similar sound
  • Practise minimal pairs like ship / sheep
  • Repeat daily aloud
🧠 Golden tip

Do not just read with your eyes. Oral English improves fastest when you speak the words aloud.

1️⃣ Understand the 3 Main Groups

Monophthongs
Meaning: One pure vowel sound.
Example: /ɪ/ in sit
Diphthongs
Meaning: A vowel sound that glides from one sound to another.
Example: /eɪ/ in day
Triphthongs
Meaning: A vowel sound that moves through three parts.
Example: /aɪə/ in fire

7️⃣ Where JAMB Usually Tests Vowel Sounds

Common question styles

  • Which word has the same vowel sound as the underlined part?
  • Which word differs in vowel sound?
  • Minimal pair comparison questions
  • Short vowel vs long vowel distinction

Most repeated patterns: /ɪ/ vs /iː/, /e/ vs /æ/, /ʊ/ vs /uː/, /aɪ/ vs /ɔɪ/.

8️⃣ Mini Self-Quiz

1. Which word has the same vowel sound as ship?
A. sheep
B. seat
C. sit
D. see
Answer: C — sit. Both have /ɪ/.
2. Which word has the same vowel sound as day?
A. die
B. play
C. boy
D. now
Answer: B — play. Both have /eɪ/.
3. Which word has the same vowel sound as book?
A. moon
B. put
C. food
D. school
Answer: B — put. Both have /ʊ/.
4. Which word has the same vowel sound as bird?
A. bed
B. beard
C. burn
D. board
Answer: C — burn. Both have /ɜː/.
5. Which word differs in vowel sound?
A. cat
B. man
C. pen
D. bag
Answer: C — pen. It has /e/, while the others have /æ/.

9️⃣ Quick JAMB Revision Tip

Best way to score high

  • Master 5–10 words for each sound first
  • Practise short vs long vowel pairs daily
  • Read the words aloud, not silently
  • Use past JAMB questions from 2018–2025
  • Group similar sounds together while revising
