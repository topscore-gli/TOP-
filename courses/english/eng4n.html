🧠 One-line recall

“Late-night call → Mrs. Ignatius → UK plan fails → DNA/paternity bombshell → family and visa crisis.”

1️⃣ Setting & Opening Scene

Mr. Bepo is in his room at Adeniyi Jones Avenue, Ikeja, Lagos, about to perform his night prayers when his phone rings late at night (around 11:55 p.m.).

Why this matters

  • JAMB loves “where + when” questions.

2️⃣ The Caller: Mrs. Ignatius (Boundary Issue)

The caller is Mrs. Ignatius, a parent of a Stardom student. Bepo feels irritated because she has become too familiar/cordial with him and crosses boundaries (late-night personal calls).

Character note: Bepo is humorous and compassionate, but he dislikes being taken for granted.

3️⃣ The “Japa” Plan (UK/London Dream)

Mrs. Ignatius calls about a crisis tied to their plan to relocate abroad for a “better future.”

  • Husband: Mr. Ibe Ignatius (manager in an oil firm)
  • He initially resisted migration but later agreed mainly for the children’s sake
  • He feared the children might grow up in Nigeria and be “superseded” by children of emigrants

4️⃣ Central Crisis: DNA Test & Paternity Doubt

The family’s visa/relocation plans collapse after a DNA test on their three children shows that one child is not biologically Mr. Ignatius’s.

Immediate consequences

  • Mr. Ignatius refuses to accept the child
  • Severe marital conflict (marriage close to collapse)
  • Mrs. Ignatius becomes emotionally distressed and seeks help

Child mentioned: Favour/Favor (Stardom student) is the one linked to the paternity controversy.

5️⃣ Bepo’s Response (Human Limit + Leadership Pressure)

Bepo sympathizes with Mrs. Ignatius but feels powerless to intervene directly. The call increases his personal pressure and shows how school heads get drawn into parents’ private crises.

🧠 Theme line

Parents’ private problems can become the principal’s burden.

6️⃣ Side Mention (Sometimes Reported): Another Parent/Student Issue

Some accounts mention another issue (e.g., Mrs. Ladele’s daughter Bibi having a nightmare about a teacher with tribal marks, leading to fear and house reassignment). But the main focus is still the Ignatius family crisis and visa denial.
