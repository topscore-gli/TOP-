🔊 Consonant Sounds Mastery Guide

JAMB 2026 • 24 consonant phonemes • RP pronunciation • easy learning • fast revision

📋 Quick Overview

JAMB Oral English tests your ability to identify and pronounce consonant sounds correctly in Received Pronunciation (RP), the British standard.

  • English has 24 consonant phonemes
  • JAMB often asks for the word with the same consonant sound
  • Minimal pairs are very common: thin / tin, ship / sip, very / berry

Step-by-step learning method

How to study well

  • Look at the IPA sound symbol
  • Say the words slowly
  • Feel where your lips, teeth, and tongue touch
  • Compare similar sounds side by side
  • Practise minimal pairs aloud every day
🧠 Easy idea

Consonants are easier when you focus on mouth position. Ask yourself: Are my lips closed? Are my teeth touching my lip? Is my tongue between my teeth?

1️⃣ Easy Meaning of Consonant Groups

Plosives
Meaning: Air is stopped completely, then released suddenly.
Examples: /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, /g/
Fricatives
Meaning: Air passes through a narrow space and makes friction.
Examples: /f/, /v/, /θ/, /ð/, /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /h/
Affricates
Meaning: A stop + friction sound together.
Examples: /tʃ/, /dʒ/
Nasals
Meaning: Air passes through the nose.
Examples: /m/, /n/, /ŋ/
Approximants / Liquids
Meaning: Air flows more smoothly with little friction.
Examples: /l/, /r/, /j/, /w/

2️⃣ Plosives

These are strong stop sounds. Air is blocked and then released.

/p/ as in “pen”
Type: Voiceless plosive
How to make it: Close both lips, then release air. No voice vibration.
Compare with: /b/
penpinpanpetpat pitpotputpaypie packparkpartpeakpick pigpilepipepinkpage
/b/ as in “bed”
Type: Voiced plosive
How to make it: Close both lips, release air, and use your voice.
bedbadbigboybag batbetbitbutbuy backballbandbankbase bearbeatbellbestbird
/t/ as in “ten”
Type: Voiceless plosive
How to make it: Touch the tongue to the upper teeth ridge and release quickly.
Compare with: /d/ and /θ/
tentintoptaptip teatietoytwotown talktalltanktapetask teamtearteethtimetire
/d/ as in “dog”
Type: Voiced plosive
How to make it: Same tongue position as /t/, but with voice vibration.
dogdaydiedodad darkdatedealdeepdesk doordowndrawdreamdrink dropdrydustdancedanger
/k/ as in “cat”
Type: Voiceless plosive
How to make it: Back of tongue touches soft palate, then releases.
Important: Letter k, c, and sometimes ch can make this sound.
catkeycakekitecup capcancarcutkeep kickkillkingkindkiss kitkneeknowknockcool
/g/ as in “go”
Type: Voiced plosive
How to make it: Same back tongue position as /k/, but with voice.

3️⃣ Fricatives

These sounds are made by forcing air through a narrow space.

/f/ as in “fan”
How to make it: Upper teeth touch lower lip. No voice.
Compare with: /v/
fanfatfarfacefact fallfeelfillfindfine firefishfivefoodfoot fourfreefromfullfun
/v/ as in “van”
How to make it: Upper teeth touch lower lip, but use voice.
vanveryvoiceviewvisit valuevaryvastverbvideo villagevinevotevoyagevalid valleyvapourveganvendor
/θ/ as in “thin”
How to make it: Put the tongue lightly between the teeth. Blow air. No voice.
Very important: JAMB loves /θ/ vs /t/.
thinthinkthankthreething thumbthrowthemethickthief thighthornthreadthroatthrone throughthunderthirtythousand
/ð/ as in “this”
How to make it: Same tongue-between-teeth position, but with voice.
Very important: JAMB also tests /ð/ vs /d/.
thisthatthesethosethen themtherethoughthusbrother fathermotherotherratherweather feathergatherleatherneithereither
/s/ as in “sit”
How to make it: Air passes through a narrow space near the teeth. No voice.
Compare with: /z/ and /ʃ/
sitseesaysunsea songsomesamesendside sixsoapsocksoftsold soonsoupspeakspeed
/z/ as in “zoo”
How to make it: Same position as /s/, but with voice vibration.

/ʃ/ as in “ship”
How to make it: Lips slightly rounded, air passes smoothly. No voice.
Compare with: /s/

/ʒ/ as in “measure”
How to make it: Similar to /ʃ/, but voiced.
Important: This sound is less common, so JAMB likes it.

/h/ as in “hat”
How to make it: Air passes out gently from the throat. No voice friction in the mouth.

/tʃ/ as in “chair”

5️⃣ Nasals

/m/ as in “man”
How to make it: Close the lips and let air pass through the nose.
manmapmaymemine moonmoremothermuchmusic makemanymarkmatchmean meetmindmoneymorningmove
/n/ as in “no”
How to make it: Tongue touches the upper ridge; air goes through the nose.
nonownewnightname nosenotenursenicenext nearneednewsninenone northnothingnumbernationnever
/ŋ/ as in “sing”
How to make it: Back of tongue lifts, air goes through the nose.
Important: This is the “ng” sound, not /n/ + /g/ in many cases.
singringkingwinglong songbankthinkdrinkthank linkpinksinkrankstrong youngbringhangwrongamong

6️⃣ Approximants / Liquids

/l/ as in “love”
How to make it: Tongue touches the upper ridge and air flows around the sides.
lovelightlooklikeland lastlateleftlifeline listlivelonglostloud lowluckylargelaughlearn
/r/ as in “red”
How to make it: Tongue bends slightly back without touching hard.
RP note: British RP is often non-rhotic, but JAMB still tests the consonant sound in words and linking /r/ situations.
redrunrightroadroom rainreadrealrestrice richrideringriserock rollroofroseroundrule
/j/ as in “yes”
How to make it: Glide sound like “y”.

/w/ as in “wet”
How to make it: Round the lips and glide into the vowel.

7️⃣ Compare Tricky Sounds

This is where JAMB usually hides the marks.

/p/ vs /b/ pat bat /p/ has no voice, /b/ has voice
/t/ vs /d/ ten den /t/ voiceless, /d/ voiced

/θ/ vs /t/ thin tin /θ/ uses tongue between teeth, /t/ does not

8️⃣ Where JAMB Usually Tests Consonants

Most common question styles

  • Which word has the same consonant sound as the underlined letter?
  • Choose the word with /k/, /ʃ/, /θ/, /ð/, or /ŋ/
  • Pick the odd word out
  • Minimal pair questions

Most repeated trouble sounds: /θ/, /ð/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /ŋ/, /r/, /v/.

9️⃣ Mini Self-Quiz

1. Which word has the same consonant sound as the underlined sound in thin?
A. tin
B. thank
C. den
D. this
Answer: B — thank. Both have /θ/.
2. Which word has the same initial consonant sound as van?
A. fan
B. very
C. pen
D. bag
Answer: B — very. Both begin with /v/.
3. Which word differs in consonant sound?
A. ship
B. shoe
C. shop
D. sip
Answer: D — sip. It has /s/, the others have /ʃ/.
4. Which word contains the /ŋ/ sound?
A. name
B. sing
C. near
D. note
Answer: B — sing. It contains /ŋ/.
5. Which word has the same consonant sound as measure?
A. ship
B. pleasure
C. chair
D. zoo
Answer: B — pleasure. Both contain /ʒ/.

🔟 Quick JAMB Revision Tips

Best way to score high

  • Practise the tricky pairs first: /θ/ vs /t/, /ð/ vs /d/, /ʃ/ vs /s/, /f/ vs /v/
  • Say the words aloud slowly
  • Use a mirror to watch your lips and tongue
  • Record yourself and compare with standard British pronunciation
  • Use past JAMB questions from 2018–2025 for repetition patterns
