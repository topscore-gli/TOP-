📋 Introduction to Chapter 12

This chapter explains how to calculate lengths, areas, surface areas, volumes, arc lengths, chord lengths, and distances on the earth’s surface.

Mensuration is one of the easiest topics to score in if you know the right formula, substitute carefully, and keep your units consistent.

  • Always identify the figure first before choosing a formula
  • Draw a sketch when one is not provided
  • Convert units before calculating
  • Composite figures should be broken into simpler shapes
  • For earth geometry, use R = 6400 km unless another radius is given
🧠 Big idea

Mensuration is mostly formula application. The hard part is not the arithmetic — it is choosing the correct formula and using the right dimensions.

1️⃣ Plane Figures (2D Shapes)

Rectangles and Squares

Example 1: Rectangle

A rectangular field is 80 m long and 60 m wide. Find its area and perimeter.

Area: A = l × b = 80 × 60 = 4800 m²

Perimeter: P = 2(80 + 60) = 2(140) = 280 m

Answer: Area = 4800 m², Perimeter = 280 m
Example 2: Square from its Diagonal

The diagonal of a square is 10√2 cm. Find its area.

For a square, diagonal = a√2

a√2 = 10√2 so a = 10 cm

Area = a² = 10² = 100 cm²

Answer: 100 cm²

Triangles

Triangle formulas
A = 1/2 × base × height
A = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] where s = (a+b+c)/2
Example 3: Heron’s Formula

Find the area of a triangle with sides 13 cm, 14 cm, and 15 cm.

s = (13 + 14 + 15)/2 = 21

A = √[21(21-13)(21-14)(21-15)]

= √[21 × 8 × 7 × 6] = √7056 = 84 cm²

Answer: 84 cm²
Example 4: Basic Triangle Area

Base = 12 cm, height = 8 cm.

A = 1/2 × 12 × 8 = 48 cm²

Answer: 48 cm²

Quadrilaterals

Example 5: Rhombus

Diagonals are 16 cm and 12 cm. Find the area.

A = 1/2 × 16 × 12 = 96 cm²

Answer: 96 cm²
Example 6: Trapezium

Parallel sides are 10 cm and 6 cm, height is 4 cm.

A = 1/2(10 + 6) × 4

= 1/2 × 16 × 4 = 32 cm²

Answer: 32 cm²

2️⃣ Circles, Arcs, Chords and Sectors

Key definitions: Arc = part of circumference, chord = straight line joining two points on a circle, sector = “pizza slice”, segment = region between chord and arc.

Arc Length and Sector Area

Example 7: Arc Length

Find the arc length subtending 60° at the center of a circle of radius 21 cm. Use π = 22/7.

Arc = (θ/360°) × 2πr

= 60/360 × 2 × 22/7 × 21

= 1/6 × 132 = 22 cm

Answer: 22 cm
Example 8: Sector Area

Find the area of a sector of radius 14 cm and angle 90°.

A = 90/360 × 22/7 × 14²

= 1/4 × 22/7 × 196

= 1/4 × 616 = 154 cm²

Answer: 154 cm²

Chord Length and Segment Area

Example 9: Chord Length

A chord subtends 120° at the center of a circle of radius 10 cm.

c = 2r sin(θ/2)

= 2 × 10 × sin(60°)

= 20 × √3/2 = 10√3 ≈ 17.32 cm

Answer: 10√3 cm or about 17.32 cm
Example 10: Segment Area

A minor segment is formed by a chord subtending 60° in a circle of radius 42 cm.

Sector area:

= 60/360 × 22/7 × 42² = 924 cm²

Triangle area:

= 1/2 × 42² × sin(60°)

= 1/2 × 1764 × √3/2 ≈ 763.83 cm²

Segment area:

924 - 763.83 = 160.17 cm²

Answer: ≈ 160.17 cm²
Sector and chord idea
Sector formulas use the fraction θ/360 of the whole circle.

3️⃣ Solid Geometry (3D Shapes)

Cuboids and Cylinders

Example 11: Cuboid

A cuboid has dimensions 8 cm × 6 cm × 4 cm. Find its volume and surface area.

Volume: V = 8 × 6 × 4 = 192 cm³

Surface area:

SA = 2(8×6 + 6×4 + 8×4)

= 2(48 + 24 + 32) = 2(104) = 208 cm²

Answer: Volume = 192 cm³, Surface Area = 208 cm²
Example 12: Cylinder

Radius = 7 cm, height = 10 cm. Find the volume and total surface area.

Volume:

V = 22/7 × 7² × 10 = 1540 cm³

Curved surface area:

CSA = 2 × 22/7 × 7 × 10 = 440 cm²

Total surface area:

TSA = 2 × 22/7 × 7 × (7 + 10) = 44 × 17 = 748 cm²

Answer: Volume = 1540 cm³, TSA = 748 cm²

Cones and Spheres

Example 13: Cone

Base radius = 6 cm, height = 8 cm. Find its volume and curved surface area.

Slant height:

l = √(r² + h²) = √(36 + 64) = 10 cm

Volume:

V = 1/3 × 22/7 × 6² × 8 = 6336/21 ≈ 301.71 cm³

Curved surface area:

CSA = 22/7 × 6 × 10 = 1320/7 ≈ 188.57 cm²

Answer: Volume ≈ 301.71 cm³, CSA ≈ 188.57 cm²
Example 14: Sphere

Radius = 21 cm. Find its volume and surface area.

Volume:

V = 4/3 × 22/7 × 21³ = 38808 cm³

Surface area:

SA = 4 × 22/7 × 21² = 5544 cm²

Answer: Volume = 38808 cm³, Surface Area = 5544 cm²

Composite Figures

Example 15: Cylinder with a Hemisphere on Top

A cylinder of radius 7 cm and height 10 cm has a hemisphere on top. Find its total surface area.

Cylinder curved area: 2 × 22/7 × 7 × 10 = 440 cm²

Base area: 22/7 × 7² = 154 cm²

Hemisphere curved area: 2 × 22/7 × 7² = 308 cm²

Total: 440 + 154 + 308 = 902 cm²

Answer: 902 cm²

4️⃣ Earth as a Sphere

Key constants

  • Radius of Earth: R = 6400 km unless stated otherwise
  • Great circle passes through the center of the Earth
  • Latitude runs from 0° to 90° North or South
  • Longitude runs from 0° to 180° East or West
Earth geometry formulas
Along a great circle: D = (θ/360°) × 2πR
Along a parallel of latitude: D = (θ/360°) × 2πR cos φ
Radius of a parallel = R cos φ
Example 16: Two Towns on the Same Longitude

Towns A and B are at 30°N and 20°S respectively on the same longitude. Find the distance between them. Use R = 6400 km.

Angular difference = 30° + 20° = 50°

D = 50/360 × 2 × 22/7 × 6400

≈ 5587.3 km

Answer: ≈ 5587.3 km
Example 17: Two Points on the Same Latitude

Points P and Q lie on latitude 60°N with longitudes 20°E and 40°E.

Longitude difference = 20°

Radius of parallel = 6400 × cos 60° = 6400 × 0.5 = 3200 km

D = 20/360 × 2 × 22/7 × 3200

≈ 1117.46 km

Answer: ≈ 1117.46 km
Example 18: Shortest Distance on the Equator

Two points on the equator are separated by 90° in longitude.

D = 90/360 × 2 × 22/7 × 6400

≈ 10057.14 km

Answer: ≈ 10057.14 km
Earth geometry idea
Distances along a latitude use the smaller radius R cos φ.

5️⃣ Exam Tips and Practice

JAMB Mensuration Strategy

  • Always draw a sketch if none is provided
  • Use π = 22/7 when the radius is divisible by 7; otherwise use π = 3.142 if needed
  • Convert all units to the same unit before solving
  • For composite figures, separate them into familiar shapes
  • For Earth questions, start by deciding whether it is a great-circle path or a latitude path
  • √2 ≈ 1.414
  • √3 ≈ 1.732
  • sin 30° = 0.5
  • cos 30° = √3/2
  • sin 45° = cos 45° = √2/2
  • sin 60° = √3/2, cos 60° = 0.5

6️⃣ Practice Problems

Q1: A sector has area 154 cm² and radius 14 cm. Find the angle.

A = θ/360 × πr²

154 = θ/360 × 22/7 × 14²

154 = θ/360 × 616

θ = 90°

Answer: 90°
Q2: A cone has volume 1232 cm³ and base radius 7 cm. Find the height.

1232 = 1/3 × 22/7 × 7² × h

1232 = 154/3 × h

h = 24 cm

Answer: 24 cm
Q3: Two places lie on latitude 30°S with longitudes 15°E and 45°E. Find the distance between them.

Longitude difference = 30°

D = 30/360 × 2πR cos 30°

Using R = 6400 km and cos 30° = √3/2,

D ≈ 3351 km

Answer: ≈ 3351 km
Q4: A tank measures 2 m × 1.5 m × 1 m and is full of water. How many liters does it contain?

Volume = 2 × 1.5 × 1 = 3 m³

1 m³ = 1000 L

3 m³ = 3000 L

Answer: 3000 liters

Also know when to use 22/7 and when to use 3.142 for π.
