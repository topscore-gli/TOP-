📋 Introduction to Chapter 8

This chapter explains how to work with Arithmetic Progression (AP) and Geometric Progression (GP). These are common JAMB topics because they combine formulas with careful interpretation of sequences.

You need to know how to identify whether a sequence is AP or GP, find the nth term, calculate sums, and solve questions involving means and missing terms.

  • In AP, the difference between consecutive terms is constant
  • In GP, the ratio between consecutive terms is constant
  • Sum to infinity only works for GP when |r| < 1
  • Most progression questions become easy once you identify a, d or r, and n
🧠 Big idea

Always check the pattern first. If it adds or subtracts a fixed amount, think AP. If it multiplies or divides by a fixed value, think GP.

1️⃣ Arithmetic Progression (AP)

What is AP?

An Arithmetic Progression is a sequence in which each term increases or decreases by the same constant value, called the common difference.

General form
a, (a+d), (a+2d), (a+3d), ...
a = first term
d = common difference
n = number of terms

Key Formulas for AP

Formula Expression
nth term Tₙ = a + (n - 1)d
Sum of first n terms Sₙ = n/2 [2a + (n - 1)d]
Alternative sum formula Sₙ = n/2 (a + l)
Common difference d = T₂ - T₁ = T₃ - T₂

Example 1: Find the 20th term of the AP 3, 7, 11, 15, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 3, d = 7 - 3 = 4

Step 2: Use Tₙ = a + (n - 1)d

T₂₀ = 3 + (20 - 1) × 4

= 3 + 76 = 79

Answer: 79
Example 2: How many terms are in the AP 5, 9, 13, ..., 101?

Step 1: Identify known values

a = 5, d = 4, Tₙ = 101

Step 2: Use Tₙ = a + (n - 1)d

101 = 5 + (n - 1)4

96 = 4(n - 1)

n - 1 = 24

n = 25

Answer: 25 terms
Example 3: Find the sum of the first 15 terms of the AP 2, 6, 10, 14, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 2, d = 4, n = 15

Step 2: Use Sₙ = n/2 [2a + (n - 1)d]

S₁₅ = 15/2 [2(2) + (15 - 1)4]

= 15/2 [4 + 56]

= 15/2 × 60 = 15 × 30 = 450

Answer: 450
Example 4: The 3rd term of an AP is 12 and the 7th term is 28. Find the first term and common difference

Step 1: Use the nth term formula

T₃ = a + 2d = 12 ... (1)

T₇ = a + 6d = 28 ... (2)

Step 2: Subtract (1) from (2)

4d = 16

d = 4

Step 3: Substitute back into (1)

a + 2(4) = 12

a = 4

Answer: a = 4, d = 4

2️⃣ Geometric Progression (GP)

What is GP?

A Geometric Progression is a sequence in which each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a constant value called the common ratio.

General form
a, ar, ar², ar³, ...
a = first term
r = common ratio

Key Formulas for GP

Formula Expression
nth term Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹
Sum of n terms (r > 1) Sₙ = a(rⁿ - 1)/(r - 1)
Sum of n terms (r < 1) Sₙ = a(1 - rⁿ)/(1 - r)
Sum to infinity S∞ = a/(1 - r), only when |r| < 1
Common ratio r = T₂/T₁ = T₃/T₂
Geometric Mean G = √ab

Example 5: Find the 6th term of the GP 3, 6, 12, 24, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 3, r = 6/3 = 2

Step 2: Use Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹

T₆ = 3 × 2⁵

= 3 × 32 = 96

Answer: 96
Example 6: Find the sum of the first 8 terms of the GP 1, 3, 9, 27, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 1, r = 3, n = 8

Step 2: Since r > 1, use Sₙ = a(rⁿ - 1)/(r - 1)

S₈ = 1(3⁸ - 1)/(3 - 1)

= (6561 - 1)/2 = 6560/2 = 3280

Answer: 3280
Example 7: Find the sum to infinity of the GP 8, 4, 2, 1, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 8, r = 4/8 = 1/2

Step 2: Check if |r| < 1

|1/2| < 1, so the sum to infinity exists.

Step 3: Use S∞ = a/(1 - r)

S∞ = 8 / (1 - 1/2)

= 8 / (1/2) = 16

Answer: 16
Example 8: The 2nd term of a GP is 6 and the 5th term is 48. Find the first term and common ratio

Step 1: Write the term equations

T₂ = ar = 6 ... (1)

T₅ = ar⁴ = 48 ... (2)

Step 2: Divide (2) by (1)

r³ = 48/6 = 8

r = 2

Step 3: Substitute into (1)

a × 2 = 6

a = 3

Answer: a = 3, r = 2

3️⃣ Special Topics

Arithmetic Mean (AM)

If a, A, b are in AP, then the middle term is the arithmetic mean.

Arithmetic Mean
A = (a + b)/2

Geometric Mean (GM)

If a, G, b are in GP, then the middle term is the geometric mean.

Geometric Mean
G = √ab

Relationship Between AM and GM

AM-GM inequality
AM ≥ GM
(a + b)/2 ≥ √ab
AP vs GP idea
In AP, terms change by equal differences. In GP, terms change by equal ratios.

AP alternative sum Sₙ = n/2 (a + l) When first and last terms are known

5️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

Common progression question types

  • Find the nth term using the correct formula
  • Find the sum of a sequence
  • Use given terms to find a and d, or a and r
  • Identify whether the question is AP or GP from the pattern
  • In AP, the difference is constant
  • In GP, the ratio is constant
  • For sum to infinity, always check |r| < 1
  • Before applying any formula, identify a, d or r, and n

6️⃣ Practice Questions

Q1: Find the sum of the first 20 terms of the AP 5, 9, 13, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 5, d = 4, n = 20

Step 2: Use the sum formula

S₂₀ = 20/2 [2(5) + (20 - 1)4]

= 10[10 + 76] = 10 × 86 = 860

Answer: 860
Q2: The sum of the first n terms of an AP is Sₙ = n² + 3n. Find the 10th term

Step 1: Use Tₙ = Sₙ - Sₙ₋₁

S₁₀ = 10² + 3(10) = 130

S₉ = 9² + 3(9) = 108

Step 2: Subtract

T₁₀ = 130 - 108 = 22

Answer: 22
Q3: Find the sum to infinity of the GP 27, 9, 3, 1, ...

Step 1: Identify the values

a = 27, r = 9/27 = 1/3

Step 2: Check if |r| < 1

Yes, so sum to infinity exists.

Step 3: Use S∞ = a/(1-r)

S∞ = 27 / (1 - 1/3)

= 27 / (2/3) = 27 × 3/2 = 81/2 = 40.5

Answer: 40.5
Q4: If the 2nd and 5th terms of a GP are 6 and 162 respectively, find the GP

Step 1: Write the equations

ar = 6 ... (1)

ar⁴ = 162 ... (2)

Step 2: Divide (2) by (1)

r³ = 162/6 = 27

r = 3

Step 3: Substitute into (1)

a × 3 = 6

a = 2

Step 4: Write the GP

2, 6, 18, 54, 162, ...

Answer: 2, 6, 18, 54, ...

7️⃣ Quick Checklist for Progression Problems

🧠 Checklist

Identify whether the sequence is AP or GP. Find the first term a and the common difference or ratio. For AP, use Tₙ = a + (n-1)d. For GP, use Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹. For sums in GP, check whether r > 1 or r < 1. For sum to infinity, verify that |r| < 1. If two terms are given, set up equations and solve for the unknowns.
