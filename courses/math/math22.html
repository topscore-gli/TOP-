🔢 Permutations & Combinations

JAMB Mathematics • factorials • arrangements • selections • circular permutations • repeated objects

📋 Chapter Overview

This chapter covers factorials, arrangements, and selections. It is a high-yield topic that appears often in JAMB CBT exams.

  • Permutation: order matters
  • Combination: order does not matter
  • Factorials appear in almost every formula here
  • Watch carefully for special cases like circular arrangement and repeated objects
🧠 Big idea

If arrangement changes the answer, use permutation. If only selection matters, use combination.

1️⃣ Factorial Notation (!)

Foundation Concept

Definition
n! = n × (n−1) × (n−2) × ... × 3 × 2 × 1
0! = 1
n! = n × (n−1)!
Examples: Factorial Calculations

4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120

6! = 720

Important: 0! = 1

JAMB shortcut: Use n! = n(n-1)! to simplify expressions quickly before multiplying everything out.

2️⃣ Permutations

Order Matters

Linear permutations
nPr = n! / (n−r)!
n = total number of items
r = number of items being arranged
  • Use permutation when order matters
  • AB is different from BA
  • Common keywords: arrange, rank, line up, seat, position
Example 1: Linear Arrangement

In how many ways can 5 students be arranged in a row of 3 seats?

5P3 = 5! / (5−3)! = 5! / 2!

= 120 / 2 = 60

Answer: 60 ways
Example 2: Number Formation

How many 3-digit numbers can be formed from 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 without repetition?

5P3 = 5! / 2! = 60

Answer: 60 numbers

Circular Permutations

Formula
(n−1)!

In a circle, there is no fixed first position. Rotations are counted as the same arrangement.

Example 3: Circular Table

In how many ways can 6 people sit around a circular table?

(6−1)! = 5! = 120

Answer: 120 ways

Permutations with Repeated Objects

Formula
n! / (n₁! × n₂! × n₃! ...)
Example 4: Repeated Letters

How many ways can the letters of BANANA be arranged?

Total letters = 6

A appears 3 times, N appears 2 times, B appears 1 time

Number of ways = 6! / (3! × 2! × 1!)

= 720 / (6 × 2 × 1)

= 720 / 12 = 60

Answer: 60 ways

3️⃣ Combinations

Order Does Not Matter

Formula
nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]

Use combinations when only selection matters and arrangement does not matter.

  • AB and BA are the same in combination
  • Common keywords: choose, select, committee, team, group
Example 5: Committee Selection

In how many ways can a committee of 3 be chosen from 10 people?

10C3 = 10! / (3! × 7!)

= (10 × 9 × 8) / (3 × 2 × 1)

= 720 / 6 = 120

Answer: 120 ways
Example 6: Mixed Selection

A team of 4 players is selected from 7 boys and 5 girls. How many ways if 2 must be boys and 2 must be girls?

Choose 2 boys from 7:

7C2 = (7 × 6)/(2 × 1) = 21

Choose 2 girls from 5:

5C2 = (5 × 4)/(2 × 1) = 10

Total ways:

21 × 10 = 210

Answer: 210 ways

4️⃣ Permutation vs Combination

Aspect Permutation Combination
Key Question In how many ways can we arrange? In how many ways can we select?
Order Matters Does not matter
Formula nPr = n!/(n−r)! nCr = n!/[r!(n−r)!]
Memory Trick P for Position / Place C for Choose / Committee
Real-life Use Race positions, passwords, queue Teams, committees, handshakes
Value Usually larger Usually smaller

Example 7: JAMB-Style Committee Problem

A committee of 5 is to be selected from 6 men and 4 women. Find the number of committees if:

(a) no restrictions

(b) exactly 2 women

(c) at least 3 men

(a) No restrictions

10C5 = 252

(b) Exactly 2 women

Choose 3 men from 6 and 2 women from 4:

6C3 × 4C2 = 20 × 6 = 120

(c) At least 3 men

Case 1: 3 men, 2 women → 6C3 × 4C2 = 120

Case 2: 4 men, 1 woman → 6C4 × 4C1 = 15 × 4 = 60

Case 3: 5 men, 0 women → 6C5 × 4C0 = 6 × 1 = 6

Total = 120 + 60 + 6 = 186

Answers: (a) 252, (b) 120, (c) 186

📋 Quick Reference Formulas

Formula When to Apply
n! Basic factorial calculations
nPn = n! Arranging all n items linearly
nPr = n!/(n−r)! Arranging r items from n
(n−1)! Circular arrangement of n distinct items
n!/(n₁!n₂!...) Arrangement with repeated identical items
nCr = n!/[r!(n−r)!] Selecting r items from n
nCr = nC(n−r) Complementary combination property
nC0 = nCn = 1 Boundary cases
nC1 = n Selecting 1 from n

✏️ Practice Questions

JAMB Standard Practice

Q1: How many ways can MATHEMATICS be arranged?

Q2: 8 people sit around a table, and 2 particular people must sit together. How many ways?

Q3: In how many ways can a principal and vice-principal be chosen from 12 teachers?

Q4: In how many ways can 4 books be selected from 7 different books?

Answers: (1) 11!/(2!2!2!) (2) 2×6! (3) 12P2 = 132 (4) 7C4 = 35

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

Best strategy

  • Master the difference between permutation and combination
  • Watch for keywords like arrange and choose
  • Always remember 0! = 1
  • Simplify factorials by cancellation before multiplying
  • For circular arrangements, use (n−1)!, not n!
  • For repeated objects, divide by factorials of identical items
  • In mixed problems, multiply separate selections
⚠️ Common traps

Do not use permutation when combination is needed. Do not forget that AB and BA are different in permutations. Do not forget to divide by repeated factorials for identical objects. Also watch carefully for “at least” and “at most” conditions.

✅ Pre-Exam Checklist

🧠 Checklist

Revise factorial notation, especially 0! = 1. Practise linear permutations, circular permutations, permutations with repeated objects, combinations, the relation between permutation and combination, keyword identification, mixed selection problems, and “at least/at most” strategies.
