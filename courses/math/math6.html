📈 Variation

JAMB Mathematics • direct variation • inverse variation • joint variation • partial variation • combined variation • percentage change

📋 Introduction to Chapter 6

This chapter explains how quantities change in relation to one another. You will learn how to solve problems involving direct, inverse, joint, partial, and combined variation.

Variation questions in JAMB are usually straightforward if you identify the type correctly, find the constant of proportionality, and write the correct equation before substituting values.

  • The keyword in the question usually tells you the variation type
  • The constant k is always very important
  • Direct variation means same direction, inverse means opposite direction
  • Partial variation usually leads to simultaneous equations
🧠 Big idea

Variation is mostly about translating words into formulas. If you identify the relationship correctly, the rest becomes simple substitution.

1️⃣ Direct Variation

Concept

When one variable increases, the other also increases in the same ratio.

Key phrases: “y varies directly as x” or “y is directly proportional to x”.

Direct variation formulas
y = kx
y₁/x₁ = y₂/x₂
Example 1: y varies directly as x. When y = 20 and x = 2, find y when x = 8

Method 1: Formula method

y = kx

20 = k(2), so k = 10

New equation: y = 10x

When x = 8, y = 10(8) = 80

Method 2: Proportion method

20/2 = y/8

2y = 160

y = 80

Answer: y = 80
Example 2: y varies directly as the square of x. When y = 4 and x = 3, find y when x = 2

Step 1: Write the equation

y = kx²

Step 2: Find k

4 = k(3²) = 9k

k = 4/9

Step 3: Write the full equation

y = (4/9)x²

Step 4: Substitute x = 2

y = (4/9)(2²) = (4/9)(4) = 16/9

Answer: 16/9

2️⃣ Inverse Variation

Concept

When one variable increases, the other decreases in such a way that their product remains constant.

Key phrases: “y varies inversely as x” or “y is inversely proportional to x”.

Inverse variation formulas
y = k/x
xy = k
x₁y₁ = x₂y₂
Example 3: y varies inversely as x. When y = 4 and x = 3, find x when y = 6

Method 1: Formula method

y = k/x

4 = k/3, so k = 12

New equation: y = 12/x

When y = 6:

6 = 12/x

6x = 12

x = 2

Method 2: Product rule

(3)(4) = x(6)

12 = 6x

x = 2

Answer: x = 2

3️⃣ Joint Variation

Concept

One variable varies directly with two or more other variables at the same time.

Key phrase: “y varies jointly as x and z”.

Joint variation formulas
y = kxz
y = kx√z if the question says “jointly as x and the square root of z”
Example 4: x varies jointly as y and the square root of z. When x = -18, y = 2, and z = 9, find y when x = 10 and z = 4

Step 1: Write the relationship

x = ky√z

Step 2: Find k

-18 = k(2)(√9)

-18 = k(2)(3) = 6k

k = -3

Step 3: Form the equation

x = -3y√z

Step 4: Substitute new values

10 = -3y√4 = -3y(2) = -6y

y = -10/6 = -5/3

Answer: y = -5/3

4️⃣ Partial Variation

Concept

A partial variation has a constant part and a varying part.

Key phrase: “y is partly constant and partly varies as x”.

Partial variation formula
y = k₁x + k₂
Example 5: y is partly constant and partly varies directly with x. When y = 4, x = 2, and when y = 16, x = 4. Find y when x = 6

Step 1: Let y = k₁x + k₂

From first condition:

4 = 2k₁ + k₂ ... (1)

From second condition:

16 = 4k₁ + k₂ ... (2)

Step 2: Subtract (1) from (2)

12 = 2k₁

k₁ = 6

Step 3: Substitute back

4 = 2(6) + k₂

4 = 12 + k₂

k₂ = -8

Step 4: Form the equation

y = 6x - 8

Step 5: When x = 6

y = 6(6) - 8 = 28

Answer: y = 28

5️⃣ Combined Variation

Concept

Combined variation mixes direct, inverse, and sometimes joint variation in one expression.

Key phrase: “y varies jointly as x and w and inversely as the square of z”.

Combined variation formula
y = kxw / z²
Example 6: y varies jointly as x and w and inversely as the square of z. When y = 100, x = 2, w = 4, and z = 20

(a) Find the equation of variation

Step 1: Write the formula

y = kxw / z²

Step 2: Substitute known values

100 = k(2)(4) / 20² = 8k / 400

8k = 100 × 400 = 40000

k = 40000 / 8 = 5000

Equation: y = 5000xw / z²

(b) Find y when x = 1, w = 5, and z = 4

y = 5000(1)(5) / 4²

= 25000 / 16 = 1562.5

Answers: (a) y = 5000xw / z², (b) y = 1562.5

6️⃣ Percentage Increase and Decrease in Variation

Concept

When variables in a variation formula change by percentages, use multipliers.

  • 10% increase means multiply by 1.10
  • 20% decrease means multiply by 0.80
  • 40% increase means multiply by 1.40
  • 10% decrease means multiply by 0.90
Example 7: The area of a triangle varies jointly with its base and height. If the base is increased by 40% and the height is decreased by 10%, what is the percentage change in the area?

Step 1: Write the original relationship

A = kbh

Step 2: Apply the new multipliers

Base becomes 1.4b

Height becomes 0.9h

Step 3: Find the new area

Aₙₑw = k(1.4b)(0.9h)

= 1.26kbh = 1.26A

Step 4: Interpret the result

1.26A - 1.00A = 0.26A

This is a 26% increase.

Answer: 26% increase

8️⃣ Quick Tips for JAMB Exam

How to score high in variation questions

  • Identify the variation type first from the keywords
  • Always find the constant k before solving the unknown
  • Write the full variation equation before substituting new values
  • For partial variation, form two equations and solve together
  • For percentage changes, use multipliers like 1.4 and 0.9
  • Check if the final answer makes sense in the context
🧠 Fast method

Read the words carefully, pick the right variation formula, find k, then substitute. Most errors happen when the variation type is identified wrongly.

9️⃣ Practice Strategy

  • Read the question carefully and underline the variation keywords
  • Write the correct general formula first
  • Substitute known values to find k
  • Write the specific equation for that problem
  • Substitute the new values and solve

🔟 Quick Checklist for Variation Problems

🧠 Checklist

Identify the variation type from keywords. Write the general formula with k. Substitute known values to find k. Write the full equation. For partial variation, use simultaneous equations. For percentage changes, use multipliers like 1.4 and 0.9. Finally, check whether the answer is reasonable.
