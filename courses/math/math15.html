1️⃣ Trigonometric Ratios

Core Ratios (SOH CAH TOA)

SOH CAH TOA: sin = opposite/hypotenuse, cos = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan = opposite/adjacent.

Reciprocal ratios
csc θ = 1/sin θ
sec θ = 1/cos θ
cot θ = 1/tan θ
Example 1: Finding Ratios

Opposite = 3 cm, Adjacent = 4 cm. Find sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ.

Step 1: Find the hypotenuse

hyp = √(3² + 4²) = √25 = 5 cm

Step 2: Calculate the ratios

sin θ = 3/5 = 0.6

cos θ = 4/5 = 0.8

tan θ = 3/4 = 0.75

Answer: sin θ = 3/5, cos θ = 4/5, tan θ = 3/4
Right triangle and SOH CAH TOA
SOH CAH TOA works on right-angled triangles.

You can use the left-hand trick for sine and cosine values of 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°.

Example 2: Evaluating Special Angles

Evaluate (sin 60° · tan 30°) / cos 45°.

= (√3/2 × √3/3) / (√2/2)

= (3/6) / (√2/2)

= 1/2 × 2/√2

= 1/√2 = √2/2

Answer: √2/2

3️⃣ Elevation and Depression

Angles of Elevation and Depression

  • Angle of elevation is measured upward from the horizontal
  • Angle of depression is measured downward from the horizontal
  • Angle of elevation equals angle of depression in matching positions
Example 3: Angle of Elevation

From a point 50 m from the base of a tower, the angle of elevation to the top is 30°. Find the height of the tower.

tan 30° = height/50

height = 50 × tan 30° = 50 × √3/3 = 50√3/3

≈ 28.87 m

Answer: 50√3/3 m or about 28.87 m
Example 4: Angle of Depression

From the top of a 100 m cliff, the angle of depression to a boat is 45°. How far is the boat from the base of the cliff?

Angle of depression = angle of elevation = 45°

tan 45° = 100/distance

1 = 100/distance

distance = 100 m

Answer: 100 m
Elevation and depression idea
Use tangent when opposite and adjacent sides are involved.

Example 5: Bearings Problem

A ship sails 20 km on a bearing of 060°, then 15 km on a bearing of 150°. Find its distance from the starting point.

First leg:

North component = 20 cos 60° = 10 km

East component = 20 sin 60° = 10√3 km

Second leg:

South component = 15 cos 30° = 7.5√3 km

East component = 15 sin 30° = 7.5 km

Total displacement:

North-South = 10 - 7.5√3 ≈ -2.99 km

East-West = 10√3 + 7.5 ≈ 24.82 km

d = √[(2.99)² + (24.82)²] ≈ 25 km

Answer: approximately 25 km

5️⃣ Trigonometric Identities

Fundamental Identities

Pythagorean identities
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ
Quotient identities
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ

Compound Angle Formulas

Compound angles
sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B
cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B
tan(A ± B) = (tan A ± tan B)/(1 ∓ tan A tan B)

Double Angle Formulas

Double angles
sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A
cos 2A = cos²A - sin²A = 2cos²A - 1 = 1 - 2sin²A
tan 2A = 2tan A / (1 - tan²A)
Example 6: Proving an Identity

Prove that (1 - cos 2θ) / sin 2θ = tan θ.

(1 - cos 2θ) / sin 2θ

Using cos 2θ = 1 - 2sin²θ and sin 2θ = 2sin θ cos θ:

= [1 - (1 - 2sin²θ)] / [2sin θ cos θ]

= 2sin²θ / 2sin θ cos θ

= sin θ / cos θ = tan θ

Answer: LHS = RHS, so the identity is true
Example 7: Using Compound Angles

Find sin 75° without a calculator.

sin 75° = sin(45° + 30°)

= sin 45° cos 30° + cos 45° sin 30°

= (√2/2)(√3/2) + (√2/2)(1/2)

= √6/4 + √2/4 = (√6 + √2)/4

Answer: (√6 + √2)/4

Example 8: Sine Rule

In ΔABC, A = 30°, B = 45°, and a = 10 cm. Find side b.

a/sin A = b/sin B

10/sin 30° = b/sin 45°

10/0.5 = b/(√2/2)

20 = 2b/√2

b = 10√2 ≈ 14.14 cm

Answer: 10√2 cm or about 14.14 cm
Example 9: Cosine Rule

In ΔABC, a = 5 cm, b = 7 cm, and C = 60°. Find side c.

c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C

c² = 25 + 49 - 2(5)(7)cos 60°

c² = 74 - 70(0.5) = 74 - 35 = 39

c = √39 ≈ 6.24 cm

Answer: √39 cm or about 6.24 cm
Example 10: Area of a Triangle

Find the area of a triangle with sides 8 cm and 10 cm, included angle 30°.

Area = 1/2 × 8 × 10 × sin 30°

= 1/2 × 80 × 0.5 = 20 cm²

Answer: 20 cm²

Example 11: Basic Equation

Solve for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°: 2 sin x - 1 = 0.

2 sin x = 1

sin x = 1/2

Reference angle = 30°

Sine is positive in Quadrants I and II.

x = 30° or x = 150°

Answer: 30°, 150°
Example 12: Quadratic Trig Equation

Solve 2 cos²x - 3 cos x + 1 = 0 for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°.

Let y = cos x.

2y² - 3y + 1 = 0

(2y - 1)(y - 1) = 0

y = 1/2 or y = 1

Case 1: cos x = 1/2 → x = 60°, 300°

Case 2: cos x = 1 → x = 0°, 360°

Answer: 0°, 60°, 300°, 360°

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

Best strategy for trigonometry

  • Always draw diagrams for bearings and elevation questions
  • Check that your calculator is in degree mode, not radian mode
  • Use the CAST rule to determine signs in different quadrants
  • Rationalize denominators when surds appear
  • Show full working because method marks matter
  • sin 30° = 1/2, cos 30° = √3/2, tan 30° = √3/3
  • sin 45° = √2/2, cos 45° = √2/2, tan 45° = 1
  • sin 60° = √3/2, cos 60° = 1/2, tan 60° = √3
🧠 CAST rule

All positive in Quadrant I, Sine positive in Quadrant II, Tangent positive in Quadrant III, and Cosine positive in Quadrant IV.
