📋 What is a Locus?

A locus is the path traced out by a point that moves according to a given rule or condition.

In simple terms, a locus is the set of all points that satisfy a particular geometric condition.

  • “Equidistant from two points” usually gives a perpendicular bisector
  • “Constant distance from a point” usually gives a circle
  • “Equidistant from a point and a line” gives a parabola
  • Many locus questions become easier after making a quick sketch
🧠 Big idea

Locus is about turning words into geometry. Once you recognize the pattern, the answer is often a familiar line or curve.

1️⃣ Equidistant from Two Points

Perpendicular Bisector

The locus of points equidistant from two fixed points is the perpendicular bisector of the line joining them.

Distance condition
PA = PB
√[(x-x₁)²+(y-y₁)²] = √[(x-x₂)²+(y-y₂)²]
Example 1: Two Points

Find the locus of P(x,y) equidistant from A(2,3) and B(6,7).

√[(x-2)²+(y-3)²] = √[(x-6)²+(y-7)²]

Square both sides:

(x-2)²+(y-3)² = (x-6)²+(y-7)²

Expand and simplify:

x²-4x+4+y²-6y+9 = x²-12x+36+y²-14y+49

8x + 8y = 72

x + y = 9

Answer: x + y = 9
Perpendicular bisector idea
Every point on the perpendicular bisector is the same distance from A and B.

2️⃣ Equidistant from Two Lines

Angle Bisectors

The locus of points equidistant from two intersecting lines is the pair of angle bisectors of the angles between the lines.

Distance from point to line
d = |ax + by + c| / √(a² + b²)
Example 2: Two Intersecting Lines

Find the locus equidistant from 3x + 4y - 7 = 0 and 5x - 12y + 3 = 0.

|3x+4y-7|/5 = |5x-12y+3|/13

This gives two cases.

Case 1:

13(3x+4y-7) = 5(5x-12y+3)

39x+52y-91 = 25x-60y+15

14x + 112y - 106 = 0

7x + 56y - 53 = 0

Case 2:

13(3x+4y-7) = -5(5x-12y+3)

39x+52y-91 = -25x+60y-15

64x - 8y - 76 = 0

16x - 2y - 19 = 0

Answer: 7x + 56y - 53 = 0 or 16x - 2y - 19 = 0

3️⃣ Constant Distance from a Point

Circle

The locus of a point at a fixed distance from a given point is a circle.

Circle equation
(x-a)² + (y-b)² = r²
Center = (a,b), Radius = r
Example 3: Circle Locus

Find the locus of a point always 5 units from (2,-3).

(x-2)² + (y+3)² = 25

Expand:

x² - 4x + 4 + y² + 6y + 9 = 25

x² + y² - 4x + 6y - 12 = 0

Answer: (x - 2)² + (y + 3)² = 25
Circle locus idea
Every point on the circle is the same distance from the center.

4️⃣ Constant Distance from a Line

Parallel Lines

The locus of points at a fixed distance from a line is a pair of parallel lines, one on each side of the given line.

Parallel line condition
If distance is d from ax+by+c=0, then:
ax + by + c = ± d√(a²+b²)
Example 4: Parallel Lines

Find the locus of points at distance 3 units from the line 4x - 3y + 5 = 0.

|4x - 3y + 5| / √(4² + (-3)²) = 3

|4x - 3y + 5| / 5 = 3

|4x - 3y + 5| = 15

Case 1: 4x - 3y + 5 = 15 → 4x - 3y - 10 = 0

Case 2: 4x - 3y + 5 = -15 → 4x - 3y + 20 = 0

Answer: 4x - 3y - 10 = 0 or 4x - 3y + 20 = 0

5️⃣ Equidistant from a Point and a Line

Parabola

A parabola is the locus of a point whose distance from a fixed point equals its distance from a fixed line.

Geometric meaning: The fixed point is the focus and the fixed line is the directrix.

Example 5: Parabola

Find the locus equidistant from the point (0,3) and the line y = -3.

√[(x-0)²+(y-3)²] = |y+3|

Square both sides:

x² + (y-3)² = (y+3)²

x² + y² - 6y + 9 = y² + 6y + 9

x² = 12y

Answer: x² = 12y
Parabola as point-line locus
A parabola contains all points equally distant from the focus and the directrix.

📝 JAMB Practice Questions

Question 1: Basic

Find the locus equidistant from A(1,2) and B(5,6).

√[(x-1)²+(y-2)²] = √[(x-5)²+(y-6)²]

After expanding and simplifying:

8x + 8y = 56

x + y = 7

Answer: x + y = 7
Question 2: Intermediate

A point P moves so that its distance from the origin is twice its distance from (3,0). Find the locus.

√(x²+y²) = 2√[(x-3)²+y²]

Square both sides:

x²+y² = 4[(x-3)²+y²]

x²+y² = 4x² - 24x + 36 + 4y²

0 = 3x² - 24x + 36 + 3y²

x² - 8x + 12 + y² = 0

(x-4)² + y² = 4

Answer: Circle with center (4,0) and radius 2
Question 3: Advanced - Ellipse

Find the locus where the sum of distances from (-3,0) and (3,0) is always 10.

This is the definition of an ellipse.

Foci at (±3,0), so c = 3

Major axis 2a = 10, so a = 5

b² = a² - c² = 25 - 9 = 16

b = 4

x²/25 + y²/16 = 1

Answer: x²/25 + y²/16 = 1

💡 Key Tips for JAMB

Best approach for locus questions

  • Always draw a quick sketch before solving
  • Use the distance formula correctly
  • Expand algebra carefully and collect like terms neatly
  • Check your final equation by testing a point if possible
  • Recognize the standard forms of circle, parabola, ellipse, and hyperbola
Distance formula reminder
Distance = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]

⚡ Common JAMB Patterns

  • Equidistant from two points → Perpendicular bisector
  • Constant distance from point → Circle
  • Sum of distances constant → Ellipse
  • Difference of distances constant → Hyperbola
  • Equidistant from point and line → Parabola
  • Constant distance from line → Parallel lines

🧠 Checklist

Memorize the distance formula. Understand how to derive the perpendicular bisector from equal distances. Know the two-case method for angle bisectors. Be comfortable writing the equation of a circle from center and radius. Revise how parallel lines arise from a constant distance to a line. Know the focus-directrix definition of a parabola. Recognize ellipse, parabola, and hyperbola from their geometric conditions. Practise algebraic expansion carefully.
