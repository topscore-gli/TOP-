🧮 Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

JAMB Mathematics • fraction operations • decimal conversions • approximations • significant figures • percentages • ratios

📋 Introduction to Chapter 2

This chapter focuses on fractions, decimals, approximations, percentages, and ratios. These topics appear often in JAMB because they test both basic understanding and speed.

Most of the questions in this area are not difficult if your method is clear. The key is to know the right formula, simplify carefully, and avoid careless mistakes.

  • Fractions and percentages are used in many real-life JAMB word problems
  • Approximation questions often test significant figures and decimal places
  • Ratios require careful attention to total parts
  • Profit, loss, and reverse percentage are very common
🧠 Main idea

Fractions, decimals, and percentages are just different ways of expressing the same value. Learn to move between them quickly.

1️⃣ Fractions

Types of Fractions

Type Definition Example
Proper Fraction Numerator is less than denominator 3/4, 2/5
Improper Fraction Numerator is greater than or equal to denominator 7/4, 5/3
Mixed Number Whole number plus proper fraction 1 3/4, 2 2/3
Equivalent Fractions Same value in different forms 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6

Addition and Subtraction with Same Denominator

Same denominator rule
a/c + b/c = (a + b)/c
Example 1: 3/8 + 5/8

Step 1: Add the numerators

(3 + 5)/8 = 8/8

Step 2: Simplify

8/8 = 1

Answer: 1

Addition and Subtraction with Different Denominators

Find the LCM of the denominators first, then rewrite both fractions using the common denominator.

Example 2: 2/3 + 1/4

Step 1: Find the LCM of 3 and 4

LCM = 12

Step 2: Convert to equivalent fractions

2/3 = 8/12 and 1/4 = 3/12

Step 3: Add

8/12 + 3/12 = 11/12

Answer: 11/12

Multiplication of Fractions

Multiplication rule
a/b × c/d = ac/bd
Example 3: 2/5 × 3/4

Step 1: Multiply numerator by numerator and denominator by denominator

(2×3)/(5×4) = 6/20

Step 2: Simplify

6/20 = 3/10

Answer: 3/10

Division of Fractions

Division rule
a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c
Example 4: 3/4 ÷ 2/5

Step 1: Change division to multiplication by reciprocal

3/4 × 5/2

Step 2: Multiply

15/8 = 1 7/8

Answer: 1 7/8

Mixed Number to Improper Fraction

Mixed to improper rule
a b/c = [(ac) + b]/c
Example 5: Convert 2 3/5 to improper fraction

Step 1: Multiply whole number by denominator

2 × 5 = 10

Step 2: Add the numerator

10 + 3 = 13

Step 3: Place over original denominator

13/5

Answer: 13/5

2️⃣ Decimals

Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

Fraction Decimal
1/20.5
1/40.25
3/80.375
2/30.666... recurring
Example 6: Convert 5/8 to decimal

Step 1: Divide numerator by denominator

5 ÷ 8 = 0.625

Answer: 0.625

Decimals to Fractions

Use place value: tenths, hundredths, thousandths, and then simplify.

  • 0.3 = 3/10
  • 0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4
  • 0.125 = 125/1000 = 1/8

Operations with Decimals

Addition and subtraction: align decimal points carefully before calculating.

Multiplication: multiply normally, then count total decimal places.

Division: make the divisor a whole number first.

Decimal operation examples

Addition:

12.45 + 3.80 = 16.25

Multiplication:

2.5 × 1.2 = 3.00 = 3

Division:

6.25 ÷ 0.5 = 62.5 ÷ 5 = 12.5

3️⃣ Approximations and Significant Figures

Rounding to Decimal Places

Number To 1 d.p. To 2 d.p. To 3 d.p.
3.14159 3.1 3.14 3.142
2.71828 2.7 2.72 2.718
Rounding rule
If the next digit is 5 or more, round up.
If the next digit is less than 5, round down.

Significant Figures

  • All non-zero digits are significant
  • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  • Leading zeros are not significant
  • Trailing zeros after a decimal point are significant
Number 1 s.f. 2 s.f. 3 s.f.
45678 50000 46000 45700
0.00456 0.005 0.0046 0.00456
3.050 3 3.1 3.05

Standard Form

Scientific notation
a × 10ⁿ where 1 ≤ a < 10
Example 7: Standard form conversions

5600 = 5.6 × 10³

0.0034 = 3.4 × 10⁻³

245000 = 2.45 × 10⁵

4️⃣ Percentages

Conversions

From To Method
Fraction Percentage Multiply by 100
Decimal Percentage Multiply by 100
Percentage Fraction Divide by 100 and simplify
Percentage Decimal Divide by 100
Example 8: Conversion examples

3/4 × 100 = 75%

0.35 × 100 = 35%

40% = 40/100 = 2/5 = 0.4

Finding a Percentage of a Quantity

Percentage formula
Percentage/100 × Whole = Part
Example 9: What is 15% of 80?

Step 1: Change percentage to fraction or decimal

15/100 × 80 = 0.15 × 80

Step 2: Evaluate

= 12

Answer: 12

Percentage Increase and Decrease

Increase and decrease rules
Increase = Original × (1 + r/100)
Decrease = Original × (1 - r/100)
Example 10: Increase 200 by 25%

200 × 1.25 = 250

Answer: 250
Example 11: Decrease 80 by 20%

80 × 0.80 = 64

Answer: 64

Reverse Percentage

If a price increased by 20% to ₦120, then the original price is 120 ÷ 1.20 = ₦100.

Profit and Loss

Profit and loss formulas
Profit % = (SP - CP)/CP × 100
Loss % = (CP - SP)/CP × 100
Example 12: A trader bought goods for ₦5000 and sold them for ₦6500

Step 1: Find the profit

6500 - 5000 = 1500

Step 2: Divide by cost price and multiply by 100

(1500/5000) × 100 = 30%

Answer: 30%

5️⃣ Ratios and Proportions

Simplifying Ratios

Write the ratio in lowest terms by dividing by the highest common factor.

Example 13: Simplify 24:36

Step 1: Find the HCF

HCF = 12

Step 2: Divide both terms by 12

24/12 : 36/12 = 2:3

Answer: 2:3

Dividing in a Given Ratio

Example 14: Divide ₦600 in the ratio 2:3

Step 1: Find total parts

2 + 3 = 5

Step 2: First share

2/5 × 600 = ₦240

Step 3: Second share

3/5 × 600 = ₦360

Answer: ₦240 and ₦360

Direct Proportion

Direct proportion rule
a₁/b₁ = a₂/b₂
Example 15: If 5 books cost ₦250, what will 8 books cost?

Step 1: Set up the proportion

5/250 = 8/x

Step 2: Cross multiply

5x = 250 × 8

Step 3: Solve

x = (250 × 8)/5 = 400

Answer: ₦400
Ratio picture idea
In ratio problems, always count total parts first before sharing the amount.

6️⃣ Practice Problems

Question 1: Simplify 3/4 + 2/5 - 1/2

Step 1: Find the LCM of 4, 5, and 2

LCM = 20

Step 2: Rewrite all fractions

3/4 = 15/20, 2/5 = 8/20, 1/2 = 10/20

Step 3: Simplify

15/20 + 8/20 - 10/20 = 13/20

Answer: 13/20
Question 2: Express 0.0456 in standard form

Step 1: Move decimal point to make a number between 1 and 10

0.0456 = 4.56 × 10⁻²

Answer: 4.56 × 10⁻²
Question 3: A student scored 45 out of 60 marks. What percentage is this?

Step 1: Use percentage formula

(45/60) × 100

Step 2: Evaluate

= 75%

Answer: 75%
Question 4: Approximate 3.987 to 2 significant figures

Step 1: First two significant digits are 3 and 9

Step 2: Next digit is 8, so round up

3.987 ≈ 4.0

Answer: 4.0
Question 5: A car depreciates by 15% annually. If bought for ₦800,000, what is its value after 1 year?

Step 1: Use decrease formula

800000 × (1 - 0.15)

Step 2: Simplify

800000 × 0.85 = 680000

Answer: ₦680,000

8️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

How to score high in Chapter 2

  • Always simplify fractions to lowest terms in final answers
  • Check units before calculating
  • For approximations, identify the required precision first
  • In percentage questions, identify the base or 100% value
  • Practise converting quickly between fractions, decimals, and percentages
🧠 Quick checklist

Find the LCM for unlike denominators. Divide by a fraction by multiplying by its reciprocal. Count decimal places carefully. Leading zeros are not significant figures. For reverse percentage, divide by (1 ± rate). In ratio questions, find total parts first.
