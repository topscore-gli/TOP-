📋 Introduction to Euclidean Geometry

This chapter covers the most important parts of Euclidean Geometry for JAMB: angle relationships, polygons, circle theorems, and basic constructions.

Geometry questions often become easy once you draw a neat sketch, label the angles clearly, and identify the correct theorem or rule.

  • Circle theorems are among the most frequently tested geometry topics in JAMB
  • Parallel line questions depend heavily on corresponding, alternate, and interior angles
  • Polygon questions usually rely on angle-sum formulas
  • Construction questions usually test recognition of procedure, not actual drawing
🧠 Big idea

In geometry, diagrams matter. Even if one is not given, sketch one quickly. It helps you see which rule or theorem applies.

Worked Example 1: Parallel Lines

Problem: AB ∥ CD, and a transversal cuts them. If one angle is 70°, find the corresponding obtuse angle.

Step 1: Angles on a straight line sum to 180°

180° - 70° = 110°

Step 2: The corresponding angle is equal to this obtuse angle

Answer: 110°
Parallel lines and transversal idea
A transversal through parallel lines creates equal corresponding and alternate angles.

2️⃣ Polygons

Triangle Properties

  • Sum of interior angles in a triangle = 180°
  • Exterior angle = sum of the two opposite interior angles
  • Sum of exterior angles of any triangle = 360°

Types of Triangles

  • Scalene: all sides and angles are different
  • Isosceles: two equal sides and two equal base angles
  • Equilateral: all sides equal and each angle is 60°
  • Right-angled: one angle is 90° and a² + b² = c²
Worked Example 2: Triangle Angles

Problem: The angles of a triangle are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find the largest angle.

Let the angles be 2x, 3x, and 4x.

2x + 3x + 4x = 180°

9x = 180°

x = 20°

Largest angle = 4x = 80°

Answer: 80°

General Polygons

Polygon formulas
Sum of interior angles = (n - 2) × 180°
Each interior angle of a regular polygon = [(n - 2) × 180°] / n
Sum of exterior angles = 360°
Each exterior angle of a regular polygon = 360° / n
Number of diagonals = n(n - 3)/2
Worked Example 3: Regular Polygon

Problem: Find the number of sides of a regular polygon if each interior angle is 156°.

[(n - 2) × 180] / n = 156

180n - 360 = 156n

24n = 360

n = 15

Answer: 15 sides

3️⃣ Circles

JAMB favorite topic

  • Circle theorems are among the highest-frequency geometry questions in JAMB
  • Mastering these theorems can give you very quick marks

Essential Circle Theorems

Theorem Statement
1. Angle at the center Angle at the center is twice the angle at the circumference standing on the same arc
2. Same segment theorem Angles subtended by the same chord or arc are equal
3. Angle in a semicircle Angle in a semicircle is 90°
4. Cyclic quadrilateral Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°
5. Exterior angle of cyclic quadrilateral Exterior angle equals interior opposite angle
6. Tangent-radius theorem Radius is perpendicular to tangent at the point of contact
7. Alternate segment theorem Angle between tangent and chord equals angle in the alternate segment
8. Intersecting chords theorem PA × PB = PC × PD
9. Secant-tangent theorem PT² = PA × PB

Worked Example 4: Angle at the Center

Problem: A chord subtends 70° at the circumference. Find the angle at the center.

Using the theorem: Center = 2 × Circumference

Angle at center = 2 × 70° = 140°

Answer: 140°
Worked Example 5: Cyclic Quadrilateral

Problem: In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD, ∠A = 85° and ∠B = 95°. Find ∠C and ∠D.

Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°.

∠A + ∠C = 180° → 85° + ∠C = 180° → ∠C = 95°

∠B + ∠D = 180° → 95° + ∠D = 180° → ∠D = 85°

Answer: ∠C = 95°, ∠D = 85°
Worked Example 6: Intersecting Chords

Problem: Chords intersect at P. If PA = 4 cm, PB = 9 cm, PC = 6 cm, find PD.

Using PA × PB = PC × PD

4 × 9 = 6 × PD

36 = 6PD

PD = 6 cm

Answer: PD = 6 cm
Circle theorem sketch idea
The angle at the center is twice the angle at the circumference standing on the same arc.

6️⃣ JAMB Exam Strategy

Best geometry approach

  • Master circle theorems first because they appear most often
  • Draw a quick sketch for every problem
  • Label unknown angles clearly with x, y, or letters
  • Mention the theorem or rule you are using
  • Watch for parallel lines because they activate transversal angle rules
  • For regular polygons, use n = 360° / exterior angle for speed
  • Parallel lines cut by a transversal → corresponding and alternate angles are equal
  • Triangle angles always sum to 180°
  • Polygon interior sum = (n - 2) × 180°
  • In a cyclic quadrilateral, opposite angles add up to 180°
  • For intersecting chords, multiply the two segments on one chord

7️⃣ Practice Questions

Q1: Parallel lines are cut by a transversal and one angle is 125°. Find x.

If x is supplementary to 125°, then:

x = 180° - 125° = 55°

If x is a corresponding or alternate angle, then:

x = 125°

Answer: 55° or 125°, depending on the position of x
Q2: A regular polygon has interior angle 162°. How many sides does it have?

Exterior angle = 180° - 162° = 18°

n = 360° / 18° = 20

Answer: 20 sides
Q3: Chords intersect with segments 3 cm, 8 cm, 4 cm, and x cm. Find x.

Using intersecting chords theorem:

3 × 8 = 4 × x

24 = 4x

x = 6 cm

Answer: 6 cm
