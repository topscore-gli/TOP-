📏 Measures of Dispersion

JAMB Mathematics • range • mean deviation • variance • standard deviation • coefficient of variation

📋 Chapter Overview

This chapter covers the main measures used to describe how widely data values are spread out from the center.

The key ideas are range, mean deviation, variance, standard deviation, and coefficient of variation.

  • Measures of location tell where the center is
  • Measures of dispersion tell how far values spread out
  • Standard deviation is the most commonly used measure
  • Coefficient of variation helps compare different data sets
🧠 Big idea

If two data sets have the same mean, measures of dispersion help you see which one is more scattered.

1️⃣ Types of Measures of Dispersion

A. Range

Range is the simplest measure of dispersion. It is the difference between the highest and lowest values.

Formula
Range = Highest Value - Lowest Value
Example 1: Range

Find the range of: 12, 18, 25, 30, 15, 22, 28

Highest = 30, Lowest = 12

Range = 30 - 12 = 18

Answer: Range = 18

Note: Range is easy to calculate, but it is strongly affected by extreme values.

B. Mean Deviation (Average Deviation)

Mean deviation is the average of the absolute distances of the data values from the mean.

Formula
Mean Deviation = ∑|x - x̄| / n
Example 2: Mean Deviation

Find the mean deviation of: 5, 8, 12, 15, 10

Step 1: Find the mean

x̄ = (5 + 8 + 12 + 15 + 10)/5 = 50/5 = 10

x x - x̄ |x - x̄|
5 5 - 10 = -5 5
8 8 - 10 = -2 2
12 12 - 10 = 2 2
15 15 - 10 = 5 5
10 10 - 10 = 0 0

Step 2: Sum of absolute deviations

5 + 2 + 2 + 5 + 0 = 14

Step 3:

Mean Deviation = 14/5 = 2.8

Answer: Mean Deviation = 2.8

C. Variance

Variance is the average of the squared deviations from the mean.

Formula
Variance (σ²) = ∑(x - x̄)² / n
Example 3: Variance

Find the variance of: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

Step 1: Find the mean

x̄ = (4 + 6 + 8 + 10 + 12)/5 = 40/5 = 8

x x - x̄ (x - x̄)²
4 4 - 8 = -4 16
6 6 - 8 = -2 4
8 8 - 8 = 0 0
10 10 - 8 = 2 4
12 12 - 8 = 4 16
← Swipe left/right to see more →

Step 2: Sum of squared deviations

16 + 4 + 0 + 4 + 16 = 40

Step 3:

Variance = 40/5 = 8

Answer: Variance = 8

D. Standard Deviation

Standard deviation is the square root of variance. It is one of the most reliable measures of spread because it returns to the original units.

Formula
σ = √Variance = √[∑(x - x̄)² / n]
Example 4: Standard Deviation

From Example 3, variance = 8

σ = √8 ≈ 2.83

Answer: Standard Deviation ≈ 2.83

Quick note: Standard deviation is usually more useful than range when comparing spread.

E. Coefficient of Variation

Coefficient of variation is a relative measure used to compare the spread of different data sets.

Formula
CV = (σ / x̄) × 100%

2️⃣ Formulas for Grouped Data

Frequency Distribution Tables

Measure Formula
Mean x̄ = ∑fx / ∑f
Variance σ² = (∑fx² / ∑f) - x̄²
Standard Deviation σ = √[(∑fx² / ∑f) - x̄²]

  • f = frequency
  • x = midpoint of each class interval
  • fx = frequency × midpoint
  • fx² = frequency × (midpoint)²

⚡ Quick Calculation Steps

  • Find the mean first
  • Find deviations (x - x̄)
  • Use absolute values for mean deviation
  • Use squared deviations for variance
  • Take square root last for standard deviation

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

Best strategy

  • Standard deviation is the most reliable measure when comparing spread
  • Range is simple but can be distorted by outliers
  • Variance is always positive because deviations are squared
  • Standard deviation has the same units as the original data
  • For grouped data, always use class midpoints
  • Use coefficient of variation when comparing different data sets
⚠️ Common mistakes

Do not forget absolute values for mean deviation. Do not use class limits instead of midpoints for grouped data. Do not confuse variance with standard deviation. Also, do not take the square root too early.
