📋 Introduction to Number and Numeration

Number and Numeration is one of the most important foundations in JAMB Mathematics. It covers number bases, fractions, approximations, indices, logarithms, surds, sets, and number systems.

If you understand how numbers are written, converted, simplified, and classified, many JAMB questions become faster and easier.

  • Base conversion questions appear regularly in JAMB
  • Indices and logarithms are common simplification topics
  • Surds and sets often test method, not just memory
  • Most questions can be solved quickly with the right formula
🧠 Big picture

Think of this chapter as the language of numbers. Once you understand the language, algebra and geometry become much easier.

1️⃣ Number Bases

A number base or radix is the number of unique digits, including zero, used to represent numbers in a positional system.

Base Name Digits Used
2 Binary 0, 1
8 Octal 0 - 7
10 Decimal 0 - 9
16 Hexadecimal 0 - 9, A - F

Converting from Other Bases to Base 10

Expand the number using powers of the base. Multiply each digit by the base raised to its position power, starting from zero on the right.

General idea
Value = Σ(digit × baseposition)
Example 1: Convert 1011₂ to base 10

Step 1: Expand using powers of 2

1011₂ = 1×2³ + 0×2² + 1×2¹ + 1×2⁰

Step 2: Evaluate

= 8 + 0 + 2 + 1 = 11

Answer: 11₁₀
Example 2: Convert 243₅ to base 10

Step 1: Expand using powers of 5

243₅ = 2×5² + 4×5¹ + 3×5⁰

Step 2: Evaluate

= 2×25 + 4×5 + 3×1 = 50 + 20 + 3 = 73

Answer: 73₁₀

Converting from Base 10 to Other Bases

Use repeated division. Divide by the new base repeatedly and read the remainders from bottom to top.

Example 3: Convert 45₁₀ to base 2
Division Quotient Remainder
45 ÷ 2221
22 ÷ 2110
11 ÷ 251
5 ÷ 221
2 ÷ 210
1 ÷ 201

Reading the remainders upward gives 101101.

Answer: 101101₂
Example 4: Convert 200₁₀ to base 5
Division Quotient Remainder
200 ÷ 5400
40 ÷ 580
8 ÷ 513
1 ÷ 501

Reading the remainders upward gives 1300.

Answer: 1300₅

Converting Between Non-Decimal Bases

Base 10 is the bridge. Convert first to base 10, then to the target base.

Example 5: Convert 101₂ to base 4

Step 1: Convert to base 10

101₂ = 1×2² + 0×2 + 1 = 5₁₀

Step 2: Convert 5₁₀ to base 4

5 ÷ 4 = 1 remainder 1

1 ÷ 4 = 0 remainder 1

Answer: 11₄

Shortcut: Binary to base 4 can be done by grouping binary digits in pairs from the right. For example, 101₂ → 01 01 → 11₄.

2️⃣ Fractions, Decimals and Approximations

Types of Fractions

  • Proper fraction: numerator is less than denominator, e.g. 3/4
  • Improper fraction: numerator is greater than or equal to denominator, e.g. 7/4
  • Mixed number: whole number plus proper fraction, e.g. 1 3/4

Recurring Decimals to Fractions

Example 6: Convert 0.3̇ to a fraction

Step 1: Let x = 0.333...

Step 2: Multiply by 10

10x = 3.333...

Step 3: Subtract

10x - x = 3.333... - 0.333...

9x = 3

x = 3/9 = 1/3

Answer: 1/3
Example 7: Convert 0.16̇ to a fraction

Step 1: Let x = 0.1666...

Step 2: Multiply by 10 and 100

10x = 1.666... and 100x = 16.666...

Step 3: Subtract

100x - 10x = 16.666... - 1.666...

90x = 15

x = 15/90 = 1/6

Answer: 1/6

Percentage Error

Percentage error formula
Percentage Error = |Exact - Approximate| / Exact × 100%
Example 8: A length is measured as 5.2 cm instead of the true value 5.15 cm

Step 1: Find the absolute error

|5.15 - 5.2| = 0.05

Step 2: Divide by exact value and multiply by 100%

(0.05 / 5.15) × 100%

Step 3: Evaluate

≈ 0.97%

Answer: ≈ 0.97%

3️⃣ Indices and Logarithms

Laws of Indices

Law Formula Example
Multiplication aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ 2³ × 2⁴ = 2⁷ = 128
Division aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ 5⁶ ÷ 5² = 5⁴ = 625
Power of power (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ (3²)³ = 3⁶ = 729
Zero index a⁰ = 1 7⁰ = 1
Negative index a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ 2⁻³ = 1/8
Fractional index a^(m/n) = ⁿ√(aᵐ) 8^(2/3) = (³√8)² = 4
Example 9: Simplify (3⁵ × 9²) / 27³

Step 1: Express all terms in base 3

9² = (3²)² = 3⁴

27³ = (3³)³ = 3⁹

Step 2: Simplify

(3⁵ × 3⁴) / 3⁹ = 3⁹ / 3⁹ = 3⁰ = 1

Answer: 1

Logarithms

If aˣ = N, then logₐN = x.

  • logₐ(MN) = logₐM + logₐN
  • logₐ(M/N) = logₐM - logₐN
  • logₐ(Mⁿ) = n logₐM
Example 10: Solve log₂x + log₂(x - 2) = 3

Step 1: Combine the logarithms

log₂[x(x - 2)] = 3

Step 2: Remove the log

x(x - 2) = 2³ = 8

Step 3: Solve the quadratic

x² - 2x - 8 = 0

(x - 4)(x + 2) = 0

Step 4: Check validity

x = 4 or x = -2, but logarithms of negative values are undefined.

Answer: x = 4

4️⃣ Surds

Surds are irrational numbers written in root form, such as √2 or ³√5.

Example 11: Simplify √50

Step 1: Find the perfect square factor

50 = 25 × 2

Step 2: Split the root

√50 = √25 × √2 = 5√2

Answer: 5√2

Rationalizing the Denominator

Example 12: Rationalize 3/√5

Step 1: Multiply top and bottom by √5

(3/√5) × (√5/√5) = 3√5/5

Answer: 3√5 / 5
Example 13: Rationalize 2/(3 - √2)

Step 1: Multiply by the conjugate (3 + √2)

[2/(3 - √2)] × [(3 + √2)/(3 + √2)]

Step 2: Simplify denominator

(3 - √2)(3 + √2) = 9 - 2 = 7

Step 3: Simplify numerator

2(3 + √2) = 6 + 2√2

Answer: (6 + 2√2) / 7
🧠 Surd shortcut

When the denominator has one surd term, multiply by that surd. When it has two terms like a ± √b, multiply by the conjugate.

5️⃣ Sets

Sets are collections of well-defined objects. They are often tested in JAMB using notation and Venn diagrams.

  • ∈ means “is an element of”
  • ⊂ means “is a subset of”
  • ∩ means intersection
  • ∪ means union
  • A' or Aᶜ means complement of A
  • n(A) means number of elements in A
Example 14: In a class of 40 students, 20 study Mathematics, 18 study Physics, and 8 study both. Find how many study neither.

Step 1: Only Mathematics

20 - 8 = 12

Step 2: Only Physics

18 - 8 = 10

Step 3: Total studying at least one subject

12 + 10 + 8 = 30

Step 4: Neither

40 - 30 = 10

Answer: 10 students
Simple Venn diagram idea
Draw a Venn diagram first before calculating. It helps avoid double-counting the intersection.

6️⃣ Number Systems

Integers ℤ

Integers are whole numbers, including negative numbers, zero, and positive numbers.

{..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...}

Rational Numbers ℚ

Rational numbers can be written as p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

Examples: 3/4, -2, 0.75, 0.333...

Irrational Numbers

Irrational numbers cannot be written as simple fractions.

Examples: √2, π, e

Real Numbers ℝ

Real numbers include both rational and irrational numbers.

Important: Every integer is a rational number, but not every rational number is an integer.

8️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

How to score high in Number and Numeration

  • Master base conversions because they appear often
  • Remember the laws of indices, especially negative and fractional powers
  • Practise logarithm equations with care
  • Always rationalize the denominator in surd answers
  • Draw Venn diagrams in set questions
  • Use base 10 as your bridge in non-decimal conversion
  • Most of these questions should take about 1 to 2 minutes each
🧠 Quick checklist

Identify the base first. Express terms in the same base for indices. Look for perfect square factors in surds. Draw the Venn diagram before calculating in set problems.

9️⃣ Practice Questions

Q1: Convert 1101₂ to base 10

Step 1: Expand in powers of 2

1101₂ = 1×2³ + 1×2² + 0×2¹ + 1×2⁰

Step 2: Evaluate

= 8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 13

Answer: 13₁₀
Q2: Simplify (2³ × 4²) / 8¹

Step 1: Express everything as powers of 2

4² = (2²)² = 2⁴

8¹ = 2³

Step 2: Simplify

(2³ × 2⁴) / 2³ = 2⁷ / 2³ = 2⁴ = 16

Answer: 16
Q3: Rationalize 5/(2 + √3)

Step 1: Multiply by the conjugate (2 - √3)

[5/(2 + √3)] × [(2 - √3)/(2 - √3)]

Step 2: Simplify denominator

(2 + √3)(2 - √3) = 4 - 3 = 1

Step 3: Final answer

5(2 - √3)

Answer: 5(2 - √3)
