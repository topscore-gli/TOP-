⚗️ Chemical Combination (JAMB)

Laws • symbols/formulae/equations • RAM • mole concept • stoichiometry (with quick calculators)

🎯 Overview

Chemical combination is how elements/compounds react to form new substances, following fixed laws and allowing calculations (moles, masses, volumes).

Reactions rearrange atoms into new substances (mass stays constant)

What exams usually test

  • State/deduce the 4 laws from data
  • Balance chemical equations correctly
  • Calculate RAM/formula mass
  • Moles ↔ mass conversions
  • Stoichiometry and limiting reactant

1️⃣ Laws of Chemical Combination

Law of Conservation of Mass (Lavoisier)

Statement: Matter is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Total mass of reactants = total mass of products.

Example deduction: 4 g H + 32 g O → 36 g H₂O (4 + 32 = 36). Mass is conserved.

Think “balanced scale”: mass in = mass out

Law of Definite Proportions (Proust)

Statement: A compound always contains the same elements in a fixed ratio by mass, regardless of source.

Water: H:O = 1:8 by mass (2 g H with 16 g O). Any sample of pure water follows this ratio.

Law of Multiple Proportions (Dalton)

Statement: If two elements form more than one compound, the masses of one that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in small whole-number ratios.

CO vs CO₂: 12 g C combines with 16 g O (CO) and 32 g O (CO₂). Ratio of O masses = 16:32 = 1:2.

Law of Reciprocal Proportions (Richter)

Statement: If A and B combine separately with C, then the ratio of masses of A and B that combine with a fixed mass of C is the same as (or a simple multiple of) the ratio in which A and B combine with each other.

How to “see it” in questions

  • They give A–C data and B–C data
  • You compare A:B using the same mass of C
  • Then check if A and B combine in that ratio (or a simple multiple)
🧠 Memory (4 laws)

Conservation (mass stays) • Definite (fixed ratio) • Multiple (small whole numbers) • Reciprocal (ratio matches through a third element)

2️⃣ Symbols, Formulae & Equations

Balanced equations obey conservation of mass (atoms count equal both sides)

Chemical symbols

Symbol: short form for an element (usually 1–2 letters). Example: H, Na, Cl.

Chemical formulae

Formula: shows the atoms present and their ratio (subscripts).

Examples

  • H₂O = 2 H atoms + 1 O atom
  • CO₂ = 1 C atom + 2 O atoms
  • Empirical: simplest ratio (e.g., CH)
  • Molecular: actual number (e.g., C₆H₆)

Chemical equations

Equation: reactants → products, must be balanced.

Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Meaning: 2 moles H₂ react with 1 mole O₂ to form 2 moles H₂O.

⚠️ Common exam mistake

  • Do not change subscripts to balance (that changes the substance). Only change coefficients.

3️⃣ Relative Atomic Mass (RAM) (¹²C = 12)

RAM is the weighted average mass of an element’s atoms compared with 1/12 of the mass of a carbon-12 atom.

RAM values are taken from the periodic table

Key notes

  • RAM accounts for isotopes (e.g., Cl ≈ 35.5 due to ³⁵Cl and ³⁷Cl)
  • Used to calculate formula mass (e.g., H₂O = 2×1 + 16 = 18)
🧠 RAM meaning

It’s not “one atom mass” exactly—it’s an average based on isotopes.

4️⃣ The Mole Concept

1 mole = amount of substance containing 6.022 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro’s number).

Mole links particles ↔ mass using molar mass

Formula: moles (n) = mass (g) ÷ molar mass (g/mol)

5️⃣ Stoichiometry of Reactions

Stoichiometry is the calculation of quantities of reactants and products using balanced equations (mole ratios).

Steps: balance → convert to moles → use ratio → convert back

From: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Mole ratio: H₂ : O₂ : H₂O = 2 : 1 : 2

Practice examples (JAMB style)

  • Mass from moles: 4 moles H₂ → 4 moles H₂O → mass = 4×18 = 72 g
  • Limiting reagent idea: compare moles to equation ratio, the smaller “allowed amount” limits
  • Empirical formula: 40% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O → CH₂O
  • Gas volumes at STP: 1 mole gas = 22.4 dm³ (22.4 L)

⚠️ Limiting reactant (quick method)

  • Step 1: Convert each reactant to moles
  • Step 2: Divide by coefficient in balanced equation
  • Step 3: Smallest value = limiting reactant
