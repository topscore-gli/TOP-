1️⃣ Introduction — What is a Chemical Industry?

A chemical industry is any industry that uses chemical reactions to convert raw materials (natural substances) into useful products.

Why it matters (Nigeria)

  • Fertilizers → farming productivity
  • Petrochemicals → plastics
  • Pharmaceuticals → healthcare
Chemical industry converts raw materials into useful products at large scale

2️⃣ Classification of Chemical Industries (By Products)

Type Products Examples
Basic (heavy) chemicals Acids, alkalis, salts in huge quantities H₂SO₄, NaOH, NH₃, HCl
Petrochemical industries Plastics, synthetic rubber, fibres Polythene, PVC, benzene
Fertilizer industries Urea, ammonium nitrate Superphosphate, NPK
Fine chemicals Drugs, dyes, perfumes Aspirin, penicillin
Polymer/plastic industries Polythene, nylon PVC, synthetic fibres

Feature Heavy chemicals Fine chemicals

5️⃣ Relevance of Chemical Industries (Nigeria)

  • Fertilizer industry: food security (higher crop yield)
  • Pharmaceutical industry: better health care (life-saving drugs)
  • Petrochemical/plastics: packaging, building materials, clothing
  • Soap/detergent: hygiene and cleanliness

General relevance: employment, economic growth, raw materials for other sectors, improved quality of life.

6️⃣ Link to Biotechnology in Industry

Biotechnology means using living things (microbes/enzymes/GMOs) in industrial chemical processes.

Biotech area What happens Example
Fermentation Yeast converts sugar → ethanol Alcohol, beer, wine
Antibiotic production Microbes produce drugs Penicillium mould → penicillin
Enzyme technology Enzymes used to speed processes Protease (detergents), amylase (food)
Biofuels Microbes ferment biomass → bioethanol Cleaner fuel

🧠 Why biotech matters

Often cheaper, cleaner, and more specific than traditional chemical methods.

📝 6 JAMB Examples (Step-by-Step)

Example 1: Classification

Classify the industry that produces H₂SO₄ in huge quantities.

H₂SO₄ is produced in bulk → basic/heavy chemicals

Answer: Basic (heavy) chemical industry

Example 2: Raw material (Haber process)

Main raw material for the Haber process?

Haber makes ammonia → N₂ from air + H₂ from natural gas

Answer: Air and natural gas

Example 3: Relevance of fertilizer industry

State two relevance of fertilizer industry to Nigeria.

Increases crop yield → food security

Creates jobs → employment

Answer: Food security and employment

Example 4: Fine vs heavy difference

Give one difference between fine and heavy chemicals.

Heavy = bulk, cheap; fine = small quantity, expensive

Answer: Quantity produced (bulk vs small)

Example 5: Biotechnology in pharmaceuticals

One example of biotechnology use in pharmaceutical industry.

Penicillium mould produces penicillin (fermentation biotech)

Answer: Production of penicillin by Penicillium

Example 6: Classify aspirin

Classify aspirin as fine or heavy chemical and give one reason.

Aspirin is a drug → small quantity, high purity

Answer: Fine chemical (small quantity, high purity)
