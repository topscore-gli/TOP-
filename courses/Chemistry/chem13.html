1️⃣ Reversible Reactions & Dynamic Equilibrium

Reversible reaction: a reaction that can go forward and backward (shown by ⇌).

Dynamic equilibrium: in a closed system, the rate of forward reaction equals the rate of backward reaction, so concentrations stay constant.

Key words JAMB expects

  • Closed system
  • Forward rate = backward rate
  • Concentrations remain constant
Dynamic equilibrium: both reactions continue, but at equal rates

2️⃣ Factors Governing Equilibrium Position (Le Chatelier)

Le Chatelier’s principle: if a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts in the direction that reduces the disturbance.

(i) Concentration

  • Add reactant → equilibrium shifts to products (right)
  • Add product → equilibrium shifts to reactants (left)
  • Remove reactant → shifts left; remove product → shifts right

(ii) Pressure (gases only)

  • Increase pressure → shifts to side with fewer moles of gas
  • Decrease pressure → shifts to side with more moles of gas
  • If equal moles of gas on both sides → pressure change has no effect

(iii) Temperature

  • Increase temperature → favours endothermic direction
  • Decrease temperature → favours exothermic direction

(iv) Catalyst

  • Speeds forward and backward reactions equally
  • Does not change equilibrium position
  • Does not change K

3️⃣ Equilibrium Constant (Kc) + What Affects It

For: aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

Kc = [C]c[D]d / ([A]a[B]b)

Important rules

  • Use equilibrium concentrations only
  • Pure solids and pure liquids are not included in Kc expression
  • Kc changes only with temperature

4️⃣ Simple Required Examples

(A) Action of steam on iron

3Fe(s) + 4H₂O(g) ⇌ Fe₃O₄(s) + 4H₂(g)

  • Only gases appear in Kc: water vapour and hydrogen gas
  • Kc = [H₂]4 / [H₂O]4

(B) Nitrogen(IV) oxide / dinitrogen tetroxide equilibrium

N₂O₄(g) ⇌ 2NO₂(g)

  • Kc = [NO₂]2 / [N₂O₄]
  • Pressure increase shifts to fewer moles (left, N₂O₄ side)
N₂O₄ ⇌ 2NO₂: more pressure favours N₂O₄ (fewer gas moles)

5️⃣ Industrial Applications of Le Chatelier

Process Equation How Le Chatelier is used
Haber process (ammonia) N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ High pressure (fewer moles on right), moderate T, catalyst
Contact process (H₂SO₄) 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃ Moderate T (exothermic), catalyst, increased pressure
Methanol synthesis CO + 2H₂ ⇌ CH₃OH High pressure favours fewer moles

🧮 6 JAMB-Style Calculations (Step-by-Step)

Example 1: Write Kc expression

N₂O₄(g) ⇌ 2NO₂(g)

Kc = [NO₂]² / [N₂O₄]

Example 2: Calculate Kc from equilibrium concentrations

If at equilibrium: [NO₂] = 0.40 mol/dm³, [N₂O₄] = 0.20 mol/dm³

Kc = (0.40)² / 0.20 = 0.16 / 0.20 = 0.80

Example 3: Find an equilibrium concentration (rearrange)

For N₂O₄ ⇌ 2NO₂, if Kc = 4.0 and [N₂O₄] = 0.50, find [NO₂]

Kc = [NO₂]² / [N₂O₄]

[NO₂]² = Kc × [N₂O₄] = 4.0 × 0.50 = 2.0

[NO₂] = √2.0 = 1.41 mol/dm³

Example 4: Effect of pressure on N₂O₄ ⇌ 2NO₂

Increase pressure → shifts to fewer moles of gas → left (towards N₂O₄)

Example 5: Effect of temperature on Kc

For an exothermic forward reaction:

Increase temperature → shifts backward → less products at equilibrium → Kc decreases

Example 6: Steam on iron Kc expression

3Fe(s) + 4H₂O(g) ⇌ Fe₃O₄(s) + 4H₂(g)

Solids not included → Kc = [H₂]⁴ / [H₂O]⁴
