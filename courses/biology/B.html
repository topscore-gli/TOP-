Topics

Living Organisms🐐🦠

Self-sustaining biological systems that exhibit metabolism, growth, reproduction, and response to stimuli—ranging from single-celled bacteria to complex multicellular life.

Evolution Amongst Living Organisms

The process by which species change over generations through genetic variation and natural selection, adapting to their environments and diversifying into new forms of life.

Adaptation🥶

Inherited traits—structural (physical features), functional (physiological processes), or behavioral (actions/responses)—that enhance an organism's survival and reproduction in its specific environment.

Internal Structure Of a Flowering 🌵

Roots, stem, leaves, and reproductive organs organized into dermal, vascular, and ground tissues for absorption, transport, photosynthesis, and reproduction.

Nutrition🍝

The process by which organisms obtain and utilize nutrients—carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, vitamins, and minerals—to fuel metabolism, growth, and energy production.

Transport🚗

The movement of water, nutrients, and gases throughout an organism—via vascular tissues (xylem and phloem) in plants, and circulatory systems in animals.

Respiration🫁

The metabolic process that releases energy from glucose—through aerobic (oxygen-dependent) or anaerobic pathways—to power cellular activities.

Excretion💩

The elimination of metabolic waste products and harmful substances from an organism to maintain internal homeostasis.

Support And Movement🚶‍♀️🚶‍♂️

Structural frameworks (skeletons, cell walls) that maintain body shape, combined with muscular or ciliary systems enabling locomotion and internal transport.

Reproduction🤰

The biological process by which organisms produce offspring—either asexually (single parent, genetically identical) or sexually (two parents, genetic variation)—ensuring species continuity.

Growth🌱

Irreversible increase in size and mass through cell division, enlargement, and differentiation—driven by nutrient uptake and metabolic activity.

Co-Ordination And Control🫀

Regulation of body functions through nervous and hormonal systems, enabling rapid responses and long-term homeostatic balance.

Factors Affecting The Distribution Of Organisms⛈️

Abiotic factors (climate, water, soil, light) and biotic factors (competition, predation, disease) that determine where species can survive and thrive.

Symbiotic Interactions

Close, long-term relationships between plants and animals—mutualism (both benefit), commensalism (one benefits), or parasitism (one harmed)—shaping ecosystem dynamics.

Natural Habitats🏡

The specific environments where organisms naturally live and thrive, characterized by distinct physical conditions and biological communities.

Local (Nigeria) Biomes🇳🇬

Major ecological regions in Nigeria—including tropical rainforests, savannas (Guinea and Sudan), freshwater wetlands, and coastal mangroves—each with distinct climate, vegetation, and wildlife adapted to specific environmental conditions.

Ecology Of Population👨‍👩‍👧‍👧👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

The study of population dynamics—size, density, distribution, age structure, and growth patterns—shaped by births, deaths, immigration, emigration, and environmental limiting factors.

Soil🌕

The upper layer of Earth's crust—composed of rock particles, organic matter, water, air, and living organisms—that supports plant growth and regulates nutrient cycling.

Humans And Environment🌎

The reciprocal relationship between human activities and the natural world—encompassing resource use, pollution, conservation, and sustainable management of ecosystems.

Variations In Population👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Differences in traits among individuals of a species—arising from genetic diversity, environmental influences, or both—providing the raw material for natural selection and adaptation.

Heredity🧬

The transmission of genetic traits from parents to offspring through DNA, determining inherited characteristics across generations.

Biotechnology💻

The application of biological systems and organisms to develop products and technologies—spanning genetic engineering, fermentation, pharmaceuticals, and agriculture.

Theories Of Evolution🦠🐛🦋🐌🐵🧑‍🦱

Scientific explanations for species change over time—encompassing natural selection, genetic drift, and speciation mechanisms that drive biodiversity.
