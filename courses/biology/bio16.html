Biomes in Nigeria 🚀

Study Guide + JAMB Quiz

What is a Biome?

A biome is a large natural community of plants, animals, and other organisms adapted to a specific climate and environment. Nigeria's biomes form a south-to-north gradient due to decreasing rainfall.

Key JAMB skills: locate biomes on a map, describe climate/vegetation/fauna/soil, and explain human activities.

1. Mangrove Swamp Forest

Location: Niger Delta coast (Lagos, Delta, Rivers, Bayelsa, Akwa Ibom, Cross River)

Climate: >2,500 mm rain, hot & humid, tidal flooding

Vegetation: Red & white mangroves, nipa palms (aerial roots, salt-tolerant)

Fauna: Crabs, fish, crocodiles, herons

Human use: Fishing, oil & gas (but pollution risk)

2. Tropical Rainforest

Location: Southern Nigeria (Edo, Delta, Ondo, Ogun, Imo, Cross River…)

Climate: >2,000 mm rain, high humidity, 25–30°C

Vegetation: Multi-layered evergreen (mahogany, iroko, oil palm, cocoa)

Fauna: Monkeys, chimpanzees, elephants, parrots

Human use: Timber, cash crops, oil — but deforestation threat

3. Guinea Savanna (Southern & Northern)

Location: Middle belt (Kogi, Benue, Kwara, Niger, Osun, Kaduna…)

Climate: 1,000–1,500 mm rain, distinct wet/dry seasons

Vegetation: Tall grasses + scattered trees (shea, locust bean, acacia)

Fauna: Antelopes, lions, giraffes (Yankari reserve)

Human use: Yams, maize, cattle rearing

4. Sudan Savanna

Location: Northern belt (Kano, Kaduna, Jigawa, southern Borno…)

Climate: 500–1,000 mm rain, long dry season

Vegetation: Short grasses, baobab, thorny acacia

Fauna: Gazelles, ostriches, camels

Human use: Millet, sorghum, nomadic herding

5. Sahel Savanna / Semi-Desert

Location: Far north (Sokoto, Kebbi, northern Katsina, Yobe, Borno)

Climate: <500 mm rain, extreme heat

Vegetation: Sparse grasses, date palms in oases

Fauna: Camels, lizards, scorpions

Human use: Nomadic herding, irrigated farming

6. Montane (Highland) Forests & Grasslands

Location: Jos Plateau, Obudu, Mambilla Plateau

Climate: Cooler (15–25°C), higher rainfall

Vegetation: Pines, eucalyptus, grasslands

Human use: Tea, coffee, potatoes, tourism

Quick Summary Table

South → North rainfall decreases → biomes get drier:

  • Mangrove → Rainforest → Guinea Savanna → Sudan Savanna → Sahel
  • Highlands = cooler pockets anywhere elevation >1,000 m
