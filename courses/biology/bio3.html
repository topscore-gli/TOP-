JAMB Chapter 3: How Organisms Survive Their Environment

🎨 A. Adaptive Colouration

Definition: Special coloration patterns that help organisms survive by blending with their environment or sending warning signals to predators/prey.

1. Counter-Shading STRUCTURAL

Mackerel showing countershading: dark top, light bottom

🔍 Key Concept

  • Dark on top, light underneath
  • From above: Dark back blends with deep water/ground
  • From below: Light belly blends with sky/sunlight
Organism Top Color Bottom Color Function
Fish (mackerel, sharks) Dark blue/black Silvery-white Camouflage from predators above & below
Toads Brown/green Pale cream Hide from predators on land
Snakes (grass snakes) Dark olive Yellowish-white Blend with vegetation and sky
Penguins Black White Hide from aquatic predators

2. Warning Colouration STRUCTURAL

Poison dart frog: bright colors warn predators of toxicity

Red poison dart frog - classic aposematic coloration

⚠️ Key Concept

  • Bright, conspicuous colors (red, orange, yellow, black)
  • Signals: "I'm poisonous/dangerous — don't eat me!"
  • Opposite of hiding — meant to be seen!
Organism Color Pattern Defense Mechanism
Mushrooms (Amanita) Red with white spots Contains deadly toxins
Poison dart frogs Bright blue, yellow, red Skin toxins (batrachotoxin)
Monarch butterflies Orange & black Toxic from milkweed diet
Coral snakes Red, yellow, black bands Venomous bite

🐝 B. Behavioural Adaptations in Social Animals

1. Termite Castes (Social Insects)

Termite castes: Queen, King, Workers, Soldiers, and Reproductives

Termite life cycle showing development into different castes

🏰 The Colony Structure

  • Division of labor — each caste has specialized function
  • Cooperative care of young
  • Overlap of generations (workers help raise siblings)
Caste Appearance Function Key Feature
Queen Largest, swollen abdomen Egg production Lays 6,000-7,000 eggs/day
King Smaller, winged initially Mates with queen Stays with queen for life
Workers Most numerous, wingless, soft Build, forage, feed, care Blind, sterile, do all work
Soldiers Large heads, powerful jaws Defend colony Cannot feed themselves!
Reproductives (Alates) Winged, dark-colored Start new colonies Leave during nuptial flight

2. Basking in Lizards BEHAVIOURAL

Basking behaviour: absorbing heat from the environment

What: Lizards lie in sun to warm up

Why: They are ectotherms (cold-blooded) — need external heat for:

  • Increased metabolism
  • Activity for hunting
  • Digestion of food

When: Early morning (after cold night)

3. Territorial Behaviour BEHAVIOURAL

Red-headed agama lizard: bright colors signal territory and fitness

Male agama displaying bright colors to defend territory

Definition: Defending specific area against same-species intruders

Purposes:

  • Secures food resources
  • Attracts mates (shows fitness)
  • Protects breeding/nesting sites

Examples: Male lions, singing birds, red-headed agama lizards

4. Hibernation vs. Aestivation BEHAVIOURAL

Bear in winter den during hibernation

African lungfish in aestivation cocoon during dry season

Lungfish buried in mud with mucus cocoon for aestivation

❄️ HIBERNATION
  • When: Winter
  • Why: Escape cold + food shortage
  • Animals: Bears, hedgehogs, bats
  • Body: Slow heartbeat, low temp
☀️ AESTIVATION
  • When: Summer
  • Why: Escape heat + drought
  • Animals: Lungfish, snails, crocodiles
  • Body: Burrow in mud, mucus cocoon
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"HiberNATION = Winter NATION" | "AestiVATION = Summer VACATION"

🌟 SPECIAL CASE: Lungfish

  • Lives in African rivers that dry up seasonally
  • Burrows into mud, secretes mucus cocoon
  • Breathes air through lungs (not just gills!)
  • Can survive months/years without water
  • Key adaptation: Both gills AND lungs
