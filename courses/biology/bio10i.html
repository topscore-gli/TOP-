🧬 Chapter 10: Reproduction

Full Master Note (Definitions + Examples) for JAMB Revision

🌟 Introduction

Definition (Reproduction): Reproduction is the biological process by which living organisms produce new individuals of their own kind, ensuring continuity of species.

General life cycle idea: growth → reproduction → new organism

🔍 Why Reproduction Matters

  • Ensures continuity of species
  • Maintains population size
  • In sexual reproduction, it creates variation (important for survival)

🔁 A. Asexual Reproduction

Definition: Asexual reproduction is a type of reproduction in which only one parent is involved and no fusion of gametes occurs, producing genetically identical offspring (clones).

Amoeba reproducing by fission (one splits into two)

1. Fission ASEXUAL

Definition: Fission is a type of asexual reproduction in which a single parent cell divides into two or more identical daughter cells.

✔ Examples

  • Paramecium
  • Amoeba
  • Euglena
  • Bacteria (e.g., E. coli)
  • Plasmodium (multiple fission)

Explanation (Simple):

  • Nucleus divides first
  • Cytoplasm divides
  • New identical organisms are formed

Multiple fission: One cell produces many offspring (e.g., Plasmodium).

2. Budding ASEXUAL

Definition: Budding is a type of asexual reproduction where a new organism develops as an outgrowth (bud) from the parent body.

Hydra budding: bud grows and may detach

✔ Examples

  • Yeast
  • Hydra
  • Sponges
  • Corals
  • Some fungi

Explanation: Bud forms → grows → detaches (or stays attached) to form a colony.

3. Natural Vegetative Propagation ASEXUAL

Definition: Natural vegetative propagation is a form of asexual reproduction where new plants grow from vegetative parts (root, stem, or leaf) of the parent plant naturally.

Potato tubers: buds (“eyes”) can grow into new plants

Vegetative Part Type Examples Key Idea
Stem Tuber/Rhizome/Bulb/Runner Potato, ginger, onion, strawberry, yam Has buds that sprout
Root Tuberous roots Sweet potato, cassava, dahlia, beetroot, carrot Stores food + can regrow
Leaf Leaf buds Bryophyllum, begonia, kalanchoe, African violet, sansevieria Leaf margins form plantlets

4. Artificial Vegetative Propagation ASEXUAL

Definition: Artificial vegetative propagation is the deliberate production of new plants from vegetative parts using human methods.

Grafting: joining two plants to grow as one

Method Definition Examples Why It’s Done
Cutting Growing a new plant from a cut stem/root/leaf Cassava, sugarcane, rose, hibiscus, croton Fast multiplication
Grafting Joining parts of two plants so they grow as one Mango, citrus, apple, pear, guava Combine good traits
Budding Inserting a bud from one plant into another plant Orange, lemon, rubber, peach, rose Improved varieties
Layering Bending a branch, covering with soil to form roots before cutting Bougainvillea, jasmine, grapevine, rubber plant, blackberry High success rate

  • Rapid multiplication of plants

⚖️ Asexual vs Sexual Reproduction

Feature Asexual Sexual
Definition One parent, no gametes Two parents, involves gametes
Variation None (clones) Present
Example Yeast, amoeba Humans, flowering plants

🌸 B. Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

Definition: Sexual reproduction in plants involves the fusion of male and female gametes to produce a zygote.

1. The Flower

Definition (Flower): A flower is the reproductive structure of flowering plants.

Basic flower structure (male + female parts)

Part Definition Function
Sepals Outer parts that protect the flower bud Protection before bloom
Petals Colored parts that attract pollinators Attraction (insects, birds)
Stamen Male part (anther + filament) producing pollen Male gamete production
Carpel/Pistil Female part (stigma, style, ovary) containing ovules Receives pollen + contains egg cells

2. Pollination

Definition: Pollination is the transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of a flower.

Pollination: pollen moves from anther to stigma

🌼 Self-Pollination
  • Definition: Transfer within same flower/plant
  • Examples: Pea, rice, wheat, tomato, groundnut
  • Effect: Less variation
🌼 Cross-Pollination
  • Definition: Transfer from one plant to another
  • Examples: Maize, cocoa, oil palm, pawpaw, date palm
  • Effect: More variation

🧭 Agents of Pollination

  • Wind
  • Insects
  • Water
  • Animals
  • Man
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"Pollen to Stigma = Pollination" (PS = Pollination Simple)

3. Fertilization

Definition: Fertilization is the fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.

Fertilization in flowering plants (pollen tube growth)

Process:

  • Pollen lands on stigma
  • Pollen tube grows down the style
  • Male nucleus fuses with egg nucleus
  • Zygote forms

⚠️ JAMB Hot Point

  • Pollination ≠ Fertilization
  • Pollination = transfer of pollen
  • Fertilization = fusion of gametes

4. Fruit and Fruit Types

Definition (Fruit): A fruit is the ripened ovary of a flower containing seeds.

Fruit develops mainly from the ovary after fertilization

Fruit Type Definition Examples
Simple Develop from one ovary Mango, tomato, orange, pepper, guava
Aggregate Develop from many ovaries of one flower Strawberry, raspberry, blackberry, custard apple, soursop
Multiple Develop from many flowers Pineapple, breadfruit, fig, mulberry, jackfruit
Succulent Fleshy fruits with high water content Banana, pawpaw, watermelon, orange, mango

👶 C. Reproduction in Mammals

Definition: Mammalian reproduction is a type of sexual reproduction involving internal fertilization and development of the embryo inside the female body.

Fertilization: sperm meets egg to form a zygote

1. Male Reproductive System

Definition: The male reproductive system consists of organs responsible for the production and delivery of sperm.

Male reproductive organs (simplified)

✔ Main Functions

  • Testes → sperm production
  • Scrotum → temperature regulation
  • Sperm duct → sperm transport
  • Penis → sperm transfer

2. Female Reproductive System

Definition: The female reproductive system consists of organs responsible for the production of eggs and development of the embryo.

Female reproductive organs (simplified)

✔ Main Functions

  • Ovary → produces ova (eggs)
  • Fallopian tube → fertilization usually happens here
  • Uterus → development of embryo/fetus
  • Vagina → birth canal

3. Fertilization in Mammals

Definition: Fertilization in mammals is the fusion of sperm and egg inside the female body (usually in the fallopian tube).

Process:

  • Sperm meets egg
  • Fusion → zygote
  • Zygote divides → embryo

4. Development

Definition: Development is the growth of the embryo into a fully formed organism.

Development: zygote → embryo → fetus → birth

Stage Meaning
Zygote First cell formed after fertilization
Embryo Early developing organism (organs begin forming)
Fetus Later stage; body parts are visible and grow bigger
Birth Baby is delivered from uterus through vagina

🧪 Modern Methods of Reproduction

1. IVF (In Vitro Fertilization)

Definition: IVF is a method where fertilization occurs outside the body in a laboratory.

IVF: egg and sperm meet in a lab, embryo is transferred to uterus

✔ Uses / Examples

  • Human fertility treatment
  • Animal breeding
  • Research use
  • Surrogacy
  • Genetic improvement

2. Birth Control

Definition: Birth control refers to methods used to prevent pregnancy.

Barrier method example: condom prevents sperm from reaching egg

Type Examples How It Works (Simple)
Barrier Condom, diaphragm, sponge, cervical cap, female condom Blocks sperm from entering uterus
Hormonal Pills, injections, implants, patches, ring Stops ovulation / thickens cervical mucus
Devices IUD, implants Prevents fertilization/implantation
Natural Abstinence, safe period, withdrawal Reduces chance of sperm meeting egg

⚠️ FINAL JAMB MASTER POINTS

  • Asexual → one parent, no variation
  • Sexual → two parents, variation
  • Pollination ≠ Fertilization
  • Fertilization = fusion of gametes
  • Ovule → seed, Ovary → fruit
  • Cross-pollination → stronger offspring
  • Mammals → internal fertilization
  • IVF → fertilization outside body
  • Vegetative propagation → important in agriculture
