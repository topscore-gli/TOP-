🚽 Excretion

JAMB Chapter: Elimination of Metabolic Wastes

📌Definition & Significance

What is Excretion?

Excretion is the elimination of metabolic waste products and other useless substances from the body of a living organism.

Why is it Important?

Function Explanation
🛡️ Homeostasis Maintains stable internal environment
☠️ Detoxification Removes harmful/toxic substances
⚖️ Osmoregulation Regulates water and salt balance
🩺 Health maintenance Prevents poisoning from waste buildup

🔬Part A: Types of Excretory Structures

1️⃣ Contractile Vacuole PROTOZOANS

Amoeba showing contractile vacuole and other organelles

🔍 Key Features

  • Location: Cytoplasm of unicellular organisms (Amoeba, Paramecium)
  • Structure: Fluid-filled sac surrounded by mitochondria
  • Function: Osmoregulation → pumps out excess water
  • Key Point: Prevents cell bursting (lysis) in hypotonic environment
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"Contractile = Control water" — It contracts to squeeze water out!

2️⃣ Flame Cell FLATWORMS

Planaria flatworm showing flame cells and gastrovascular cavity

🔥 Why "Flame" Cell?

  • Hollow cell with bunch of cilia that beat rapidly
  • Looks like flickering flame under microscope
  • Simplest form of "kidney" in invertebrates!

Function: Filters waste from intercellular fluid. Cilia beat to drive waste through tubules opening to exterior via pores.

4️⃣ Malpighian Tubule INSECTS

Malpighian tubules attached to insect gut junction

🎯 Key Features

  • Long, thin, blind-ended tubules attached to gut junction
  • Bathed in hemolymph (insect "blood")
  • Absorbs nitrogenous waste (uric acid) from hemolymph
  • Reabsorbs water and salts — waste passes to hindgut
  • Super efficient: Allows life in dry environments!

💡 JAMB Favorite: Desert insects excrete uric acid (insoluble, needs minimal water) vs. aquatic insects excrete ammonia (soluble, needs lots of water).

5️⃣ Kidney ⭐ VERTEBRATES

Kidney structure and nephron — the functional unit

Detailed nephron showing Loop of Henle and reabsorption sites

The Nephron (Functional Unit)

1. GLOMERULUS — High pressure filtration (180L filtrate/day!)
2. PCT — Reabsorbs glucose, amino acids, water, salts
3. LOOP OF HENLE — Creates concentration gradient in medulla
4. DCT — Fine-tuning (hormone-controlled: ADH, Aldosterone)
5. COLLECTING DUCT — Final water reabsorption → urine to renal pelvis

Osmoregulation in Kidneys

Condition Hormone Action Result
Dehydration ADH ↑ DCT & collecting ducts more permeable to water Concentrated urine
Overhydration ADH ↓ DCT & collecting ducts less permeable Dilute urine
Low blood Na⁺ Aldosterone Increases Na⁺ reabsorption in DCT Retains salts, maintains BP

6️⃣ Stomata PLANTS

Stomata showing guard cells and stomatal pores

Microscopic view of guard cells controlling pore opening

🌿 Key Features

  • Location: Mostly on lower leaf epidermis
  • Structure: Two kidney-shaped guard cells with chloroplasts
  • Function: Transpiration (99% of water uptake!), gas exchange
  • Mechanism: Guard cells swell → stomata OPEN; lose water → CLOSE

7️⃣ Lenticels WOODY PLANTS

Lenticels on woody stem — the "breathing pores" of bark

Function: Gas exchange in woody stems when stomata are covered by bark. Called "breathing pores" — allows O₂ in, CO₂ & water vapor out.

⚠️ CRITICAL JAMB EXAM TIPS

  • Contractile vacuole: Found in Amoeba/Paramecium — mainly for osmoregulation (NOT true excretion)
  • Flame cell: Flatworms only — cilia create "flame" appearance
  • Malpighian tubule: Insects only — uric acid excretion (water conservation)
  • Loop of Henle: Creates concentration gradient for urine concentration
  • ADH: Increases water reabsorption → concentrated urine
  • Desert insects: Excrete uric acid (insoluble, saves water) vs. ammonia (aquatic)
  • Plant products: Tannins (tanning), Resins (varnish), Alkaloids (medicine)
  • Structure evolution: Contractile vacuole → Flame cell → Nephridium → Malpighian tubule → Kidney

📋Quick Revision Checklist

Must-Know Definitions:

  • Excretion: Elimination of metabolic wastes
  • Osmoregulation: Regulation of water/salt balance
  • Ultrafiltration: High pressure filtration in glomerulus
  • Selective reabsorption: Taking back useful substances in PCT
Structures
  • Contractile vacuole: Amoeba
  • Flame cell: Flatworms
  • Nephridium: Earthworms
  • Malpighian tubule: Insects
Hormones
  • ADH: Water reabsorption
  • Aldosterone: Salt reabsorption
  • Both act on DCT/collecting duct
