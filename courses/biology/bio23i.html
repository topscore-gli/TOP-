🦴 Chapter 23: Evolution (JAMB)

Theories of evolution + evidences (fossils, anatomy, embryology, genetics, mutation)

1️⃣ What is Organic Evolution?

Organic evolution is the sum total of all adaptive changes that have taken place over a very long period of time. These changes result in the diversity of forms, structures, and functions among organisms.

Evolution explains how organisms become diverse over long periods

Simple meaning: Life started simple, and over millions of years organisms changed to fit their environments, forming many species.

🧠 Memory line:

"Evolution = adaptive changes over time → diversity."

2️⃣ Theories of Evolution

a) Lamarck’s Theory

Jean-Baptiste Lamarck (early 1800s) proposed early ideas about evolution.

Classic example: giraffe neck (Lamarck vs Darwin explanation)

Key Ideas

  • Use and disuse: used parts become stronger; unused parts weaken
  • Inheritance of acquired characteristics: acquired changes passed to offspring

✅ Important Note

  • Lamarck helped introduce the idea that species change, but his mechanism (acquired traits inherited) is disproved.

b) Darwin’s Theory (Natural Selection)

Charles Darwin (1859, “On the Origin of Species”) proposed natural selection (survival of the fittest).

Natural selection: favourable traits survive and increase in population

Key Ideas (Step-by-step)

  • Overproduction: more offspring are produced than can survive
  • Variation: individuals are different
  • Competition: limited resources
  • Survival + reproduction: favourable traits survive and reproduce more
  • Inheritance: favourable traits passed to offspring
  • Gradual change: over many generations, species evolve

Example: During drought, birds with longer beaks reach food → survive → reproduce → more long-beak birds in later generations.

✅ Key Point

  • Darwin’s natural selection is accepted today and forms the foundation of modern evolution.

c) Organic Theory (General idea)

This refers to the general idea that living things evolved from simpler ancestors through gradual changes. Lamarck and Darwin both contributed, but Darwin’s mechanism is accepted.

Comparison Table (Lamarck vs Darwin)

Aspect Lamarck Darwin (Natural Selection)
Main mechanism Use & disuse + acquired traits inherited Natural selection on existing variations
When changes happen During lifetime Over many generations
Acquired traits inherited? Yes (wrong) No (genetic traits inherited)
Role of environment Directly causes change Selects which variations survive
Accepted today? No Yes

Lamarck = “lifetime change” | Darwin = “selection over generations”

3️⃣ Evidences of Evolution

JAMB asks you to state and explain evidences. Know at least 4 well.

1) Fossil records (Palaeontology)

Fossils show organisms that lived long ago

  • Fossils are remains/traces of ancient organisms in rocks
  • Deeper layers usually have older, simpler fossils
  • Transitional fossils show gradual change (e.g., Archaeopteryx)

Archaeopteryx: transitional fossil linking reptiles and birds

2) Comparative anatomy

Definition: comparing structures in different organisms.

a. Homologous structures

Meaning: same basic structure, different functions → common ancestry.

Homologous forelimbs: same bones, different uses (arm, wing, flipper)

b. Analogous structures

Meaning: different structure, same function → convergent evolution.

Analogous wings: bird vs insect (same function, different structure)

c. Vestigial structures

Meaning: reduced structures with little/no function → remnants from ancestors.

Vestigial structures: appendix, tailbone, wisdom teeth

3) Physiology / Biochemistry

Idea: similar enzymes, hormones, and biochemical pathways suggest common ancestry.

Many organisms share key enzymes/proteins (biochemical similarity)

4) Embryology (Comparative embryology)

Idea: early embryos of related organisms often look alike (gill slits, tail in early stages).

Similar early embryos suggest common ancestry

5) Modern evidence (Genetics + mutation)

Genetic/DNA evidence: closely related species have more similar DNA.

Mutation: creates new genetic variation (raw material for natural selection).

DNA comparison: more similarity = closer relationship

Observed Evolution (Easy example)

  • Antibiotic resistance: bacteria mutate; resistant ones survive and reproduce.

Antibiotic resistance is evolution happening in real time

Other evidence (sometimes tested)

  • Biogeography: distribution of organisms (e.g., unique island species)
  • Evolutionary trends: plants (algae → moss → ferns → seed plants); animals (invertebrates → fish → amphibians → reptiles → birds/mammals)

📌 Quick Summary for JAMB

  • Organic evolution = adaptive changes over time → diversity
  • Lamarck = use/disuse + acquired traits (wrong mechanism)
  • Darwin = natural selection (correct mechanism)
  • Evidences = fossils, anatomy (homologous/vestigial), physiology, embryology, genetics + mutation
🧠 Evidence shortcut:

"F-A-P-E-G" → Fossils, Anatomy, Physiology, Embryology, Genetics

⚡ Exam Tips

  • Memorize the definition of organic evolution as written
  • Be able to list 4 evidences with short explanations
  • Always compare Lamarck vs Darwin in a table form (easy marks)
  • Many JAMB questions repeat on fossils, homologous vs analogous, and natural selection
