Clear Definitions + Germination (Epigeal vs Hypogeal) for JAMB

🌱 8a. Meaning of Growth

Definition (Growth): Growth is the irreversible permanent increase in size and dry mass of an organism over time.

Plants grow at meristems (regions of active cell division)

Feature Explanation
Irreversible Once growth occurs, it cannot be reversed
Permanent The increase in size is lasting, not temporary
Increase in dry mass True growth increases protoplasm (living matter), not just water absorption
Cellular basis Growth results from cell division (mitosis) + cell enlargement

⚠️ Important Distinction

  • Swelling (e.g., seed absorbing water) is NOT growth
  • Reason: it is reversible and does not increase dry mass

Types of Growth in Organisms

🌿 Unlimited (Indeterminate)
  • Where: Plants
  • How: Grow continuously at meristems
  • Key idea: Growth continues throughout life
🧍 Limited (Determinate)
  • Where: Animals
  • How: Stop growing after maturity
  • Key idea: Growth has an endpoint
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"Plants keep growing, animals stop at maturity."

🌾 8b. Germination of Seeds

Definition (Germination): Germination is the process by which a seed develops into a new seedling (young plant). It begins when the dormant embryo resumes metabolic activity and starts growing.

Seed germination: water uptake → enzyme activity → embryo growth

Conditions Necessary for Germination

Condition Why It's Needed Practical Example
Water Softens seed coat; activates enzymes; mobilizes food reserves Soaking seeds before planting
Oxygen For aerobic respiration to release energy for growth Well-aerated soil (not waterlogged)
Suitable Temperature Activates enzymes for metabolic processes Usually 25–30°C for tropical seeds
Viable Seed Embryo must be alive and capable of growth Fresh seeds (not old/damaged)
Enzymes Break down stored food into usable forms Amylase: starch→sugars; Protease: proteins→amino acids

🧠 MEMORY TIP:

Remember "WOVE" — Water, Oxygen, Viable seed, Enzymes (temperature is implied because enzymes need it).

🧪 Application (JAMB Style)

  • Seeds planted in waterlogged soil may fail to germinate because of lack of oxygen for respiration.

Types of Germination ⭐ (Exam Favorite)

Epigeal vs Hypogeal germination (cotyledon position is key)

Feature EPIGEAL GERMINATION HYPOGEAL GERMINATION
Meaning "Epi" = above; cotyledons rise above ground "Hypo" = below; cotyledons remain below ground
Cotyledon position Pushed above soil surface Stay underground in the soil
Fate of cotyledons Emerge, turn green, photosynthesize temporarily Stay in soil as food storage; shrivel after use
Hypocotyl Elongates rapidly Does not elongate much
Epicotyl Stays short initially Elongates to push plumule up
Examples Beans, groundnut, cotton, sunflower, castor oil Maize, rice, wheat, sorghum, pea, mango
