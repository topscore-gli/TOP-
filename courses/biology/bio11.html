– JAMB Topic 8: Growth Made Super Easy

1. Meaning of Growth 🌱

Growth is the permanent and irreversible increase in the size, mass, volume, length, or number/complexity of cells in a living organism.

It results from three main processes:

  • Cell division (mainly mitosis in somatic cells)
  • Cell enlargement/elongation
  • Cell differentiation (cells become specialized, e.g., root hair, xylem)

Plants: Indeterminate growth (continues lifelong at apical, lateral, intercalary meristems).

Animals: Determinate growth (stops at maturity).

2. Mitosis (For growth, repair, asexual reproduction) 🔬

Produces 2 identical diploid daughter cells (2n → 2n).

Phases of Mitosis (PMAT mnemonic):

  • Prophase: Chromosomes condense & visible; nuclear membrane disappears; spindle forms.
  • Metaphase: Chromosomes line up at equator; spindle attaches to centromeres.
  • Anaphase: Sister chromatids separate & move to opposite poles.
  • Telophase: Chromosomes decondense; nuclear membrane reforms; cytokinesis (cell splits).

3. Meiosis (Gamete production – reduction division) 🧬

Produces 4 genetically different haploid gametes (2n → n). Two divisions: Meiosis I (reduction) + Meiosis II.

Crossing-Over (Key in Prophase I of Meiosis I): Exchange of genetic material between non-sister chromatids → genetic variation.

Feature Mitosis Meiosis
Divisions 1 2
Daughter cells 2 identical, diploid 4 different, haploid
Crossing-over No Yes (Prophase I)
Purpose Growth, repair Gamete production, variation

4. Plant Hormones (Phytohormones) 🌿

These chemicals control growth and responses.

Hormone Site Key Roles in Growth Effect
Auxin (IAA) Shoot tip Cell elongation, phototropism, apical dominance Promoter
Gibberellins Young leaves, seeds Stem elongation, break dormancy Promoter
Cytokinins Root tips Cell division, delay senescence Promoter
Abscisic Acid Mature leaves, roots Inhibits growth, closes stomata Inhibitor
Ethylene Ripening fruits Fruit ripening, abscission Mostly Inhibitor

5. Animal Growth Hormones 🐾

Hormone Gland Main Functions Deficiency/Excess
Growth Hormone (GH) Anterior Pituitary Bone/cartilage growth, protein synthesis Dwarfism / Gigantism
Thyroxine (T3/T4) Thyroid Metabolic rate, brain growth Cretinism / Hyperthyroidism
Insulin Pancreas Glucose uptake, protein/fat synthesis Diabetes (poor growth)
Testosterone Testes Muscle/bone growth, male puberty Delayed development
Estrogen Ovaries Breast/bone growth, female puberty Delayed puberty

6. Tropisms in Plants (Directional growth responses) 🌞

Controlled by auxin redistribution (Cholodny-Went theory).

Type Stimulus Shoot Response Root Response Example
Phototropism Light Positive (towards) Negative (away) Shoot bends to sun
Gravitropism Gravity Negative (up) Positive (down) Roots grow down
Hydrotropism Water — Positive (towards) Roots seek moisture
Thigmotropism Touch — — Tendrils coil around support

Auxin Mechanism: Higher auxin on shaded/lower side → shoots elongate more → bending.

7. Nastic Movements (Non-directional, reversible) 🔄

Due to turgor pressure changes (not growth).

Type Stimulus Example Response Plant Example
Thigmonasty/Seismonasty Touch/shock Leaves fold downward Mimosa pudica, Venus flytrap
Photonasty Light change Petals open/close Tulip, Oxalis
Nyctinasty Darkness (night) Leaves fold ("sleep") Legumes, Oxalis

8. Germination of Seeds 🌱

Viable dormant seed awakens under favourable conditions.

Essential Conditions (JAMB must-know):

  • Water – Imbibition, enzyme activation
  • Oxygen – Aerobic respiration for ATP
  • Suitable temperature – 25–35°C for enzymes
Feature Epigeal Hypogeal
Elongating part Hypocotyl Epicotyl
Cotyledons position Above ground (green, photosynthesize) Below ground (food storage)
Examples Bean, groundnut, sunflower Maize, rice, pea
