BIOLOGY

Co-ordination and Control

JAMB Biology – Detailed & Easy-to-Learn Notes

1. Introduction

Co-ordination and control enable organisms to detect changes (stimuli) and respond appropriately to maintain homeostasis.

  • Nervous system → rapid electrical impulses
  • Endocrine (hormonal) system → slower chemical messages via blood

Plants use phytohormones for growth and responses.

2. Nervous System

Central Nervous System (CNS) – Brain & Spinal Cord
  • Cerebrum – largest part; voluntary actions, intelligence, memory, reasoning, interpretation of senses
  • Cerebellum – balance, posture, coordination of voluntary movements
  • Medulla oblongata – involuntary vital functions: breathing, heart rate, blood pressure, swallowing
  • Hypothalamus – links nervous & endocrine systems; regulates body temperature, hunger, thirst, sleep-wake cycle
  • Spinal cord – grey matter (H-shaped – cell bodies) inside, white matter (myelinated axons) outside; relays impulses & handles reflexes
Peripheral Nervous System (PNS) & Neuron Structure

PNS: 12 cranial nerves + 31 spinal nerves

  • Somatic → voluntary control of skeletal muscles
  • Autonomic → involuntary
    • Sympathetic → fight or flight (↑ heart rate, pupil dilation)
    • Parasympathetic → rest & digest (↓ heart rate, digestion)

Neuron parts:

  • Dendrites → receive signals
  • Cell body → nucleus & organelles
  • Axon → long fibre that carries impulse away (myelin sheath + nodes of Ranvier = saltatory conduction → faster)
  • Axon terminals → release neurotransmitters (e.g. acetylcholine) at synapse
Reflex Action & Reflex Arc

Fast, involuntary, protective response (usually spinal, minimal brain involvement).

Pathway (reflex arc): Receptor → Sensory (afferent) neuron → Relay neuron (spinal cord) → Motor (efferent) neuron → Effector (muscle or gland)

Examples: knee jerk, withdrawal from hot object, blinking when dust enters eye.

3. Sense Organs – Detailed Key Parts & JAMB Defects

Key Parts of the Five Sense Organs

1. Skin (Tactile – Touch, Pressure, Temperature, Pain)

  • Meissner's corpuscles – light touch, low-frequency vibration, texture
  • Pacinian corpuscles – deep pressure, high-frequency vibration
  • Merkel discs – sustained light pressure, fine detail
  • Ruffini endings – skin stretch, sustained pressure
  • Free nerve endings – pain, temperature (hot/cold)

2. Nose (Olfactory – Smell)

  • Olfactory epithelium – chemoreceptors detect odor molecules
  • Mucus dissolves volatile chemicals
  • Olfactory nerve (CN I) → brain

3. Tongue (Gustatory – Taste)

  • Taste buds on papillae – detect sweet, salty, sour, bitter, umami
  • No strict tongue map – all areas sense all tastes

4. Eye (Visual – Sight)

  • Cornea – major refraction
  • Iris & pupil – control light entry
  • Lens – accommodation (shape change for focus)
  • Retina – rods (dim light), cones (colour, detail)
  • Optic nerve – to brain

5. Ear (Auditory & Balance)

  • Outer ear – pinna + canal
  • Middle ear – eardrum + ossicles (amplify)
  • Inner ear – cochlea (hearing), semicircular canals + vestibule (balance)
JAMB High-Yield: Defects of Sense Organs & Corrections

Eye Defects (Most Common in JAMB)

  • Myopia (Short-sightedness)
    • Image forms in front of retina
    • Cause: elongated eyeball or strong lens
    • Symptoms: distant objects blurry
    • Correction: Concave (diverging) lens
  • Hypermetropia (Long-sightedness)
    • Image forms behind retina
    • Cause: short eyeball or weak lens
    • Symptoms: near objects blurry
    • Correction: Convex (converging) lens
  • Presbyopia
    • Age-related loss of lens elasticity → poor near vision
    • Correction: bifocal or progressive lenses
  • Cataract
    • Cloudy lens (protein denaturation)
    • Correction: surgical removal + artificial lens

Ear Defects

  • Conductive deafness
    • Cause: blockage or damage in outer/middle ear (wax, ossicle damage, perforated eardrum)
    • Correction: hearing aid or surgery
  • Sensorineural deafness
    • Cause: damage to cochlea hair cells or auditory nerve (noise, infection, ageing)
    • Correction: cochlear implant (severe cases)
Quick Self-Test – Sense Organs & Defects (JAMB Style)

Q1. Short-sightedness (myopia) is corrected using which type of lens?

Answer

Concave lens

Q2. Cloudy lens is a symptom of which eye defect?

Answer

Cataract

Q3. Damage to the cochlea or auditory nerve causes which type of deafness?

Answer

Sensorineural deafness

5. Homeostasis

Thermoregulation & Osmoregulation

Thermoregulation (37°C): Hypothalamus → sweating/vasodilation (hot), shivering/vasoconstriction (cold).

Osmoregulation: ADH → more water reabsorption (concentrated urine); Aldosterone → Na⁺ retention (water follows).

Quick Self-Test – Homeostasis

Q1. Hormone that increases water reabsorption in kidneys?

Answer

ADH
