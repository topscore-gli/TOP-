JAMB Chapter 5: Modes, Types & Processes

🌿 A. Modes of Nutrition

Overview: Nutrition is the process by which organisms obtain and utilize food for growth, repair, and energy. There are two main modes: Autotrophic and Heterotrophic.

1. AUTOTROPHIC NUTRITION

Autotrophs (plants, algae) make their own food vs Heterotrophs (animals, fungi) depend on others

🔍 Key Features

  • Definition: Organisms synthesize their own organic food from simple inorganic substances
  • Energy Source: Light energy (photosynthesis) or chemical energy (chemosynthesis)
  • Examples: Green plants, algae, photosynthetic bacteria, chemosynthetic bacteria
  • Role: Producers in the food chain

2. HETEROTROPHIC NUTRITION

🔍 Key Features

  • Definition: Organisms cannot synthesize their own food; depend on ready-made organic substances
  • Energy Source: Organic compounds from other organisms
  • Examples: Animals, fungi, most bacteria, some plants
  • Role: Consumers in the food chain
🌳 AUTOTROPHS
  • Make own food
  • Use CO₂ + H₂O
  • Need chlorophyll (mostly)
  • Producers
  • Green plants, algae
🐄 HETEROTROPHS
  • Cannot make food
  • Depend on others
  • No chlorophyll
  • Consumers
  • Animals, fungi, bacteria
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"Auto = Self" (self-feeding) | "Hetero = Other" (feeding on others)

📋 B. Types of Nutrition

1. AUTOTROPHIC TYPES

a) Photosynthesis (Photoautotrophs)

Process by which green plants use light energy to convert CO₂ and water into glucose and oxygen.

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

b) Chemosynthesis (Chemoautotrophs)

Chemosynthesis in bacteria using hydrogen sulfide at hydrothermal vents

Process by which certain bacteria synthesize organic compounds using chemical energy instead of light.

🔍 Examples of Chemosynthetic Bacteria

  • Nitrifying bacteria: Convert ammonia to nitrites/nitrates
  • Sulphur bacteria: Oxidize hydrogen sulphide
  • Iron bacteria: Oxidize ferrous iron

2. HETEROTROPHIC TYPES

Type Description Examples
Holozoic Ingesting complex organic food, digesting internally Sheep, Man, Dog
Parasitic Living on/in host, absorbing nutrients Roundworm, Tapeworm, Loranthus
Saprophytic Feeding on dead/decaying organic matter Rhizopus, Mushroom
Carnivorous Plants Plants that trap and digest insects Sundew, Bladderwort

🌱 C. Plant Nutrition

1. PHOTOSYNTHESIS

Light reactions (thylakoid) and Dark reactions/Calvin Cycle (stroma)

Requires light? Yes No (uses ATP & NADPH)

🔍 Requirements for Photosynthesis

  • Light: Provides energy (trapped by chlorophyll)
  • Chlorophyll: Green pigment in chloroplasts
  • Carbon (IV) oxide: Raw material from air
  • Water: Raw material from soil
  • Optimum temperature: For enzyme activity (25-35°C)
🧠 MEMORY TRICK:

"L-I-G-H-T" = Light, Inorganic substances (CO₂, H₂O), Green pigment (chlorophyll), Heat (temperature), Time

2. MINERAL REQUIREMENTS

Visual guide to nutrient deficiency symptoms in plants

Macro-nutrients (needed in large quantities):

Element Function Deficiency Symptoms
Nitrogen (N) Protein synthesis, chlorophyll Yellowing (chlorosis), stunted growth
Phosphorus (P) Energy transfer (ATP), roots Purple discoloration, poor roots
Potassium (K) Enzyme activation, water regulation Leaf edge browning, weak stems
Calcium (Ca) Cell wall formation Stunted root tips, distorted leaves
Magnesium (Mg) Chlorophyll component Interveinal chlorosis (yellow veins)
Sulphur (S) Protein synthesis Yellowing of young leaves

3. THE MAMMALIAN TOOTH

Cross-section of a mammalian tooth showing enamel, dentine, pulp cavity

Structure of a Tooth:

  • Crown: Visible part above gum
  • Root: Embedded in jawbone
  • Enamel: Hardest substance, protects tooth
  • Dentine: Calcified tissue under enamel
  • Pulp cavity: Contains nerves and blood vessels
  • Cementum: Covers the root

Types of Teeth:

Types of mammalian teeth: Incisors, Canines, Premolars, Molars

Type Structure Function
Incisors Chisel-shaped, flat Cutting and biting
Canines Pointed, conical Tearing and piercing
Premolars Flat with ridges Grinding and crushing
Molars Broad, flat surface Grinding and chewing

5. NUTRITION PROCESS

Major digestive enzymes and their functions in different organs

Stages of Nutrition:

  • Ingestion: Taking food into the mouth
  • Digestion: Breakdown of food into absorbable units
  • Absorption: Uptake of digested nutrients (mainly in small intestine)
  • Assimilation: Using absorbed nutrients for body functions
  • Egestion: Removal of undigested waste

Digestive Enzymes:

Enzyme Source Substrate Product pH
Salivary amylase Salivary glands Starch Maltose Neutral
Pepsin Stomach Proteins Peptones Acidic

Pancreatic amylase Pancreas Starch Maltose Basic
Lipase Pancreas Fats/Oils Fatty acids + Glycerol Basic
Maltase Small intestine Maltose Glucose Basic
Erepsin Small intestine Peptides Amino acids Basic

⚠️ CRITICAL JAMB EXAM TIPS

  • Photosynthesis: Know the equation, light vs dark reactions, and requirements (light, CO₂, chlorophyll, water)
  • Chemosynthesis: Bacteria use chemical energy (not light) - common in nitrifying bacteria
  • Heterotrophic types: Be able to identify holozoic, parasitic, saprophytic, and carnivorous plants
  • Mineral deficiencies: N (yellowing), P (purple), K (brown edges) - most commonly tested
  • Food tests: Iodine (starch = blue-black), Benedict's (sugar = brick-red), Biuret (protein = violet)
  • Dental formula: Man (2123/2123), Sheep (0033/4033), Dog (3142/3143)
  • Digestive enzymes: Know where each is produced and what they digest
  • Vitamin deficiencies: Vitamin C = Scurvy, Vitamin D = Rickets, Protein = Kwashiorkor
  • End products: Carbohydrates → Glucose, Proteins → Amino acids, Fats → Fatty acids + Glycerol

📋 Quick Revision Checklist

Must-Know Concepts:

  • ✅ Autotrophs make food; Heterotrophs eat other organisms
  • ✅ Photosynthesis needs light, chlorophyll, CO₂, water
  • ✅ Light reaction produces ATP and NADPH; Dark reaction produces glucose
  • ✅ N, P, K are the three major plant nutrients
  • ✅ Six classes of food: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Fats, Vitamins, Minerals, Water
  • ✅ Food test colors: Starch (blue-black), Sugar (brick-red), Protein (violet)
  • ✅ Four types of teeth: Incisors, Canines, Premolars, Molars
  • ✅ Man has 32 teeth, Dog has 42, Sheep has 32 (but no upper incisors/canines)
  • ✅ Digestion ends with: Glucose, Amino acids, Fatty acids + Glycerol
  • ✅ Small intestine is the main site of absorption

End Products of Digestion:

🍞 Carbohydrates
  • Starch → Maltose → Glucose
  • Enzymes: Amylase, Maltase
🥩 Proteins
  • Proteins → Peptones → Peptides → Amino acids
  • Enzymes: Pepsin, Trypsin, Erepsin
🧈 Fats & Oils
  • Fats → Fatty acids + Glycerol
  • Enzyme: Lipase
💧 Water
  • Absorbed in large intestine
  • Vitamins & minerals absorbed directly
