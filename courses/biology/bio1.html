1. Characteristics of Living Things 🧬

How do we know something is alive? Use MRS GREN!

MRS GREN stands for:

  • Movement – Animals run 🏃, plants grow to light ☀️
  • Respiration – Get energy from food
  • Sensitivity – Feel and react (hot? Pull hand! 🔥)
  • Growth – Get bigger forever
  • Reproduction – Make babies/offspring
  • Excretion – Throw out waste (sweat, pee, CO₂)
  • Nutrition – Eat or make food (plants use sun 🌱)

Non-living things like rocks can't do these!

2. Cell Theory – The Big Rule 📜

Everything alive is made of cells!

  • All living things = 1 (Unicellular) or more cells (Multicellular)
  • Cell = basic unit of life
  • New cells come from old cells (no magic!)

All living things are made of cells! This big idea came from many scientists.

Key discoveries:

  • 1665 – Robert Hooke saw "cells" in cork under microscope
  • 1670s – Leeuwenhoek saw living cells (bacteria, protozoa)
  • 1838 – Schleiden: All plants are made of cells
  • 1839 – Schwann: All animals are made of cells
  • 1855 – Virchow: "All cells come from pre-existing cells"

Three main rules today:

  1. All organisms are made of one or more cells
  2. Cell is the basic unit of structure, function, and life
  3. All cells come from other cells (via division)

3. Cell Structure – Plant vs Animal Cells 🔬

Plant 🌿 and animal 🐱 cells are tiny life factories — similar but different!

Plant Cell Parts

  • Cell wall (only plants)
  • Chloroplasts (only plants)
  • Large central vacuole (only plants)
  • Cell membrane
  • Nucleus
  • Mitochondria
  • Ribosomes
  • Endoplasmic reticulum (ER)
  • Golgi apparatus
  • Cytoplasm

Easy Functions – What each part does:

  • Cell wall: Strong wall — protects & gives shape (like a box!)
  • Cell membrane: Gatekeeper — controls what goes in/out
  • Chloroplasts: Food factory — uses sun to make sugar (photosynthesis ☀️)
  • Large central vacuole: Big storage bag — holds water, keeps plant stiff & fresh
  • Nucleus: Brain of cell — holds DNA, controls everything
  • Mitochondria: Powerhouse — makes energy (ATP) from food
  • Ribosomes: Tiny protein builders — make proteins for the cell
  • Endoplasmic reticulum (ER): Highway — rough makes proteins, smooth makes fats
  • Golgi apparatus: Packaging center — wraps & sends proteins out
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly inside — where most reactions happen

Animal Cell Parts

  • No cell wall, no chloroplasts, no large vacuole
  • Centrioles (only animals)
  • Cell membrane
  • Nucleus
  • Mitochondria
  • Ribosomes
  • Endoplasmic reticulum (ER)
  • Golgi apparatus
  • Cytoplasm
  • Small vacuoles & lysosomes

Easy Functions – What each part does:

  • Cell membrane: Gatekeeper — lets good stuff in, bad out
  • Centrioles: Help cell divide — like organizers for splitting
  • Nucleus: Boss — holds DNA & tells cell what to do
  • Mitochondria: Energy factory — turns food into power (ATP)
  • Ribosomes: Protein makers — build what cell needs
  • Endoplasmic reticulum (ER): Transport tubes — rough for proteins, smooth for fats
  • Golgi apparatus: Post office — packs & ships proteins
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly space — reactions happen here
  • Lysosomes (more in animals): Trash bags — break down waste
  • Small vacuoles: Little storage pockets

Quick Comparison

  • Both have: membrane, nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, ER, Golgi, cytoplasm
  • Only Plant: cell wall, chloroplasts, large vacuole
  • Only Animal: centrioles, more lysosomes

4. Levels of Organization 📏

  1. Cell → basic unit (e.g. Euglena, Paramecium)
  2. Tissue → same cells together (e.g. skin tissue)
  3. Organ → tissues team up (e.g. heart, onion bulb)
  4. System → organs work together (e.g. digestive system)
  5. Organism → whole living thing (you! 👦)
