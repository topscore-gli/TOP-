a. Monera (Prokaryotes) 🦠

🔬 Key Characteristics

  • No true nucleus - DNA floats freely (nucleoid)
  • No membrane-bound organelles - no mitochondria, ER, etc.
  • Unicellular only - never form tissues
  • Cell wall: Peptidoglycan (unique to bacteria)
  • Reproduction: Binary fission (asexual)

Examples & Features

Organism Type Key Features
Bacteria Heterotrophic/Autotrophic Rod, spherical, spiral shapes; decomposers
Blue-green algae (Cyanobacteria) Photosynthetic First oxygen producers; nitrogen fixers

📋 Structure Notes:

  • Cell wall: Protection & shape (peptidoglycan)
  • Plasma membrane: Controls entry/exit of substances
  • Cytoplasm: Contains enzymes for reactions
  • Nucleoid: Circular DNA (not in nucleus!)
  • Ribosomes: Protein synthesis (smaller than eukaryotes)
  • Flagellum: Movement (when present)
  • Capsule: Extra protection layer (in some)
  • Pilus: Attachment to surfaces/conjugation
🧠 JAMB Trick:

Blue-green algae are NOT true algae! They are bacteria (cyanobacteria) - prokaryotes, not eukaryotes!

b. Protista (Protozoans & Protophyta) 🔬

🔬 Key Characteristics

  • First eukaryotes - have true nucleus & organelles
  • Mostly unicellular - some colonial
  • "Catch-all" kingdom - organisms that don't fit elsewhere
  • Three types: Animal-like (protozoa), Plant-like (algae), Fungus-like (slime molds)

Major Examples Comparison

Organism Type Movement Key Feature
Amoeba Protozoan Pseudopodia Shape-shifter, engulfs food
Paramecium Protozoan Cilia Two nuclei (macro & micro)
Euglena Both! Flagellum Eyespot, mixotrophic

Amoeba Details

  • Pseudopodia: "False feet" for movement & feeding (phagocytosis)
  • Contractile vacuole: Pumps out excess water (osmoregulation)
  • Food vacuole: Digests engulfed food
  • Nucleus: Controls cell activities
  • Ectoplasm/Endoplasm: Outer gel layer, inner fluid

Euglena Details

  • Flagellum: Whip-like movement
  • Eyespot (Stigma): Detects light direction
  • Chloroplasts: Photosynthesis (plant-like)
  • Contractile vacuole: Water balance
  • Paramylon: Stored carbohydrate (not starch!)
🧠 JAMB Trick:

Euglena is the "JACK OF ALL TRADES" - has both plant (chloroplasts) AND animal (eyespot, flagellum, eats food) characteristics!

Paramecium Details

  • Cilia: Thousands for movement & sweeping food
  • Oral groove: Mouth entrance
  • Two nuclei: Macronucleus (daily functions) & Micronucleus (reproduction)
  • Contractile vacuoles: Two of them for osmoregulation
  • Trichocysts: Defense structures
  • Anal pore (Cytoproct): Waste exit

c. Fungi 🍄

🔬 Key Characteristics

  • Heterotrophic absorbers - secrete enzymes, absorb nutrients
  • Cell wall: CHITIN (NOT cellulose like plants!)
  • No chlorophyll - cannot photosynthesize
  • Body: Thread-like hyphae forming mycelium
  • Mostly multicellular - except yeast (unicellular)
Organism Common Name Key Features Reproduction
Rhizopus Bread mold White cottony growth, sporangia Spores (asexual) & Zygospores (sexual)
Mushroom Toadstool Fruiting body, gills, underground mycelium Basidiospores

Mushroom Structure

  • Pileus (Cap): Protects gills underneath
  • Gills: Produce millions of basidiospores
  • Stipe (Stalk): Supports cap
  • Annulus: Ring from broken partial veil
  • Volva: Cup at base (remnant of universal veil)
  • Mycelium: Underground network of hyphae (main body!)

Rhizopus Structure

  • Sporangium: Black round spore case at top
  • Sporangiophore: Stalk holding sporangium
  • Columella: Dome supporting sporangium
  • Stolon: Horizontal hyphae connecting groups
  • Rhizoids: Root-like hyphae for anchoring & absorption
🧠 JAMB Trick:

Fungi cell walls = CHITIN (same as insect exoskeletons!), NOT cellulose. This distinguishes them from plants!

d. Plantae (Plants) 🌿

🌱 Evolutionary Progression

Thallophyta → Bryophyta → Pteridophyta → Spermatophyta
(Algae) → (Mosses) → (Ferns) → (Seed Plants)

  • Trend: Aquatic → Terrestrial
  • Trend: No vascular → Vascular tissues
  • Trend: Spores → Seeds (naked → enclosed in fruits)

i. Thallophyta (Algae)

Feature Description
Body Thallus (undifferentiated - no true roots, stems, leaves)
Habitat Mostly aquatic (freshwater & marine)
Vascular tissue ❌ Absent
Reproduction Asexual (fragmentation) & Sexual (conjugation in Spirogyra)
Example Spirogyra (filamentous with spiral chloroplasts)

← Swipe table to see more →

Spirogyra Structure

  • Spiral chloroplasts: Characteristic ribbon-like spirals
  • Pyrenoid: Protein body in chloroplast for starch storage
  • Conjugation tube: Sexual reproduction between filaments
  • Septa: Cross-walls between cells

ii. Bryophyta (Mosses & Liverworts)

💡 Memory Aid:

"Amphibians of the Plant World" - first land plants but NEED WATER to reproduce (sperm swims to egg)

Feature Description
Body Simple leafy structure (no true roots)
Vascular tissue ❌ Absent (no xylem/phloem)
Attachment Rhizoids (not true roots)
Life cycle Alternation of generations (gametophyte dominant)
Examples Brachymenium (moss), Marchantia (liverwort)

← Swipe table to see more →

Marchantia (Liverwort)

  • Thallus: Flat, lobed, dichotomously branched
  • Gemma cups: Contain gemmae for asexual reproduction
  • Rhizoids: Anchor and absorb water
  • Antheridiophore: Male structure with antheridia (sperm)
  • Archegoniophore: Female structure with archegonia (egg)

iii. Pteridophyta (Ferns)

🌿 Key Advancement

  • First vascular plants! - have xylem & phloem
  • True roots, stems (rhizomes), and leaves (fronds)
  • Can grow taller due to vascular tissues
  • Still need water for fertilization (swimming sperm)

Dryopteris (Fern)

  • Frond: Large divided leaf (megaphyll)
  • Pinnae: Leaflets of the frond
  • Sori: Clusters of sporangia on underside
  • Fiddlehead: Young coiled frond (circinate vernation)
  • Rhizome: Underground stem
  • Prothallus: Small heart-shaped gametophyte

iv. Spermatophyta (Seed Plants)

Gymnosperms
  • Naked seeds
  • No flowers/fruits
  • Cones for reproduction
  • Needle-like leaves
  • Examples: Pines, Cycads
Angiosperms
  • Seeds in fruits
  • Flowers present
  • Enclosed ovules
  • Broad leaves
  • Examples: Maize, Beans

Angiosperm Division

Maize (Monocot) Structure

Bean Seed (Dicot) Structure

  • Cotyledons: Two seed leaves storing food
  • Plumule: Shoot tip (future stem & leaves)
  • Radicle: Root tip (future root system)

e. Animalia (Animals) 🐾

🔬 Major Division

  • Invertebrates: No backbone (95% of animal species)
  • Vertebrates: Have backbone (fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals)

i. Invertebrates (No Backbone)

Phylum Key Features Example

Platyhelminthes Bilateral, flattened, no body cavity, parasitic Taenia (tapeworm)
Nematoda Round, pseudocoelom, complete gut Ascaris
Annelida Segmented, true coelom, setae Earthworm
Arthropoda Exoskeleton (chitin), jointed legs, segmented Insects, spiders
Mollusca Soft body, mantle, muscular foot Snails, octopus

Hydra (Coelenterata)

  • Tentacles: Surround mouth, armed with cnidocytes (stinging cells)
  • Cnidocytes: Contain nematocysts (stinging organelles)
  • Gastrovascular cavity: Digestive chamber (one opening)
  • Budding: Asexual reproduction
  • Basal disc: Attachment to substrate

Taenia (Tapeworm - Platyhelminthes)

  • Scolex: Head with hooks and suckers for attachment
  • Proglottids: Segments containing reproductive organs
  • No digestive system: Absorbs nutrients through tegument
  • Hermaphroditic: Each segment has both male & female organs

Earthworm (Annelida)

  • Clitellum: Saddle-like structure for cocoon formation
  • Setae: Bristles for grip and movement
  • Segments: Metamerism (repeated segments with organs)
  • Crop & Gizzard: Food storage and grinding
  • Nephridia: Excretory organs in each segment
  • Hermaphroditic: Exchange sperm during copulation

Arthropoda Classes

Class Features Examples

Arachnida 2 body parts, 8 legs, no antennae Spider, scorpion
Crustacea 2 pairs antennae, mostly aquatic Crab, crayfish

Mosquito (Insecta)

  • Proboscis: Piercing-sucking mouthpart (only female bites)
  • Compound eyes: Made of many ommatidia
  • Antennae: Sensory (male has feathery antennae)
  • Wings: One pair (hind wings reduced to halteres)
  • Complete metamorphosis: Egg → Larva → Pupa → Adult

Snail (Mollusca)

  • Shell: External protection (secreted by mantle)
  • Mantle: Tissue layer secreting shell
  • Foot: Muscular for creeping locomotion
  • Radula: Rasping tongue for feeding
  • Tentacles: Upper pair has eyes at tips

Fish → Amphibians → Reptiles → Birds → Mammals

🔥 Key Evolutionary Trends in Animals

  • Symmetry: Radial → Bilateral
  • Body cavity: Acoelom → Pseudocoelom → True coelom
  • Organization: Tissue → Organ → Organ systems
  • Skeleton: Hydrostatic → Exoskeleton → Endoskeleton
  • Temperature: Ectothermic (cold-blooded) → Endothermic (warm-blooded)
  • Reproduction: External fertilization → Internal fertilization → Live birth
  • Habitat: Aquatic → Semi-terrestrial → Fully terrestrial

⚠️ CRITICAL JAMB EXAM TIPS

  • Euglena is frequently tested as having BOTH plant and animal characteristics
  • Blue-green algae are bacteria (prokaryotes), NOT true algae
  • Fungi cell walls = Chitin (not cellulose like plants)
  • Bryophytes need water for sperm to swim to egg
  • Gymnosperms have naked seeds (no fruits)
  • All arthropods have chitin exoskeletons
  • Birds and mammals are endothermic (warm-blooded)
  • Amphibians are the evolutionary link between water and land vertebrates
  • Earthworms are hermaphrodites (both male and female)
  • Spirogyra reproduces by conjugation (sexual) and fragmentation (asexual)
