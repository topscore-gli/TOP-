–Symbiotic Interactions & Nutrient Cycling

1. Types of Symbiotic Interactions

Symbiotic interactions are close, long-term relationships between different species. JAMB loves asking for examples and the type of benefit/harm.

Mutualism – Both benefit

  • Bees and flowers (bee gets nectar, flower gets pollinated)
  • Rhizobium bacteria in root nodules of legumes (beans, cowpea, groundnut) – bacteria fix nitrogen, plant gives sugar
  • Lichen (algae + fungus)

Commensalism – One benefits, other unaffected

  • Cattle egrets and cattle (egrets eat insects stirred by cattle)
  • Orchids growing on tree trunks
  • Remora fish on sharks

Parasitism – One benefits, other harmed

  • Ticks/lice on dogs or cattle
  • Mistletoe on cocoa or mango trees
  • Tapeworm in human intestine

Saprophytism – Feed on dead matter

  • Mushrooms and fungi on rotting wood
  • Bacteria breaking down dead leaves/animals

Competition, Predation & Others

  • Competition – both harmed (plants fighting for light in a farm)
  • Predation – lion eats zebra
  • Amensalism – penicillin mould kills bacteria

2. Energy Flow in the Ecosystem

Energy flows in one direction from the sun and is lost as heat at each step (only \~10% transferred).

Food Chains

Linear sequence showing who eats whom.

Example (savanna): Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Hawk

Food Webs

Many food chains linked together – more realistic.

Trophic Levels

  • Producers (Level 1) – green plants
  • Primary consumers (Level 2) – herbivores
  • Secondary consumers (Level 3) – carnivores
  • Tertiary consumers (Level 4) – top predators

3. Nutrient Cycling in Nature

Carbon Cycle

Carbon moves between atmosphere, organisms and oceans.

  • Plants take CO₂ during photosynthesis
  • Animals and plants release CO₂ during respiration
  • Decomposers and burning fossil fuels release more CO₂

Significance: Maintains O₂/CO₂ balance. Excess CO₂ causes global warming.

Water Cycle

Water evaporates, condenses, rains, and returns to oceans. It carries nutrients and supports all other cycles.

Nitrogen Cycle

78% of air is nitrogen but plants cannot use it directly.

  • Nitrogen fixation by Rhizobium in root nodules of legumes (beans, groundnut)
  • Nitrification → nitrates for plants
  • Decomposers return nitrogen to soil
