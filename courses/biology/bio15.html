– JAMB Topic 3: Natural Habitats

1. What are Natural Habitats?

A habitat is the natural home or environment where an organism lives and obtains everything it needs (food, water, shelter, mates).

JAMB divides habitats into two main types:

  • Aquatic (water-based): ponds, streams, lakes, seashores, mangrove swamps
  • Terrestrial / Arboreal (land/tree-based): tree-tops, abandoned farmland, dry grassy savanna field, burrow or hole

You must know:

  • Typical plants and animals in each habitat
  • Adaptive features that help them survive there

2. Aquatic Habitats

Types & Examples

  • Freshwater: ponds, streams, lakes
  • Brackish / Marine: seashores, mangrove swamps

Organisms & Adaptive Features

Organism Typical Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Water lily / Water lettuce Ponds, lakes Floating leaves with air-filled tissues, stomata on upper surface, flexible petioles
Tilapia, Catfish Streams, ponds, lakes Gills with large surface area, streamlined body, lateral line system
Red mangrove Mangrove swamps Prop roots & pneumatophores for support & oxygen in anaerobic mud, salt glands
Mudskipper, Fiddler crab Mangrove swamps / seashores Modified gills + skin breathing, strong pectoral fins for walking, burrowing

3. Terrestrial / Arboreal Habitats

Types & Examples

  • Tree-tops (arboreal)
  • Abandoned farmland / dry grassy savanna field
  • Burrow or hole in soil

Organisms & Adaptive Features

Organism Typical Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Oil palm, Iroko, Mahogany Tree-tops / forest Tall straight trunk, broad leaves for light capture, deep tap roots
Monkey, Parrot, Chameleon Tree-tops Prehensile tail / grasping hands/feet, strong claws, binocular vision
Elephant grass, Guinea grass Savanna field / abandoned farmland Deep fibrous roots, fire-resistant base, rapid regrowth after burning
Antelope, Grasshopper, Lizard Dry grassy savanna Camouflage colour, long legs for speed/jumping, large eyes for spotting predators
Earthworm, Termite, Burrowing rodent (rat) Burrow / soil hole Moist permeable skin, strong setae/claws for digging, nocturnal activity
