🌿 Chapter 15: Natural Habitats

Aquatic + Terrestrial habitats with organisms and adaptive features

📚 Overview of Habitats

Definition (Habitat): A habitat is the natural environment where an organism lives. It provides food, water, shelter, and suitable conditions for survival and reproduction.

Different habitats support different communities of organisms

Two Main Categories

  • Aquatic (water-based)
  • Terrestrial/Arboreal (land/tree-based)

🌊 Part A: Aquatic Habitats

Definition (Aquatic habitat): A habitat in which organisms live in water (freshwater, marine, or brackish environments).

Freshwater ecosystems include ponds, streams, and lakes

1. Freshwater Habitats

Ponds

Ponds: still water, shallow, lots of vegetation

Habitat Characteristics Common Organisms Adaptive Features
Ponds Still/standing water; shallow; high vegetation; low oxygen at bottom Water lily, duckweed, algae; tadpoles, frogs, water snails, dragonfly nymphs, pond skaters Floating leaves; air spaces for buoyancy; gills; webbed feet; streamlined bodies
Streams Flowing water; high oxygen; cooler; rocky/sandy bottom Water cress, mosses; trout, crayfish, mayfly nymphs, water striders Flattened bodies; hooks/claws; streamlined shape to resist current
Lakes Large standing water; zones (littoral/limnetic/profundal); varying depths Lotus, reeds, water weeds; tilapia, catfish, water birds, plankton Long stems to surface; swim bladder; waterproof feathers; temperature tolerance

Lakes

2. Marine (Saltwater) Habitats

Marine habitats include shorelines, reefs, and open oceans

Seashores (Intertidal Zone)

Intertidal zone: alternating wet/dry + wave action

Mangrove Swamps (Brackish water)

Mangroves live in brackish, waterlogged, salty conditions

Habitat Characteristics Common Organisms Adaptive Features
Seashores (Intertidal) Alternating wet/dry; waves; varying salinity; rocky/sandy Seaweeds, algae; crabs, starfish, sea anemones, barnacles, mussels, periwinkles Hard shells; closing shells to prevent drying; tube feet/attachment; camouflage
Mangrove swamps Brackish water; waterlogged soil; low oxygen; high salinity; aerial roots Mangroves; mudskippers, crabs, oysters, shrimp, birds (egrets/herons) Pneumatophores; prop roots; salt-excreting glands; vivipary; salinity tolerance

🔑 JAMB Hot Point: Mangrove Survival

  • Pneumatophores = breathing roots (low oxygen soil)
  • Prop roots = support in mud
  • Salt-excreting glands = remove excess salt
  • Vivipary = seeds germinate on parent plant

🌳 Part B: Terrestrial & Arboreal Habitats

Definition (Terrestrial habitat): A habitat found on land where organisms depend on air for breathing and must conserve water.

Terrestrial habitats include savanna, forests, and underground burrows

Tree-tops (Arboreal/Canopy)

Canopy: high sunlight, wind exposure, limited water

Abandoned Farmland (Secondary Succession)

Secondary succession: pioneer species → shrubs → young trees

Dry Grassy Field (Savanna)

Savanna: seasonal rain, long dry season, fire-prone

Burrows / Holes

Burrows provide stable temperature, humidity, and protection

Habitat Characteristics Common Organisms Adaptive Features
Tree-tops (Arboreal) High sunlight; windy; limited water; lots of fruits/leaves Epiphytes; monkeys, squirrels, parrots, chameleons, tree frogs, insects, snakes Prehensile tails; sharp claws; camouflage green; binocular vision; lightweight bodies; aerial roots
Abandoned farmland Disturbed soil; high sunlight; pioneer species; changing community Grasses/weeds/shrubs; grasshoppers, rodents, lizards, birds Fast growth; wind-dispersed seeds; high reproduction; generalist feeding; migration
Savanna Seasonal rainfall; long dry season; scattered trees; fire-prone Elephant grass, acacia, baobab; zebras, antelopes, lions, giraffes, termites Thick bark; deep roots; waxy leaves; thorns; migration; camouflage; burrowing; water conservation
Burrows Underground; dark; stable temperature; humid; protected Rabbits, rats, earthworms, termites, foxes, meerkats Strong claws/teeth; reduced eyesight; enhanced senses; streamlined bodies; tolerate low oxygen; nocturnal behavior

🔑 Key Concepts to Remember

Adaptive Features Explained

Feature Type Meaning Purpose Examples
Structural (Morphological) Physical body parts Helps survival in habitat Webbed feet (duck), gills (fish), pneumatophores (mangrove)
Physiological Internal body processes Maintains body function Salt excretion (mangrove), water conservation, swim bladder (fish)
Behavioral Actions/behaviours Avoids danger/extremes Migration, burrowing, nocturnal activity

📋 Quick Revision Checklist

  • For each habitat: name 3 plants + 3 animals
  • Explain 3 adaptive features of organisms there
  • Describe environmental conditions
  • Compare pond vs lake; stream vs pond; mangrove vs seashore

⚡ Likely JAMB Questions & Answers

  • Q1: Mangrove survival in waterlogged soil?
    A: Pneumatophores, prop roots, salt glands, vivipary.
  • Q2: Why savanna trees have thick bark?
    A: Fire protection + reduces water loss.
  • Q3: Arboreal animals adaptations?
    A: Prehensile tail, claws, binocular vision, lightweight body.
  • Q4: Pond plants float how?
    A: Air spaces for buoyancy + broad floating leaves.
  • Q5: Why stream animals flattened?
    A: Reduce resistance + avoid being swept away.
