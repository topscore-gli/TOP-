🌱 REPRODUCTION

JAMB Chapter 10 - Complete Master Note

🌟 Introduction

Definition: Reproduction is the biological process by which living organisms produce new individuals of their own kind, ensuring continuity of species.

  • Ensures continuity of life
  • Occurs in all living organisms
  • Two types: Asexual & Sexual
  • Important for evolution and survival

🔁 Asexual Reproduction

Definition: Asexual reproduction is a type of reproduction in which only one parent is involved, producing genetically identical offspring (clones).

1. Fission

Definition: Fission is when a single parent cell divides into two or more identical daughter cells.

Examples: Amoeba, Paramecium, Euglena, Bacteria (E. coli), Plasmodium (multiple fission)

Process: Nucleus divides → Cytoplasm divides → New identical organisms are formed.

Note: Multiple fission produces many offspring at once (e.g., Plasmodium).

2. Budding

Definition: Budding is when a new organism develops as an outgrowth (bud) from the parent body.

Examples: Yeast, Hydra, Sponges, Corals, some fungi

Process: Bud forms → Grows → Detaches or remains attached.

3. Natural Vegetative Propagation

Definition: New plants grow naturally from vegetative parts (root, stem, leaf).

StructureExamples
StemPotato (tuber), Ginger (rhizome), Onion (bulb), Strawberry (runner), Yam
RootSweet potato, Dahlia, Cassava, Carrot, Beetroot
LeafBryophyllum, Begonia, Kalanchoe, African violet, Sansevieria

4. Artificial Vegetative Propagation

Definition: Deliberate production of plants using human methods.

MethodDefinitionExamples
CuttingNew plant grows from cut part of stem/root/leafCassava, Sugarcane, Rose, Hibiscus
GraftingJoining parts of two plants to grow as oneMango, Citrus, Apple, Guava
BuddingInserting a bud from one plant into anotherOrange, Lemon, Rubber, Peach
LayeringBending branch to ground to form roots before cuttingBougainvillea, Jasmine, Grapevine, Blackberry

Asexual reproduction → rapid multiplication, preserves traits, important in agriculture.

🌸 Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

Definition: Involves fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.

Flower

Sepals: Protect flower bud

Petals: Attract pollinators

Stamen: Male part, produces pollen

Carpel/Pistil: Female part, contains ovules

Pollination

Transfer of pollen from anther → stigma

Self-pollination
Same flower/plant (Pea, Rice, Tomato, Wheat, Groundnut)
Cross-pollination
Different plant (Maize, Cocoa, Oil Palm, Pawpaw, Date Palm)

Agents: Wind, Insects, Water, Animals, Man

Advantages of Cross-pollination: Genetic variation, stronger offspring, disease resistance

Fertilization

Fusion of male & female gametes → zygote formation

Fruits

Definition: Ripened ovary containing seeds

TypeDefinitionExamples
SimpleDevelop from one ovaryMango, Tomato, Orange, Pepper, Guava
AggregateDevelop from many ovaries of one flowerStrawberry, Raspberry, Blackberry, Custard Apple, Soursop
MultipleDevelop from many flowersPineapple, Breadfruit, Fig, Mulberry, Jackfruit
SucculentFleshy, high water contentBanana, Pawpaw, Watermelon, Orange, Mango

👶 Reproduction in Mammals

Definition: Sexual reproduction involving internal fertilization and development.

Male Reproductive System

Functions:

  • Testes → sperm production
  • Scrotum → temperature regulation
  • Sperm duct → transport
  • Penis → delivery

Female Reproductive System

Functions:

  • Ovary → produces ova
  • Fallopian tube → fertilization site
  • Uterus → embryo development
  • Vagina → birth canal

Fertilization & Development

Sperm + Egg → Zygote → Embryo → Fetus → Birth

Factors affecting development: Poor nutrition, Alcohol, Drugs, Smoking, Diseases → birth defects/miscarriage/poor growth

🧪 Modern Methods of Reproduction

1. IVF (In Vitro Fertilization)

Definition: Fertilization occurs outside the body in laboratory.

Examples: Human fertility, Animal breeding, Research, Surrogacy, Genetic improvement

2. Birth Control

Definition: Methods to prevent pregnancy

TypeExamples

🎯 JAMB Quick Points

  • Asexual → one parent, no variation
  • Sexual → two parents, genetic variation
  • Pollination ≠ Fertilization
  • Fertilization = fusion of gametes
  • Ovule → seed, Ovary → fruit
  • Cross-pollination → stronger offspring
  • Mammals → internal fertilization
  • IVF → fertilization outside body
  • Vegetative propagation → important in agriculture
