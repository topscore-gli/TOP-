– Factors Affecting Distribution of Organisms

1. What Controls Where Organisms Live?

The distribution of plants and animals is not random. It depends on two main groups of factors:

  • Abiotic factors – non-living physical & chemical conditions
  • Biotic factors – interactions with other living organisms (including humans)

JAMB expects you to know how each factor affects distribution and how to measure some abiotic factors.

2. Abiotic Factors (Non-living)

Temperature

Affects enzyme activity and metabolic rate.

  • Too hot/cold → enzymes denature or slow down → organism dies or cannot reproduce
  • Tropical areas → high species diversity
  • Polar regions → few species (mostly adapted animals/plants)

Rainfall & Relative Humidity

Controls water availability.

  • High rainfall → rainforests (high plant & animal diversity)
  • Low rainfall → deserts (xerophytes, camels, few species)
  • High humidity → reduces water loss (important for amphibians & insects)

Wind Speed & Direction

Affects pollination, seed dispersal, transpiration, and cooling.

  • Strong winds → stunted trees on mountains/coasts
  • Wind carries seeds/pollen → spreads species

Altitude

Temperature decreases \~6.5 °C per 1,000 m rise → vegetation zones change.

  • Low altitude → tropical forest
  • High altitude → alpine tundra (few plants, hardy animals)

Salinity, Turbidity & pH (Aquatic)

  • Salinity – freshwater vs marine species (e.g., mangroves tolerate brackish water)
  • Turbidity – cloudy water reduces light → less photosynthesis (affects algae & fish)
  • pH – most organisms prefer 6.5–7.5; acidic water (acid rain) harms fish & amphibians

Edaphic (Soil) Factors

Soil texture, nutrients, pH, aeration, water-holding capacity.

  • Loamy soil → best for most plants
  • Acidic soil → favours heather, blueberries
  • Nutrient-poor soil → few large plants

3. Measuring Abiotic Factors (JAMB Equipment)

  • Temperature → thermometer (air, water, soil)
  • Rainfall → rain gauge
  • Relative humidity → hygrometer
  • Wind speed → anemometer
  • Wind direction → wind vane
  • Turbidity (water clarity) → Secchi disc
  • pH → pH meter or universal indicator
  • Salinity → refractometer or conductivity meter
  • Soil factors → soil auger, pH kit, sieve

4. Biotic Factors (Living Influences)

How other organisms affect distribution:

  • Competition (for food, space, light)
  • Predation & parasitism
  • Symbiosis (mutualism, commensalism, amensalism)
  • Disease outbreaks

5. Human Activities (Very Important for JAMB)

Humans are the biggest biotic factor today:

  • Deforestation → (Cutting down of trees) destroys habitats
  • Pollution → poisons air, water, soil
  • Over-hunting & over-fishing → reduces populations
  • Introduction of invasive species → out-compete natives
  • Agriculture & urbanisation → changes land use
  • Climate change → shifts temperature & rainfall zones
Quick JAMB Self-Test

Q1. Which tool measures water clarity?

Answer

Secchi disc

Q2. Deforestation is an example of which type of factor?

Answer

Biotic (human activity)

Q3. Which abiotic factor drops \~6.5°C per 1,000 m rise?

Answer

Temperature (altitude effect)
